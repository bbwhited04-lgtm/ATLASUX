# GDPR Compliance Framework

## Overview
Atlas UX maintains full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 for processing personal data of EU residents, with designated representation and comprehensive data protection measures.

## GDPR Principles Implementation

### Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
- **Lawful Basis**: Explicit lawful basis for all data processing activities
- **Privacy Notices**: Clear, concise privacy notices at point of collection
- **Transparency**: Open communication about data processing purposes
- **Fair Processing**: Fair and ethical data processing practices

### Purpose Limitation
- **Specified Purposes**: Data collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- **No Further Processing**: No processing incompatible with original purposes
- **Purpose Documentation**: Detailed documentation of processing purposes
- **Purpose Reviews**: Regular reviews of processing purpose compliance

### Data Minimization
- **Minimum Necessary**: Collection of only data necessary for specified purposes
- **Data Relevance**: Ensuring data is relevant to processing purposes
- **Data Limitation**: Limiting data collection to what is adequate
- **Retention Policies**: Data retention based on necessity

### Accuracy
- **Data Quality**: Maintaining accurate and up-to-date personal data
- **Update Procedures**: Procedures for data accuracy maintenance
- **Error Correction**: Timely correction of inaccurate data
- **Data Verification**: Regular data accuracy verification processes

### Storage Limitation
- **Retention Periods**: Defined retention periods for all data categories
- **Review Criteria**: Regular review of data retention necessity
- **Secure Deletion**: Secure deletion procedures for expired data
- **Data Mapping**: Comprehensive data inventory and mapping

### Integrity and Confidentiality
- **Security Measures**: Appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- **Encryption**: Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest
- **Access Controls**: Role-based access control with principle of least privilege
- **Security Testing**: Regular security testing and vulnerability assessments

### Accountability
- **Compliance Documentation**: Comprehensive GDPR compliance documentation
- **Data Protection Impact Assessments**: DPIAs for high-risk processing
- **Data Protection Officer**: Designated DPO with appropriate authority
- **Compliance Monitoring**: Ongoing compliance monitoring and review

## Data Subject Rights Implementation

### Right to be Informed
- **Privacy Notices**: Comprehensive privacy notices at data collection
- **Layered Notices**: Layered privacy information approach
- **Clear Language**: Plain language, no legal jargon
- **Timing**: Information provided at time of collection

### Right of Access
- **Access Requests**: Procedures for handling data subject access requests
- **Response Time**: Response within one month of request receipt
- **Format Provision**: Data provided in commonly used electronic format
- **Verification**: Identity verification procedures for access requests

### Right to Rectification
- **Correction Procedures**: Procedures for correcting inaccurate data
- **Response Time**: Rectification within one month of request
- **Data Sources**: Information about data sources when available
- **Notification**: Notification of third parties about corrections

### Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)
- **Erasure Criteria**: Clear criteria for when erasure applies
- **Erasure Procedures**: Secure data deletion procedures
- **Third Parties**: Notification of third parties about erasure
- **Public Interest**: Balancing erasure with public interest considerations

### Right to Restrict Processing
- **Restriction Criteria**: Clear criteria for processing restriction
- **Restriction Procedures**: Implementation of processing restrictions
- **Notification**: Notification of third parties about restrictions
- **Storage**: Secure storage of restricted data

### Right to Data Portability
- **Portability Criteria**: When portability right applies
- **Data Formats**: Machine-readable, structured data formats
- **Transfer Mechanisms**: Secure data transfer mechanisms
- **Direct Transfer**: Direct transfer to another controller when possible

### Right to Object
- **Objection Rights**: Clear objection rights for direct marketing and processing
- **Objection Procedures**: Procedures for handling objections
- **Response Time**: Response within one month of objection
- **Continued Processing**: Conditions for continued processing after objection

### Rights in Relation to Automated Decision Making and Profiling
- **Automated Decision Making**: Information about automated decision-making
- **Profiling Information**: Details about profiling activities
- **Human Intervention**: Right to human intervention
- **Expression of Views**: Right to express views and contest decisions

## Lawful Bases for Processing

### Consent
- **Freely Given**: Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- **Granular Consent**: Separate consent for separate processing purposes
- **Easy Withdrawal**: Easy withdrawal of consent mechanisms
- **Consent Records**: Documentation of consent and withdrawal

### Contract
- **Contractual Necessity**: Processing necessary for contract performance
- **Pre-contractual Steps**: Processing necessary for pre-contractual steps
- **Contract Terms**: Clear contract terms about data processing
- **Contract Documentation**: Documentation of contractual necessity

### Legal Obligation
- **Legal Requirements**: Processing necessary for legal compliance
- **Obligation Identification**: Clear identification of legal obligations
- **Legal Documentation**: Documentation of legal requirements
- **Compliance Measures**: Measures to ensure legal compliance

### Vital Interests
- **Life Protection**: Processing necessary to protect someone's life
- **Incapacity**: Processing when person is physically or legally incapable
- **Emergency Situations**: Emergency medical treatment situations
- **Documentation**: Documentation of vital interest processing

### Public Task
- **Official Authority**: Processing based on official authority
- **Public Interest**: Processing in the public interest
- **Legal Basis**: Clear legal basis for public task processing
- **Transparency**: Transparent communication about public task processing

### Legitimate Interests
- **Interest Assessment**: Three-part legitimate interests assessment
- **Balancing Test**: Balancing interests against individual rights
- **Safeguards**: Appropriate safeguards for legitimate interests
- **Documentation**: Documentation of legitimate interests assessment
