## Breach Notification Rule

### Breach Definition
- **Acquisition**: Unauthorized acquisition of PHI
- **Access**: Unauthorized access to PHI
- **Use**: Unauthorized use of PHI
- **Disclosure**: Unauthorized disclosure of PHI

### Notification Requirements
- **Individual Notification**: Within 60 days of breach discovery
- **HHS Notification**: For breaches affecting 500+ individuals
- **Media Notification**: For breaches affecting 500+ individuals in a state
- **Business Associate Notification**: Immediate notification to covered entity

### Risk Assessment Process
- **Four Factor Assessment**: Nature and extent of PHI involved
- **Unauthorized Use**: Whether PHI was actually acquired or used
- **Risk Mitigation**: Extent to which risk was mitigated
- **Harm Assessment**: Likelihood of re-identification

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Civil Monetary Penalties
- **Tier 1**: $100 - $50,000 per violation (reasonable cause)
- **Tier 2**: $1,000 - $50,000 per violation (reasonable cause not met)
- **Tier 3**: $10,000 - $50,000 per violation (willful neglect corrected)
- **Tier 4**: $50,000+ per violation (willful neglect not corrected)

### Criminal Penalties
- **False Pretenses**: Up to $250,000 and 10 years imprisonment
- **Personal Gain**: Up to $1,000,000 and 10 years imprisonment
- **Wrongful Disclosure**: Up to $250,000 and 5 years imprisonment

### Enforcement Actions
- **HHS Investigations**: HHS Office for Civil Rights investigations
- **State Attorney General**: State-level enforcement actions
- **Private Lawsuits**: Individual lawsuits for HIPAA violations
- **Professional Sanctions**: Professional licensing board actions
