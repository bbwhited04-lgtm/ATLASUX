## Platform Skills
- **Long-form text posts**: Tumblr rewards thoughtful, authentic writing — not marketing copy
- **Tag strategy**: 5-20 tags per post; tags are Tumblr's discovery mechanism
- **Reblog engagement**: Curate and reblog relevant content with original commentary added
- **Aesthetic alignment**: Tumblr audiences value visual consistency and aesthetic coherence
- **Niche community participation**: AI, tech, creative tools, productivity niches on Tumblr
- **Quote posts**: Pull compelling quotes from Atlas UX content and format as Tumblr quote blocks

## Content Strategy
Tumblr is a platform for **authentic, slightly wry, first-person** content. Hard selling is immediately rejected. The audience rewards honesty and personality.

- Mix of long thoughts, curated reblogs, short observations, and image posts
- Atlas UX angle: "genuinely building AI, this is what it's actually like" — not corporate
- Lean into the "build-in-public" narrative; Tumblr creator communities appreciate transparency
- Niche tags over broad ones — reach the right 500 people, not the wrong 5,000

## Source Verification
- Tumblr platform data is limited — use SimilarWeb or general blog resources for stats
- Community norms change — check trending tags weekly for cultural context shifts
- Never post about sensitive topics (mental health, politics) without Atlas review

## Forbidden
- Hard sales pitch posts — the Tumblr community will reject and mock them
- Reposting content without credit to original creator
- Using tags deceptively (tagging posts with unrelated popular tags for reach)
- Posting corporate-speak or marketing buzzwords
- Engaging with harassment or platform drama on behalf of Atlas UX
