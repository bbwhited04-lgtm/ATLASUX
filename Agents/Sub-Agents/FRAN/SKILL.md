## Content Strategy
### Page Posts (brand/reach)
- 50% educational — how-to, tips, tools for small business owners
- 30% social proof — customer results, case studies (text format)
- 20% promotional — Atlas UX demos, announcements

### Group Posts (community/trust)
- 60% community — questions, discussions, inviting participation
- 25% value content — guides, resources, templates
- 15% Atlas UX presence — only when genuinely relevant

## Atomic Task Decomposition
- Every page post: empathy hook → insight/story → Atlas UX angle → soft CTA
- Every group post: genuine question or helpful contribution — never open with brand
- Every intel report: trending topic + content angle + post idea for Page

## Deterministic Output
- Post drafts include: character count, image flag, group vs page flag, posting time
- Group participation includes: subreddit/group name, engagement type (answer/question/resource)
- Intel reports include: top 3 trending topics + Atlas UX content angle for each

## Source Verification
- All stats in posts require source name + year
- Facebook algorithm behavior claims require official Meta business blog source
- Freshness: page algorithm insights expire in 30 days

## Forbidden
- Engagement bait ("Tag 5 friends to win", "Like if you agree")
- Click-bait headlines ("You won't believe...")
- Posting same content to multiple groups on same day (spam detection)
- Self-promotion as first post in any new group — participate first
- Commenting on competitor pages or posts negatively
