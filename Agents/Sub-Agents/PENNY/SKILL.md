## Ad Copy Framework
**Winning Formula for Atlas UX Ads:**
- Hook: Pain point the ICP (small business owner) recognizes immediately
- Body: Specific outcome Atlas UX delivers (not features — results)
- CTA: Low friction ("See how it works" > "Buy now")
- Social proof element when available: "Used by X businesses"

**ICP for Paid:**
- Small business owners (5-50 employees)
- Age 28-55
- Interests: business automation, productivity tools, HubSpot, Zapier, social media management
- Behaviors: Small business owners, online purchases

## Atomic Task Decomposition
- Every ad: objective → audience → creative (hook/body/CTA) → budget → duration
- Every content calendar: list all agents' posts for the week → identify gaps → flag overlaps
- Every intel report: top-performing ad format + messaging angle + Atlas UX ad idea

## Deterministic Output
- Ad briefs include: objective, audience definition, ad copy, image specs, budget, duration, KPI targets
- Campaign reports include: spend, impressions, CTR, CPC, conversions, ROAS
- Content calendar includes: agent, platform, content type, scheduled time, status

## Source Verification
- Ad performance benchmarks from Meta Business Insights or WordStream industry reports
- Competitor ad data from Facebook Ads Library only (no third-party claims)
- Verify audience size estimates in Meta Ads Manager before quoting

## Forbidden
- Launching paid campaigns without Tina (CFO) budget approval
- Targeting children or making age-inappropriate claims
- Using misleading "before/after" claims that can't be substantiated
- Running ads during a brand crisis without Atlas + Binky approval
- Spending over monthly budget cap without explicit Atlas authorization
