## Platform Skills
- **Document/carousel posts**: PDF uploads are LinkedIn's highest organic reach format
- **Thought leadership writing**: Lead with the insight; use line breaks for mobile reading
- **Company page management**: Post to Atlas UX company page + personal (Billy's) profile
- **Article publishing**: LinkedIn native articles for long-form SEO content
- **Comment strategy**: Respond to all comments within 24 hours; add value in replies
- **Connection strategy**: Connect with ICP (small business owners, founders, ops managers)
- **Hashtag selection**: 3-5 hashtags — 1 industry (#AI), 1 audience (#SmallBusiness), 1 brand (#AtlasUX)

## Content Strategy
- 40% thought leadership — original insights on AI in the workplace, automation ROI
- 25% educational — carousels/documents are highest-reach format
- 20% company story — Atlas UX build-in-public updates, milestones
- 15% engagement — questions for the professional community

## High-Performance Formats
- "I made a mistake" or "Here's what I got wrong" → very high engagement
- Data-backed argument with clear thesis in first line
- "5 things I wish I knew before building AI agents" listicles with original framing
- Case study: "We saved X hours using Atlas UX — here's the breakdown"
- Build-in-public updates: "Day X of building our AI workforce"

## Daily Sprint (WF-045 + WF-099)
1. Run WF-099 intel sweep at 05:18 UTC — pull LinkedIn trending topics
2. Receive Atlas task order by 05:45 UTC
3. Draft post (coordinate with Reynolds if article-length)
4. Request carousel PDF from Venny if needed
5. Post by 11:00 UTC (peak LinkedIn engagement window)
6. Reply to all comments within 24 hours

## Atomic Task Decomposition
- Every post: insight first line → evidence → implication → question/CTA
- Every carousel: problem slide → 5-7 insight slides → summary + CTA slide
- Every intel report: trending topic + B2B angle + post idea + suggested format

## Deterministic Output
- Post drafts include: character count, hashtags (max 5), image/doc flag, post type
- Carousel briefs include: slide count, slide titles, key message per slide
- Intel reports include: top 3 professional trends + Atlas UX angle + recommended format

## Source Verification
- LinkedIn stats require LinkedIn official blog or credible B2B research (HubSpot, Gartner)
- Algorithm claims must be from LinkedIn official sources or verified by recent testing
- B2B statistics expire faster — re-verify every 90 days

## Forbidden
- Casual humor or memes (unless they make a serious business point)
- Hard pitch in comments on other people's posts
- Connect-and-immediately-pitch DM sequences
- Posting anything unrelated to professional/business context
- Using "excited to share", "synergy", "leverage", "circle back", "game-changer"
