# Common Workflow Tools — Platform Overview and Selection Guide

## The Workflow Platform Landscape

The workflow automation market has exploded. From simple Zapier zaps to enterprise Workato recipes to AI-native Lindy agents, there's a platform for every use case and budget. This guide covers the 14 most relevant platforms with honest assessments.

## No-Code Automation

### Zapier
**What it is:** The original "connect everything" automation platform. 6,000+ app integrations.
**Best for:** Simple A→B automations. "When a form is submitted, add a row to Google Sheets and send a Slack message."
**Limitations:** Complex branching gets expensive. No self-hosting. Limited error handling.
**Pricing:** Free (100 tasks/mo) → $29.99/mo (750 tasks) → Enterprise

### Make (formerly Integromat)
**What it is:** Visual scenario builder with advanced data manipulation and branching.
**Best for:** Complex multi-step automations with data transformation. More powerful than Zapier for technical users.
**Limitations:** Steeper learning curve. Operations-based pricing can get expensive at scale.
**Pricing:** Free (1,000 ops/mo) → $10.59/mo (10,000 ops) → Enterprise

### Activepieces
**What it is:** Open-source Zapier alternative. Self-hostable. AI-native.
**Best for:** Teams that want Zapier-like ease with self-hosting control and AI-first design.
**Limitations:** Smaller integration library than Zapier/Make. Newer, less battle-tested.
**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted) → $10/mo (cloud) → Enterprise

## Developer-First Automation

### n8n
**What it is:** Open-source workflow automation with visual builder AND code support. Self-hostable.
**Best for:** Technical teams wanting visual workflows with the ability to drop into JavaScript/Python when needed.
**Limitations:** Requires technical skill for advanced use. Self-hosting means you manage infrastructure.
**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted) → $24/mo (cloud) → Enterprise

### Orkes
**What it is:** Enterprise-grade workflow orchestration built on Netflix's Conductor.
**Best for:** Microservice orchestration, complex distributed workflows, AI agent pipelines.
**Limitations:** Enterprise pricing. Overkill for simple automations.
**Pricing:** Contact sales → Enterprise

## Business Process Platforms

### Monday.com
**What it is:** Work operating system with workflow automations, dashboards, and project management.
**Best for:** Teams that want project management + automation in one platform. Visual boards with automated status changes.
**Limitations:** Automations are tied to Monday's ecosystem. Limited external integrations compared to Zapier.
**Pricing:** $12/seat/mo → $19/seat/mo → Enterprise

### ClickUp
**What it is:** All-in-one project management with custom workflow automations.
**Best for:** Teams already using ClickUp for project management wanting to add automations.
**Limitations:** Automation depth is limited compared to dedicated workflow platforms.
**Pricing:** Free → $10/seat/mo → Enterprise

### Kissflow
**What it is:** Purpose-built low-code business process management platform.
**Best for:** Structured business processes: approvals, onboarding, procurement, IT requests.
**Limitations:** Less flexible than code-based solutions. Limited AI capabilities.
**Pricing:** $1,500/mo (50 users) → Enterprise

### Notion
**What it is:** All-in-one workspace with databases, docs, and workflow automation.
**Best for:** Knowledge-driven workflows where documentation and process live together.
**Limitations:** Automation capabilities are basic compared to dedicated platforms. Better for organizing than orchestrating.
**Pricing:** Free → $12/seat/mo → Enterprise

## AI-Native Platforms

### Lindy
**What it is:** AI employee platform. Build AI agents that execute workflows autonomously.
**Best for:** Creating AI agents for customer support, sales outreach, scheduling, data processing.
**Limitations:** Newer platform. AI quality depends on prompt engineering.
**Pricing:** Free trial → $49/mo → Enterprise

### Gumloop
**What it is:** Visual AI workflow builder. Drag-and-drop LLM pipeline construction.
**Best for:** Building AI-powered workflows with LLM steps, data extraction, classification.
**Limitations:** AI-specific — not a general-purpose automation tool.
**Pricing:** Free tier → Usage-based → Enterprise

## Enterprise Integration

### Workato
**What it is:** Enterprise iPaaS with 1,000+ connectors and enterprise-grade security.
**Best for:** Large organizations needing SOC 2 compliant integrations with SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow.
**Limitations:** Enterprise pricing ($10K+/year minimum). Overkill for small teams.
**Pricing:** Enterprise (contact sales)

### Adobe Workfront
**What it is:** Work management for marketing and creative teams. Part of Adobe Creative Cloud.
**Best for:** Marketing teams managing content creation, approval, and publication workflows.
**Limitations:** Adobe ecosystem lock-in. Expensive for small teams.
**Pricing:** Contact sales (typically $30-50/user/mo)

### Box
**What it is:** Cloud content management with workflow, security, and compliance.
**Best for:** Document-centric workflows with heavy compliance requirements (healthcare, legal, financial).
**Limitations:** Focused on content/documents — not a general automation platform.
**Pricing:** $20/user/mo → Enterprise

## Quick Selection Guide

| Your Situation | Recommended Platform |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Solo founder, simple automations | Zapier or Make |
| Technical team, want control | n8n (self-hosted) |
| Building AI agents | Lindy or Gumloop |
| Enterprise with compliance needs | Workato or Box |
| Marketing/creative team | Adobe Workfront or Monday |
| Structured business processes | Kissflow |
| Already using project management | ClickUp or Monday |
| Open-source requirement | n8n or Activepieces |
