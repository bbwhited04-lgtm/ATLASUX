# Runway ML — Best Use Case Ratings

Rating scale: **Excellent** (best-in-class) | **Good** (strong, reliable results) | **Fair** (usable with effort) | **Poor** (not recommended)

## Use Case Ratings

| Use Case | Rating | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Cinematic Stills | **Excellent** | Runway's core strength. The video-trained model produces film-quality frames with natural depth of field, accurate lighting, and cinematic composition. Anamorphic lens simulation, film grain, and color grading prompts all work exceptionally well. No other image generator matches Runway for frames that look like they were pulled from a shot film. |
| Storyboards | **Excellent** | Purpose-built for pre-production workflows. Generate storyboard frames, immediately test them in motion via image-to-video, and iterate on composition. Style consistency across panels via Custom Styles makes multi-frame storyboard sequences practical. The direct pipeline from still frame to animated sequence is unique to Runway. |
| VFX Concepts | **Excellent** | Matte painting style, environment concepts, creature design, and set extension previsualization all perform at professional quality. The model understands volumetric lighting, atmospheric effects, and material rendering at the level required for VFX concept approval meetings. Reference image support helps maintain design continuity across concept iterations. |
| Film / Video Frames | **Excellent** | Generating keyframes, title cards, and visual references for film and video projects is Runway's natural habitat. The output inherently carries the temporal awareness of a video model — frames feel dynamic and directional rather than static. Useful for pitch decks, visual treatments, and mood boards in film pre-production. |
| Product Photography | **Good** | Produces clean, well-lit product shots with accurate material rendering (metal, glass, matte surfaces). Studio lighting setups are well understood. Falls short of Midjourney for photorealistic product detail at extreme close-up, and text on product packaging remains unreliable. Best for hero shots and lifestyle product contexts rather than technical catalog imagery. |
| Marketing / Advertising | **Good** | Strong for campaign concept visualization, mood boards, and creative direction exploration. Lifestyle imagery, brand aesthetic development, and ad creative prototyping work well. The Custom Styles system helps maintain brand consistency. Weaker for high-volume ad variant production due to limited batch generation and the credit cost of iterating across many variations. |
| Portraits | **Good** | Produces natural, well-composed portraits with accurate skin tones and believable lighting. Excels at cinematic and editorial portrait styles (dramatic lighting, film-look color grading). Less precise than Midjourney for hyperrealistic portrait detail. Occasional issues with fine hair rendering and edge artifacts. Reference image support helps maintain character consistency across multiple portrait generations. |
| Abstract Art | **Fair** | Runway can produce abstract compositions, but the model's strength in photorealistic and cinematic rendering means abstract prompts often pull toward representational elements. Pure geometric abstraction, color field work, and non-representational compositions are possible but require careful prompting to prevent the model from introducing recognizable objects. Midjourney and DALL-E handle abstract concepts more intuitively. |
| Editorial Photography | **Good** | Performs well for fashion editorial, documentary-style, and magazine feature imagery. Responds accurately to publication-specific aesthetic references (Vogue, National Geographic). The model's understanding of camera specifications (Leica, Hasselblad, specific lenses) influences output in meaningful ways. Strong for mood and concept development; less reliable for the precise compositional control that art directors require for final publication. |
| Architectural Visualization | **Fair** | Produces acceptable architectural renders for early-stage concept exploration and mood boards. Interior and exterior scenes with dramatic lighting work reasonably well. However, the model struggles with precise geometric accuracy, straight lines, and architectural detailing that archviz professionals require. Dedicated architectural visualization tools (or Midjourney with --style raw) produce more technically accurate results. Runway is better suited for the emotional and atmospheric qualities of architectural spaces than for technical representation. |

## Ideal User Profiles

**Best fit:**
- Film and video production teams needing concept art that feeds into animation
- Creative agencies producing multi-format campaigns (stills + video)
- Storyboard artists and pre-visualization specialists
- Brand teams requiring consistent visual identity across large asset sets

**Acceptable fit:**
- Marketing teams producing campaign imagery alongside video content
- Editorial and publishing teams exploring visual directions
- Product teams prototyping visual concepts before traditional photoshoots

**Not ideal fit:**
- Users needing only standalone image generation (Midjourney is better value)
- Businesses requiring high-volume batch image production (limited automation)
- Architectural firms needing precise technical visualization
- Artists working primarily in abstract or non-representational styles
- Small businesses needing simple, occasional image generation (DALL-E via ChatGPT is simpler)
