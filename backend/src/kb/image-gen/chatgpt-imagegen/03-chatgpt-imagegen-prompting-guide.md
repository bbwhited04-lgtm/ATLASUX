# ChatGPT Image Generation — Prompting Guide with 10 Examples

## How Prompting Differs from Other Generators

ChatGPT's native image generation accepts natural language — the same way you would talk to a designer. You do not need special syntax, weighted tokens, negative prompts, or technical parameters. The model understands conversational English, can handle follow-up instructions, and maintains context across turns. This makes it the most accessible image generator for non-technical users, but it also means skilled prompters can achieve excellent results through precise, descriptive language rather than arcane syntax.

## The Multi-Turn Advantage

Every example below includes follow-up turns to demonstrate iterative refinement. This is the single most important capability that separates ChatGPT image generation from standalone tools like Midjourney or Stable Diffusion.

---

### 1. Conversational Image Creation — Business Scene

**Turn 1:**
> "Create an image of a friendly female receptionist answering a phone at a modern plumbing company office. The desk has a small plant, a computer monitor, and a branded coffee mug. Warm lighting, professional but approachable."

**Turn 2:**
> "The office looks great. Can you add a window behind her showing a sunny suburban neighborhood? And make her headset wireless instead of a desk phone."

**Turn 3:**
> "Perfect. Now remove the plant and replace it with a framed 5-star Google review on the desk."

---

### 2. Iterative Refinement — Logo Design

**Turn 1:**
> "Design a logo for 'FastDrain Plumbing' — a modern plumbing company. The logo should include a stylized water drop and a wrench. Use blue and silver as the primary colors. Clean, vector-style flat design."

**Turn 2:**
> "Good concept. Make the wrench gold instead of silver, and move the company name below the icon instead of beside it."

**Turn 3:**
> "Now make the water drop more angular and geometric — less rounded and organic. And increase the font weight on 'FastDrain' so it reads better at small sizes."

**Turn 4:**
> "Give me a version on a dark navy background with white text for the company name."

---

### 3. Ghibli / Anime Style Transformation

**Turn 1:**
> "Create a Studio Ghibli-style illustration of a small-town HVAC repair shop on a warm summer day. Include a friendly technician loading tools into a van parked outside. Soft watercolor lighting, lush green trees, puffy cumulus clouds. The scene should feel warm, nostalgic, and inviting — like a frame from Spirited Away but set in modern suburban America."

**Turn 2:**
> "Beautiful. Add a calico cat sleeping on the shop's front step, and put a hand-painted sign above the door that reads 'Cool Breeze HVAC'."

**Turn 3:**
> "Make the sky more golden — like late afternoon golden hour. And add a few fireflies starting to appear near the trees."

### 4. Professional Headshot Enhancement

**Turn 1:**
> "Generate a professional corporate headshot of a middle-aged man in a navy blue suit with a subtle gray tie. He has short brown hair, a friendly smile, and light stubble. The background is a soft gradient from light gray to white. Studio lighting with a slight catchlight in the eyes."

**Turn 2:**
> "Great, but make him look more approachable — slightly wider smile, and relax the posture a bit. Less 'corporate CEO' and more 'trusted advisor'."

**Turn 3:**
> "Now generate the same person but in a more casual setting — button-down shirt with no tie, arms crossed, standing in front of a blurred modern office interior."

### 5. Infographic with Text and Data

**Turn 1:**
> "Create an infographic titled 'Why Trade Businesses Miss Revenue' with three sections stacked vertically: (1) '62% of calls go unanswered after hours' with a phone icon, (2) 'Each missed call costs $200-500 in lost revenue' with a dollar sign icon, (3) 'AI receptionists capture 98% of calls 24/7' with a robot icon. Use a clean blue and white color scheme with sans-serif fonts."

**Turn 2:**
> "The text is clear but the icons feel too generic. Make the phone icon a ringing smartphone, the dollar sign a stack of bills, and the robot icon more friendly — like a headset-wearing assistant rather than a mechanical robot."

**Turn 3:**
> "Add a footer bar at the bottom that reads 'CallLucy.ai — Your 24/7 AI Receptionist' in a slightly darker blue."

### 6. Product Mockup with Readable Text

**Turn 1:**
> "Create a product mockup of a white coffee mug on a clean wooden desk. The mug has text on it that reads 'World's Best Plumber' in a bold navy serif font. Morning light coming from the left side, soft shadow on the right. A few coffee beans scattered near the base of the mug."

**Turn 2:**
> "The text rendered well. Now change it to read 'I Fix What Your Husband Broke' in a playful handwritten-style font. Keep everything else the same."

**Turn 3:**
> "Now show the same mug but as a product photo on a pure white background — e-commerce style, no desk, no beans. Just the mug with a subtle drop shadow."

### 7. Meme Generation

**Turn 1:**
> "Create a meme in the classic 'distracted boyfriend' format but illustrated (not using real photos). The boyfriend is labeled 'Homeowner', the girlfriend he's walking with is labeled 'Calling back tomorrow', and the other woman he's looking at is labeled 'AI receptionist that answers NOW'. Cartoon style, bright colors, bold Impact font labels."

**Turn 2:**
> "Funny. Now make the characters look more like tradespeople — the boyfriend in a hard hat, the girlfriend in office clothes, and the 'other woman' wearing a headset with a friendly smile."

**Turn 3:**
> "Add a small logo watermark in the bottom right corner that says 'CallLucy.ai' in small white text."

### 8. Photo Editing via Conversation

**Turn 1:**
> "Generate a photo-realistic image of a suburban house with a 'For Sale' sign in the front yard. Red brick exterior, white trim, green lawn, blue sky. The house should look like a typical $350K American home — two stories, attached garage, mature trees."

**Turn 2:**
> "Now change the 'For Sale' sign to say 'SOLD' with a red banner across it. Add a happy family of four standing in the driveway — parents and two kids — celebrating."

**Turn 3:**
> "Remove the family but keep the SOLD sign. Change the season from summer to autumn — add fall foliage colors to the trees, some leaves on the lawn, and make the sky slightly overcast."

### 9. Brand Asset Creation

**Turn 1:**
> "Create a brand color palette card for a business called 'Atlas UX'. Show five color swatches in a horizontal row: deep navy (#1a2332), electric blue (#3b82f6), clean white (#ffffff), slate gray (#64748b), and accent gold (#f59e0b). Below each swatch, display the hex code. Modern, minimal design on a light background."

**Turn 2:**
> "Now create a set of four app icons using this color palette — one for 'Dashboard' (grid icon), one for 'Messages' (chat bubble), one for 'Calendar' (calendar), and one for 'Settings' (gear). All icons should use the navy background with white or gold icon elements, rounded square shape."

**Turn 3:**
> "Make the icons slightly more rounded — more iOS-style radius. And add a subtle gradient to the navy background going from the deep navy at top-left to a slightly lighter navy at bottom-right."

### 10. Architectural Concept Visualization

**Turn 1:**
> "Create an architectural concept rendering of a modern single-story commercial building for a plumbing supply showroom. Floor-to-ceiling glass storefront, flat roof with a slight overhang, exposed steel beams visible through the glass. The exterior is a mix of charcoal metal cladding and warm wood accents. Landscaped entrance with low desert plants. Late afternoon lighting."

**Turn 2:**
> "Good sense of scale. Add a parking lot in front with about 8 spaces. Put a large illuminated sign on the facade that reads 'PipeMaster Supply Co.' in clean white lettering."

**Turn 3:**
> "Now show the same building at night — interior lights on so the showroom is visible through the glass. The exterior sign is backlit. Add a few cars in the parking lot and warm string lights along the entrance walkway."

## Prompting Tips

1. **Be specific about style:** "photo-realistic," "flat vector," "watercolor," "Studio Ghibli," "architectural rendering" — the model responds well to explicit style direction.
2. **Describe lighting:** Lighting dramatically affects mood. Specify "warm golden hour," "harsh midday," "soft diffused," "dramatic side lighting."
3. **Name colors explicitly:** Use color names or hex codes. "Navy blue" is more reliable than "dark blue."
4. **Use follow-ups for refinement:** Do not try to get everything perfect in one prompt. Start with composition and style, then refine details across turns.
5. **Reference what to keep:** When editing, say "keep everything the same but change X" to avoid the model regenerating from scratch.
6. **Text in images:** For text rendering, use quotes around the exact text you want and specify the font style (serif, sans-serif, handwritten, Impact, etc.).
7. **Composition language:** Use terms like "centered," "rule of thirds," "close-up," "wide shot," "bird's eye view" to control framing.
