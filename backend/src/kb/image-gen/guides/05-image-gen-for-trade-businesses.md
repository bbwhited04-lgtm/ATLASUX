# AI Image Generation for Trade Businesses

This guide is specifically for Atlas UX's core customers: plumbers, HVAC technicians, electricians, salon owners, contractors, and similar trade professionals. Every use case below is something you can implement today to improve your marketing, online presence, and customer communications — without hiring a photographer or graphic designer.

## Why Trade Businesses Need AI Image Generation

Trade businesses face a specific marketing challenge: the work is visual (beautiful bathrooms, clean ductwork, styled hair, remodeled kitchens), but most tradespeople do not have time to photograph their work professionally, and hiring a photographer for every job is not practical.

AI image generation fills this gap:
- Generate professional-looking images of your type of work instantly
- Create seasonal promotional graphics without a designer
- Build a consistent visual brand across all platforms
- Produce content for social media at the volume required to stay visible

**Important note:** AI-generated images should supplement, not replace, real photos of your actual work. Customers want to see your real projects. Use AI images for general marketing, promotional graphics, seasonal content, and placeholders. Use real photos for portfolio and before/after showcases.

## Use Case 1: Before/After Project Visualization

**What:** Show potential customers what a renovation, repair, or installation could look like.

**How it works:** Generate "before" images showing common problems (outdated bathroom, broken HVAC unit, overgrown yard) and "after" images showing the transformed result. Pair them in social posts, proposals, or your website.

**Best platforms:** Midjourney (best photorealism), FLUX Pro (close to Midjourney at lower cost), DALL-E 3 (good with ChatGPT for easy iteration).

**Example prompts:**
- Before: "An outdated 1990s bathroom with beige tile, brass fixtures, yellowed grout, fluorescent lighting, editorial real estate photography"
- After: "A modern renovated bathroom with large format white tile, matte black fixtures, frameless glass shower, warm LED recessed lighting, editorial real estate photography"

**Tips:**
- Keep the room layout and camera angle consistent between before and after by using similar compositional language in both prompts
- Use img2img if available — generate the "before," then use it as input for the "after" with a transformation prompt
- These work exceptionally well as Instagram carousel posts and Facebook ads

## Use Case 2: Social Media Content

**What:** Regular visual content for Instagram, Facebook, Google Business, and other platforms.

**Content types:**
- Tip graphics ("3 Signs Your Water Heater Needs Replacing")
- Seasonal reminders ("Time to schedule your fall HVAC tune-up")
- Holiday greetings with your branding
- Team spotlight backgrounds
- Industry humor/meme-style content

**Best platforms:** FLUX Schnell (speed and cost for volume), Ideogram (when text is needed in the image), Canva Magic Media (all-in-one design and generation).

**Volume strategy:** Trade businesses should post 3-5 times per week on primary platforms. That is 12-20 images per month. Using FLUX Schnell at $0.002/image, the generation cost for a month of content is under $0.50.

**Tips:**
- Create a prompt template for your business and swap out the subject matter each week
- Always generate at the correct aspect ratio: 1:1 for Instagram feed, 9:16 for Stories/Reels, 16:9 for Facebook/LinkedIn/YouTube thumbnails
- Add your logo and contact info in a post-processing step (Canva, Figma, or even PowerPoint) rather than trying to generate it in the image

## Use Case 3: Logo and Brand Creation

**What:** Generate logo concepts, brand color palettes, and visual identity elements.

**Limitations:** AI-generated logos are starting points, not final products. They lack the precision, scalability (vector format), and trademark-readiness of professionally designed logos. Use them for:
- Brainstorming logo directions before hiring a designer
- Temporary branding for a new business that cannot afford a designer yet
- Social media profile images and watermarks

**Best platforms:** Midjourney (strongest design aesthetics), Ideogram (if the logo includes text), DALL-E 3 (easy iteration via ChatGPT).

**Example prompt:**
"A minimalist logo for 'ProFlow Plumbing,' featuring a clean pipe/water drop icon, navy blue and white color scheme, modern sans-serif font, vector style, white background, professional and trustworthy"

**Tips:**
- Generate at 1:1 ratio on a white or transparent background
- Use the output as a reference for a designer to create the final vector version
- For social media avatars, AI-generated logos work well at small sizes even without vectorization

## Use Case 4: Google Business Profile Images

**What:** Images for your Google Business listing — the first thing potential customers see when they search for your services.

**Why it matters:** Businesses with 100+ Google Business photos get 520% more calls than average. Most trade businesses have 5-10 photos. AI generation can help fill the gap.

**Image types to generate:**
- Exterior of a well-maintained service van/truck
- Clean, organized workshop or workspace
- Professional team member in branded uniform (use generic/representative, not specific people)
- Completed project showcases (supplement with real photos)
- Equipment and tools in organized display

**Best platforms:** FLUX Pro or Midjourney for photorealism. Google Business images need to look real.

**Important:** Google's guidelines prohibit "stock photos" and AI-generated images may fall into a gray area. Use AI images for supplementary content (generic service scenarios) and prioritize real photos of your actual team, equipment, and completed work for the core listing.

## Use Case 5: Truck Wrap and Vehicle Mockups

**What:** Visualize how your branding will look on a work vehicle before committing to a $2,000-5,000 wrap.

**How it works:** Generate images of service vans or trucks with your branding, colors, and contact information applied. Share with wrap companies as a reference, or use for social media announcements.

**Best platforms:** DALL-E 3 (decent text rendering on vehicles), Ideogram (best text accuracy), Midjourney (best vehicle photorealism but weaker text).

**Example prompt:**
"A white Ford Transit cargo van with a professional vehicle wrap for 'CoolBreeze HVAC,' blue and white color scheme, phone number '555-COOL' on the side, clean modern design, parked in a suburban driveway, bright daylight, product photography"

**Tips:**
- Text rendering on vehicles is still imperfect — expect to need several generations to get readable text
- Use the best result as a reference for the actual wrap designer rather than a final design
- Generate multiple angles: side view, three-quarter view, rear view

## Use Case 6: Flyer and Print Material Design

**What:** Promotional flyers, door hangers, mailer inserts, and business cards.

**Why AI helps:** A single flyer traditionally costs $50-200 from a graphic designer. AI generation plus a template tool (Canva) can produce professional results for nearly free.

**Workflow:**
1. Generate the hero image for the flyer using AI (the main visual — a happy customer, a beautiful finished project, a seasonal scene)
2. Import into Canva or similar tool
3. Add your text, logo, contact info, and call-to-action using the design tool's text features (do not rely on AI text generation for body copy)
4. Export for print (300 DPI, CMYK if your print shop requires it)

**Best platforms:** Ideogram (if you want text baked into the generated image), FLUX Pro or Midjourney (for the hero image that you will add text to separately).

**Seasonal flyer ideas for trade businesses:**
- Spring: "Spring AC Tune-Up Special — $79"
- Summer: "Beat the Heat — Emergency HVAC Service Available 24/7"
- Fall: "Winterize Your Plumbing Before the First Freeze"
- Winter: "Furnace Not Working? Same-Day Repair Available"
- Holiday: Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year greeting cards with your branding

## Use Case 7: Seasonal Promotional Campaigns

**What:** Visual content tied to seasons, holidays, and weather events — the natural marketing rhythm for trade businesses.

**Why it matters:** Trade businesses are inherently seasonal. HVAC peaks in summer and winter. Plumbing sees frozen pipe emergencies in winter. Landscaping peaks in spring and fall. Aligning your visual content with these cycles makes your marketing feel timely and relevant.

**Batch generation strategy:**
1. At the start of each quarter, generate all promotional images for the next 3 months
2. Use the template batching approach from the Budget Optimization Guide
3. Schedule posts in advance using your social media management tool

**Example quarterly batch for an HVAC company:**
- January: "New Year, New Comfort" — cozy home interior with modern HVAC
- February: "Love Your Home's Air Quality" — Valentine's themed
- March: "Spring Is Coming — Schedule Your AC Checkup" — transition season
- 4 social post images per month = 12 images per quarter
- Cost at FLUX Schnell rates: $0.024 for the entire quarter

## Use Case 8: Customer Proposal Visuals

**What:** Include professional images in customer proposals and estimates to increase close rates.

**How:** When preparing a proposal for a bathroom remodel, kitchen renovation, or HVAC installation, include AI-generated images showing what the finished result could look like. This transforms a dry estimate document into a visual proposal.

**Best platforms:** Midjourney or FLUX Pro (photorealism is essential for credibility in proposals).

**Tips:**
- Match the style of the generated image to the customer's actual space as closely as possible (describe their kitchen layout, their color preferences, their fixtures)
- Label images clearly: "Conceptual visualization — actual results may vary"
- Even rough visual concepts significantly increase proposal acceptance rates because customers can see what they are paying for

## Getting Started Checklist

1. **Pick one platform** to learn first. For most trade businesses, DALL-E 3 via ChatGPT is the easiest starting point (you may already be paying for ChatGPT Plus).
2. **Generate your first 5 images:** one social post, one before/after pair, one seasonal graphic, one logo concept.
3. **Save your best prompts.** Start a simple document (or use the prompt templates in this KB) with prompts that produced good results for your business type.
4. **Post consistently.** Even 2-3 AI-generated social images per week, combined with real project photos, dramatically increases your online visibility.
5. **Track what works.** Note which images get the most engagement and generate more in that style.
6. **Graduate to volume.** Once comfortable, move to API-based generation (FLUX via fal.ai) for batch content creation at minimal cost.
