# Veo 3.1 vs Sora 2: Full Comparison of Length, Consistency, Audio, and Quality

## Key Takeaways
- Veo 3.1 offers 4K at 60fps vs Sora 2's 1080p
- Veo 3.1 supports up to 2-minute clips vs Sora 2's 20-25 seconds
- Both models generate native audio but with different strengths
- Sora 2 wins on physics simulation and multi-shot transitions
- Veo 3.1 wins on resolution, length, and lip synchronization
- Choose based on whether you prioritize cinematic realism (Sora) or production versatility (Veo)

## Pros/Cons by Model

### Veo 3.1
**Pros:**
- 4K resolution at 60fps
- Up to 2-minute video length
- Built-in dialogue, sound effects, and ambient audio synchronized with video
- Excellent controlled lighting and materials rendering
- "Advertising-grade realism" aesthetic
- Better for multi-shot bridging with predictable consistency
- Supports vertical video for social media

**Cons:**
- Slower generation (up to 45 seconds for a 12-second clip)
- Higher cost ($0.20-0.60/sec depending on resolution and audio)
- Requires Google ecosystem subscription
- Can feel less "filmic" and more "commercial" in aesthetic

### Sora 2
**Pros:**
- Faster generation (~30 seconds for 12-second video)
- Superior physics simulation
- More naturally flowing physics in standalone clips
- Better multi-shot transitions with smooth, seamless cuts
- Slightly softer, more filmic aesthetic
- Better for "authentic" feeling content

**Cons:**
- Limited to 1080p resolution
- Maximum 20-25 second clips
- More expensive subscription ($200/month for Pro)
- Less predictable for bridging multiple reference frames
