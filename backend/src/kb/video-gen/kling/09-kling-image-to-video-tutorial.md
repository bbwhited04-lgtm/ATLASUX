## Key Takeaways

- Image-to-video turns static images into dynamic video by adding movement and animation
- Key features: Start/End frame, Motion Brush, Camera Movement controls
- Prompts should focus on subject and movement (scene is already provided by the image)
- High-quality input images produce significantly better results
- Motion Brush allows drawing specific motion paths on image elements
- Static Brush locks areas that should not move

## Content

### Overview

Kling AI's image-to-video feature transforms static images into dynamic, high-quality videos. The AI analyzes the input image and generates realistic motion for elements within the scene -- flowing water, swaying trees, animated characters, moving vehicles, and more.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

1. **Upload or Generate Image**
   - Upload an existing high-resolution image
   - Or generate one using Kling's AI image generator
   - Supported formats: JPG, PNG, WebP
   - Recommended: at least 1024x1024 resolution

2. **Write Your Prompt**
   - Focus on describing the MOVEMENT, not the scene (the image provides the scene)
   - Specify what actions characters should perform
   - Describe direction and speed of motion
   - Keep it concise -- 1-2 sentences is often enough

3. **Configure Settings**
   - Select model version (Kling 3.0 recommended)
   - Choose duration (3-15 seconds)
   - Select quality mode (Standard or Professional)
   - Set aspect ratio (auto-detected from image or override)

4. **Apply Motion Controls** (optional)
   - Motion Brush: Draw paths on specific elements
   - Camera Movement: Set pan, tilt, zoom, etc.
   - Start/End Frame: Define beginning and ending states

5. **Generate and Download**
   - Click Generate to start processing
   - Wait for generation (30 seconds to several minutes)
   - Preview and download the result

### Motion Brush

The Motion Brush is Kling's most powerful image-to-video tool:

**How It Works:**
- Select an element in the image by painting over it
- Draw an arrow or path showing the direction of movement
- Adjust the strength/speed of the motion
- Apply to multiple elements independently

**Static Brush:**
- Paint over areas that should NOT move
- Essential for keeping backgrounds stable while animating foreground elements
- Prevents unwanted camera drift or background warping

**Best Uses:**
- Animate a character's arm wave or head turn
- Make water flow in a specific direction
- Create wind effects on hair or clothing
- Move vehicles along a road
- Open/close doors, windows

### Start/End Frame Mode

Define the beginning and ending states of your video:

- **Start Frame Only**: Upload an image as the starting point, let AI determine the ending
- **End Frame Only**: Upload where you want to end up, let AI figure out the beginning
- **Both Frames**: Upload start and end images, AI generates the transition between them

This is powerful for:
- Morphing effects between two states
- Predictable camera movements (same scene, different angle)
- Before/after transformations

### Camera Movement Controls

Available camera movements for image-to-video:
- **Pan Left/Right**: Horizontal camera sweep
- **Tilt Up/Down**: Vertical camera movement
- **Zoom In/Out**: Push toward or pull away from subject
- **Dolly**: Physical camera movement toward/away (different depth effect than zoom)
- **Orbit**: Camera circles around the subject
- **Custom Path**: Define precise camera trajectory

### Prompt Writing for Image-to-Video

**Do:**
- Describe the ACTION: "The woman turns her head to the left and smiles"
- Specify speed: "slowly walks," "quickly turns," "gently sways"
- Include environmental motion: "wind blows the curtains," "rain falls on the window"

**Don't:**
- Repeat the scene description (the image already provides this)
- Write overly long prompts (2-4 sentences max)
- Contradict what's in the image (don't say "red dress" if the image shows blue)

### Input Image Quality Tips

1. **Resolution matters**: Higher resolution = better output
2. **Sharp focus**: Avoid blurry or noisy input images
3. **Good lighting**: Well-lit images produce more natural animation
4. **Clear subjects**: Distinct subjects are easier for the model to animate
5. **Stable composition**: Images with clear foreground/background separation work best
6. **AI-generated images**: Kling-generated images often work best with Kling's video model (same latent space)

### Common Use Cases

- **Product photography to product video**: Animate product shots for e-commerce
- **Portrait to talking head**: Animate a photo into a speaking character
- **Landscape animation**: Add motion to nature photography
- **Illustration animation**: Bring artwork and illustrations to life
- **Architectural visualization**: Animate building renders with people and vehicles
- **Social media content**: Transform static posts into engaging video content
