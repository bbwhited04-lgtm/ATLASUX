## Content

### What Makes Omni Different

Unlike previous versions that processed audio and video as separate layers, the Omni model generates synchronized audio and video natively. Both emerge from the same generation pass rather than being composited afterward. This means:

- Lip movements perfectly match spoken dialogue
- Sound effects are temporally aligned with visual events
- Ambient audio matches the environment being rendered
- Music and rhythm sync with visual pacing

### Audio Capabilities

Generate synchronized voiceovers, dialogue, sound effects, and ambient audio directly with your video -- all in one pass with perfect lip sync.

**Supported Audio Types:**
- Spoken dialogue (multiple languages)
- Sound effects (footsteps, doors, impacts, nature)
- Ambient atmosphere (city noise, forest, ocean)
- Music and singing
- Character-specific voices

**Language Support:**
- Chinese (Mandarin, Cantonese, regional dialects)
- English (American, British, Australian accents)
- Japanese
- Korean
- Spanish (Castilian, Latin American variants)

### Character Consistency with Voice

Kling 3.0 Omni supports multi-image character creation with voice input:

1. Upload multiple images of your character (1-4 reference images)
2. Provide a voice clip longer than 3 seconds
3. The model binds visual identity and voice together
4. Lip sync becomes more accurate
5. Expressions feel more alive
6. Storytelling gains emotional depth

### Elements 3.0 Omni (Advanced)

For even stronger consistency, Elements 3.0 Omni allows:
- Video character references of 3-8 seconds
- Extraction of both visual traits and voice characteristics simultaneously
- Consistent character across multiple generations
- Voice preservation across different scenes and prompts

### Multi-Character Scenes

You can create scenes where:
- Each character speaks in a different language or dialect
- Characters have distinct voices and speaking styles
- Dialogue exchanges are naturally paced
- Turn-taking in conversation feels organic

### Technical Specifications

- **Resolution**: Native 1080p and 4K
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps with 16-bit HDR color
- **Duration**: Up to 15 seconds per generation
- **Audio Channels**: Stereo output
- **Audio Sample Rate**: High-fidelity (platform-dependent)

### Best Practices for Audio Generation

1. **Be specific about sound**: "The wooden door creaks open slowly" rather than just "door opens"
2. **Describe ambient environment**: "Quiet library with distant page-turning and soft air conditioning hum"
3. **Specify language and accent for dialogue**: "The character speaks in British English with a warm, conversational tone"
4. **Use onomatopoeia when helpful**: "The rain patters against the window" gives the model audio cues
5. **Layer audio descriptions**: Describe foreground sounds, dialogue, and ambient sounds separately in your prompt
