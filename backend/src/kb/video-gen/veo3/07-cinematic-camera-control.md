# Mastering Veo 3: Expert Guide to Prompt Structure and Cinematic Camera Control

## Key Takeaways

- Veo 3 interprets professional cinematography terms with high accuracy
- Prompt structure should mirror a director's shot description
- Camera movement choice dramatically affects the emotional tone of the video
- Combining multiple camera techniques in one prompt is possible but requires careful ordering
- Film stock and lens references (35mm, anamorphic) significantly affect visual style

## Content

### Optimal Prompt Structure (Director's Format)

Think of your prompt as a director briefing a cinematographer:

```
[SHOT TYPE] + [CAMERA MOVEMENT] + [LENS/FILM LOOK] +
[SUBJECT DESCRIPTION] + [ACTION] +
[ENVIRONMENT + LIGHTING] +
[AUDIO DESCRIPTION] +
[MOOD/STYLE REFERENCE]
```

### Camera Movement Reference

#### Smooth Movements (Calm, Professional)
- **Dolly in/out** -- "Slow dolly push toward the subject's face"
- **Track left/right** -- "Camera tracks alongside the subject as they walk"
- **Crane up** -- "Crane rises from ground level to reveal the cityscape"
- **Steadicam follow** -- "Steadicam follows behind the subject through the hallway"
- **Slider shot** -- "Smooth lateral slider revealing the landscape"

#### Dynamic Movements (Energy, Tension)
- **Whip pan** -- "Whip pan from the speaker to the audience reaction"
- **Handheld shake** -- "Handheld camera follows the subject running"
- **Quick zoom** -- "Snap zoom into the subject's eyes"
- **Orbit/arc** -- "Camera orbits 180 degrees around the subject"

#### Dramatic Movements (Suspense, Impact)
- **Dolly zoom (Vertigo effect)** -- "Dolly zoom creates a disorienting vertigo effect"
- **Dutch angle** -- "Dutch angle tilts 15 degrees, creating unease"
- **Low angle push** -- "Low angle camera pushes forward, making the subject loom large"
- **Crash zoom** -- "Crash zoom into the detail on the desk"

### Lens and Film Stock References

Veo 3 responds well to specific lens and film references:

- **"Shot on 35mm film"** -- Organic grain, warm tones, slightly soft
- **"Anamorphic lens"** -- Cinematic widescreen, oval bokeh, horizontal flares
- **"50mm prime lens"** -- Natural perspective, moderate depth of field
- **"85mm portrait lens"** -- Compressed background, beautiful subject isolation
- **"Wide-angle 24mm"** -- Expansive, slight distortion at edges
- **"Macro lens extreme close-up"** -- Tiny details fill the frame
- **"Vintage Cooke lens"** -- Warm, organic, classic cinema look
- **"IMAX format"** -- Massive scale, incredible detail

### Lighting Terminology

| Term | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Golden hour | Warm, low-angle sunlight |
| Blue hour | Cool, twilight tones |
| Chiaroscuro | Dramatic light/shadow contrast |
| Rim lighting | Edge light separating subject from background |
| Practical lighting | Light from sources visible in scene |
| Motivated lighting | Justified by a visible or implied source |
| High-key | Bright, even, minimal shadows |
| Low-key | Dark, dramatic, deep shadows |
| Dappled light | Light filtered through leaves/blinds |
| Neon glow | Colorful artificial light sources |

### Example Prompts Using Film Language

**Thriller Scene:**
> Dutch angle close-up, shot on 35mm with high grain. A man in a dark suit sits alone at a dimly lit bar. A single practical light overhead casts harsh shadows across his face. He slowly lifts a glass, pauses, and says in a low voice, "They know." The ice clinks in the glass. Tense, minimal string score builds underneath. Low-key chiaroscuro lighting. Smoke drifts through the frame.

**Commercial/Lifestyle:**
> Steadicam tracking shot, anamorphic lens with natural flares. A young couple walks hand-in-hand through a sunlit European street market. Warm golden hour lighting bathes the scene. Shallow depth of field keeps the background a soft bokeh of colorful market stalls. Ambient sounds of market chatter, distant accordion music. The woman laughs and points at something off-screen. Warm, nostalgic color grading.

**Epic/Documentary:**
> Drone crane shot rising from ground level, IMAX format. A lone figure stands at the edge of a vast canyon at sunrise. The camera rises slowly, revealing the enormous scale of the landscape. Dramatic rim lighting silhouettes the figure. Wind sounds intensify as the camera gains altitude. A deep, resonant orchestral note swells. Cinematic color grading with deep oranges and cool shadows.

### Combining Multiple Camera Techniques

You can chain camera movements within a single prompt, but keep it logical:

> The shot begins as a close-up on trembling hands, then a slow dolly pulls back to reveal the full figure standing at the edge of a rooftop, finishing with a crane up to show the sprawling city below.

**Rules for chaining:**
1. Use temporal language: "begins as," "then," "transitions to," "finishing with"
2. Maximum 2-3 movements per 8-second clip
3. Ensure movements flow naturally -- no jarring transitions
4. Each movement should serve the narrative purpose
