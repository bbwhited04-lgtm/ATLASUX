## Key Takeaways
- The AI video generation landscape has matured from experimental to production-ready
- Picking the best model is harder than ever with distinct use case categories
- The gap between the best and rest is widening

- Seedance 2.0 has an identity-lock system for consistent characters
- Multi-model workflows are becoming the professional standard

## Pros/Cons by Model

### Sora 2
**Pros:**
- Film-grade cinematic quality
- Olympic gymnastics routines with realistic physics
- Accurate backflips on paddleboards with realistic buoyancy dynamics
- Figure skating triple axels with proper physics
- Best at understanding complex prompts with multiple subjects, camera movements, and lighting

**Cons:**
- Limited availability
- Higher pricing tier
- 1080p resolution ceiling

### Veo 3.1
**Pros:**
- Native 4K output
- Character consistency
- Vertical video support
- $19.99/month entry (Gemini Advanced)
- Added native dialogue generation

**Cons:**
- Full features require higher tier subscription
- Less accessible API

### Kling 3.0
**Pros:**
- Visual fidelity at 8.4/10 (top of benchmarks)
- Multi-language audio output
- Subject consistency across shots
- Free tier availability
- Crisp, temporally consistent output

**Cons:**
- Less sophisticated physics than Sora
- Some prompt interpretation limitations

### Seedance 2.0
**Pros:**
- Generates synchronized sound alongside visual output
- Identity-lock system for character consistency
- Multi-modal input support
- Good for template-based workflows

**Cons:**
- Newer entrant still building ecosystem
- Less documentation and community resources

## Content

This complete comparison guide covers the full landscape of AI video generation in 2026, focusing on the four major contenders.
