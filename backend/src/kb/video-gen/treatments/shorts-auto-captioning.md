## Content

### Why Captions Matter for Shorts

In 2026, captions are one of the most important drivers of retention and engagement on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Key reasons:

1. **Mute viewing:** 80%+ of social video is watched without sound initially
2. **Retention boost:** Videos with well-styled subtitles see significantly higher completion rates
3. **Accessibility:** Required for deaf/hard-of-hearing viewers; also helps non-native speakers
4. **SEO/Discovery:** Platform algorithms can index caption text for search and recommendations
5. **Dual-channel reinforcement:** Text + audio together increases comprehension and recall

### Caption Styles for Shorts

| Style | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Word-by-word highlight** | Each word lights up as spoken | High energy, motivational, educational |
| **Phrase-by-phrase** | 2-4 word chunks appear together | Conversational, storytelling |
| **Karaoke-style** | Words change color as spoken | Music-heavy content, trend clips |
| **Pop-up animated** | Words bounce, scale, or slide in | Entertainment, comedy |
| **Minimal subtitle** | Clean bottom-third text | Professional, tutorial |
| **Emoji-enhanced** | Relevant emojis auto-inserted | Casual, lifestyle, reaction |
| **Keyword emphasis** | Key terms bolded or colored differently | Educational, how-to |

### Core Technologies

**OpenAI Whisper (ASR Engine)**
- Open-source speech recognition model
- Supports 50+ languages
- Powers many third-party captioning tools (ZapCap, Kapwing, etc.)
- Can be self-hosted for API-level integration
- Accuracy: ~95-97% out of the box, higher with fine-tuning

**Platform-Native Auto-Captions**
- YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram all offer built-in auto-captions
- Generally lower quality and fewer styling options than dedicated tools
- No animated highlighting or emoji support
- Good as a fallback but not for professional output

### Caption Best Practices

1. **Font:** Bold, sans-serif (Montserrat, Poppins, or platform-specific trending fonts)
2. **Size:** Large enough to read on mobile (minimum 40px at 1080p)
3. **Position:** Center of frame or lower-third, always within safe zone
4. **Contrast:** White text with black outline/shadow, or colored background bar
5. **Animation:** Word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase reveal synced to speech
6. **Max words:** 2-4 words visible at a time for readability
7. **Emphasis:** Bold or color-change key words (nouns, numbers, action words)
8. **Review:** Always manually review for proper nouns, brand names, and technical terms

## Integration Notes for Atlas UX
- Venny/Victor treatment pipeline should auto-generate captions as a default step, never skip
- Primary ASR: Use OpenAI Whisper API (already have OpenAI key) for transcription, then apply styling in post
- Caption style should be a treatment parameter: `caption_style: "word_highlight" | "phrase" | "karaoke" | "minimal" | "emoji"`
- Store caption timing data as SRT/VTT alongside the video asset for re-styling without re-transcription
- Consider ZapCap API ($8/mo) as a fallback/comparison for Whisper accuracy
- Caption placement must respect per-platform safe zones from `shorts-platform-specs.md`
- Auto-review step: flag captions containing "[inaudible]" or low-confidence segments for human review
- Brand glossary: maintain a per-tenant dictionary of proper nouns (business name, service names) to improve ASR accuracy
- Bulk processing: captions should be generated in batch for all clips from a single long-form source
