# Sora 2 vs Kling AI: Which Delivers More Realistic Videos?
**Source:** https://crepal.ai/blog/aivideo/sora-2-vs-kling-ai-realism/
**Author:** CrePal
**Date:** 2025-2026
**Models Compared:** Sora 2, Kling AI

## Key Takeaways
- Sora 2 focuses on world physics; Kling focuses on human physics
- Kling's 3D body reconstruction achieved 94% anatomical accuracy vs Sora 2's 89%
- Sora 2 has the edge in general physics simulation (fluids, collisions, gravity)
- Kling excels at complex human actions without body distortion
- For unfiltered realism of the physical world, Sora 2 remains the benchmark
- Kling dominates the mass-market and value segments

## Pros/Cons by Model

### Sora 2
**Pros:**
- Superior general physics simulation (fluids, collisions, gravity)
- Multi-object momentum conservation
- Better at simulating environmental interactions
- Unmatched for scenes requiring physical world realism
- Higher precision in complex dynamic scenes

**Cons:**
- 89% anatomical accuracy (vs Kling's 94%)
- More expensive
- Less accessible
- Not as strong at human-centric content

### Kling AI
**Pros:**
- 94% anatomical accuracy (compared to real motion capture data)
- Excels at Kung Fu, dancing, running without "spaghetti limbs"
- Better human body rendering
- More affordable (~$0.10/sec)
- Better accessibility with free tier
- Dominates mass-market and value segments

**Cons:**
- Less precise with environmental physics
- Object interactions less sophisticated than Sora
- Multi-object momentum less accurate

## Content

This article focuses specifically on the realism dimension of AI video generation, testing Sora 2 and Kling AI with prompts designed to stress-test physical accuracy and human motion rendering. The key insight is that "realism" means different things: Sora 2 excels at world-level physics (how objects interact, how liquids flow, how light behaves), while Kling excels at human-level physics (how bodies move, how muscles flex, how facial expressions change). The 94% vs 89% anatomical accuracy comparison using motion capture data as ground truth is particularly revealing. The article recommends choosing based on whether your content is environment-focused (Sora) or human-focused (Kling).
