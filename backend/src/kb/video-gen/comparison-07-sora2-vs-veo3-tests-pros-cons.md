# Sora 2 vs Veo 3: Tests, Pros/Cons & Which One to Choose
**Source:** https://skywork.ai/blog/sora-2-vs-veo-3-tests-pros-cons-which-one-to-choose/
**Author:** Skywork AI
**Date:** 2025-2026
**Models Compared:** Sora 2, Veo 3, Runway Gen-3

## Key Takeaways
- Sora 2 shines with multi-shot consistency
- Veo 3 excels at audio-visual integration and creative control
- Sora 2 has faster generation times
- Veo 3 offers higher resolution output
- The choice depends on whether you prioritize physics realism or production polish
- Both models represent a major leap from previous generations

## Pros/Cons by Model

### Sora 2
**Pros:**
- Multi-shot consistency with uniform characters and lighting across angle switches
- Unparalleled understanding of light, shadow, and object interaction
- Faster generation (~30 seconds for 12-second video)
- Best at depicting motion, interaction, and environmental physics
- Good for shareable, fun content
- Free access available (with limitations)

**Cons:**
- 1080p maximum resolution
- 20-second maximum length (25 for Pro)
- Less creative control over narrative elements
- No start-end frame conditioning like Veo

### Veo 3
**Pros:**
- Major step forward in creative control with audio and narrative elements
- Users can guide tone, add synchronized dialogue, and layer sound effects
- Realistic candle lighting, smoke effects, and natural-looking human fingers
- Start-frame to end-frame conditioning for precise scene control
- 4K resolution capability
- Up to 2-minute clips

**Cons:**
- Slower processing times
- Higher cost
- Requires Google ecosystem investment
- Less "natural" physics in standalone clips compared to Sora

### Runway Gen-3
**Pros:**
- Affordable entry point ($12/month)
- Motion brushes for fine-grained control
- Strong character persistence

**Cons:**
- No native audio generation
- Lower benchmark scores than Sora 2 or Veo 3

## Content

Skywork AI conducted structured tests comparing Sora 2 and Veo 3 across multiple creative scenarios. The testing methodology included identical prompts for cinematic nature shots, human action sequences, dialogue scenes, and physics-heavy scenarios. The article provides practical advice: Sora 2 is for creators after fun, shareable content, while Veo 3 is a workhorse for professionals. Choose Veo 3.1 for narrative consistency, precise multi-shot control, and integrated audio workflow. Choose Sora 2 for high-impact, cinematically realistic single clips where physical accuracy and stunning visuals are paramount.
