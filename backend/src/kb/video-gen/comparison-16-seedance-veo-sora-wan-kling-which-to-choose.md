# Seedance, Veo, Sora, Wan, Kling, Vidu: Which to Choose
**Source:** https://opencreator.io/blog/ai-video-models-comparison-2026
**Author:** OpenCreator
**Date:** 2026
**Models Compared:** Seedance 2.0, Veo 3.1, Sora 2, Wan 2.6, Kling 3.0, Vidu Q2

## Key Takeaways
- If budget matters most, start with Kling 3.0
- If you need maximum creative input flexibility, choose Seedance 2.0
- If physics realism and longer clips are essential, Sora 2 is your model
- Veo for product and character lock, Sora for mood and realism
- Wan 2.6 leads on cinematic, multi-shot narrative (best if you can plan shots)
- The practical approach is combining multiple models by use case
- While Sora chases physics and Kling chases acting, Wan chases efficiency

## Pros/Cons by Model

### Seedance 2.0
**Pros:**
- Maximum creative input flexibility
- References up to 12 files per generation
- Best for camera tests and multi-modal workflows
- Strong template-based creation

**Cons:**
- Still building ecosystem
- Less community support

### Veo 3.1
**Pros:**
- Best for product videos and character lock
- Broadcast-ready aesthetics at 24fps
- Integrated audio-visual generation
- Enterprise-grade with Google Cloud SLAs

**Cons:**
- Highest cost
- Google ecosystem dependency

### Sora 2
**Pros:**
- Best for mood, atmosphere, and realism
- Sora 2 calculates shatter patterns, liquid physics, and reflections consistently
- Physics realism benchmark leader
- Best for longer clips (up to 25 seconds)

**Cons:**
- Higher per-second cost
- 1080p maximum
- Limited availability

### Wan 2.6
**Pros:**
- Leads on cinematic, multi-shot narrative
- Fastest Time to First Frame (TTFF) in benchmarks
- Open-source (Apache 2.0) and free to self-host
- Best for brand and narrative content
- Most efficient inference engine

**Cons:**
- Requires shot planning for best results
- Quality gap in some edge cases
- Technical setup for self-hosting

### Kling 3.0
**Pros:**
- Best overall value (~$0.50 per 1080p generation)
- Best for IP and dialogue content
- Strong human motion and acting
- Multi-language audio support
- Free tier available

**Cons:**
- Physics less accurate than Sora
- Less precise for complex environmental scenes

### Vidu Q2
**Pros:**
- Best for volume and matrix production
- Fast generation
- Good for batch workflows

**Cons:**
- Less widely known
- Limited English documentation

## Content

OpenCreator's guide takes a practical, use-case-driven approach to choosing between six leading AI video models. Rather than declaring a single winner, the article maps each model to specific production scenarios: Veo for product showcases, Sora for atmospheric realism, Seedance for camera tests, Wan for brand narratives, Kling for character-driven dialogue, and Vidu for high-volume batch production. The key recommendation is that it is no longer about picking one model; the practical approach is combining multiple models by use case, switching by scene or stage to balance quality, cost, and throughput.
