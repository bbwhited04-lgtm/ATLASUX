# Editing Post Drafts

Not every post needs to go out immediately. Atlas UX lets you save posts as drafts so you can review, edit, and polish them before they go live. This is especially useful if you create content in batches or want someone else on your team to review before publishing.

## Finding Your Drafts

All saved drafts live in **Social Media > Drafts**. You'll see a list showing:

- The post text (first few lines)
- A thumbnail of the attached media
- The platforms it's targeted for
- When it was created
- Whether AI-generated or written manually

Click any draft to open it in the editor.

## Editing a Draft

When you open a draft, you're back in the same editor you used to create it. Everything is editable:

### Text Content

Rewrite, shorten, or expand your post copy. If you want a fresh take, click **Regenerate with AI** to get a new version based on the same topic. You can also ask the AI to adjust the tone -- make it more casual, more professional, or more urgent.

### Media

Swap out the image or video. You can:

- Upload a new photo or video
- Pick a different image from your asset library
- Regenerate the AI image with a different prompt
- Add additional images (for carousel posts on Instagram or LinkedIn)

Remember: every post needs at least one image or video. You can't remove all media from a draft. See [Media Requirements](media-requirements.md) for format details.

### Target Platforms

Change which platforms the post will go to. Add or remove platforms before publishing. Each platform shows a small indicator so you can see at a glance where the post will appear.

### Schedule

If the draft was saved without a scheduled time, you can add one now. Or change an existing scheduled time. Options:

- **Publish immediately** when you're ready
- **Pick a specific date and time** -- Atlas publishes automatically
- **Use suggested timing** -- Atlas recommends posting times based on when your audience is most active

## Previewing Your Post

Before publishing, preview how your post will look on each platform. Click **Preview** to see a mockup showing:

- How the text appears (truncated or full, depending on the platform)
- How the media displays (square crop for Instagram, landscape for LinkedIn, etc.)
- Character count and whether you're within the platform's limits
- Hashtag formatting

The preview helps you catch issues before they go live. For example, a caption that looks great on Facebook might be too long for X (280-character limit) and need trimming.

## Bulk Draft Management

If you've created several drafts, you can manage them efficiently:

- **Sort by date** -- Newest first or oldest first
- **Filter by platform** -- Show only drafts targeted for a specific platform
- **Bulk delete** -- Select multiple drafts and remove them
- **Bulk schedule** -- Select several drafts and assign publishing times in sequence (e.g., one per day for the next week)

## Collaboration

If someone else on your team helps with social media -- an office manager, a marketing person, a spouse -- they can review and edit drafts too. Any team member with access to your Atlas UX account can:

- View all drafts
- Edit text and swap media
- Approve and publish
- Add notes or comments on a draft

This makes it easy to split the work: you snap the photos on the job site, and someone back at the office writes the captions and schedules the posts.

## When AI Creates Drafts for You

Atlas UX agents can proactively create draft posts on your behalf. For example, if you log a completed job, Atlas might generate a "job well done" post with a before-and-after template. These appear in your Drafts inbox for review -- they never publish without your approval.

This means you can accumulate a library of ready-to-go posts without sitting down to write them.

## Common Questions

**How long do drafts stay saved?**
Indefinitely. Drafts are never deleted automatically. They stay in your list until you publish or delete them.

**Can I duplicate a draft?**
Yes. Open the draft, click the menu icon, and select **Duplicate**. This creates a copy you can modify -- useful for creating variations of the same post for different platforms.

**What if I edit a draft that's already scheduled?**
Your changes are saved and the updated version will be published at the scheduled time. If you want to pause the schedule, switch it back to "Draft" status.
