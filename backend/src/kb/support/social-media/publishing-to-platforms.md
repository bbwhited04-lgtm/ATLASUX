# Publishing Posts to Platforms

Once your post is ready, Atlas UX handles the publishing. Your content is delivered to each platform through Postiz, a professional social media publishing engine that supports over 30 platforms. You don't interact with Postiz directly -- Atlas UX manages everything behind the scenes.

## Supported Platforms

Atlas UX can publish to all of these platforms:

| Platform    | Content Types              | Notes                                      |
|-------------|----------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| Instagram   | Images, carousels, Reels   | Requires a connected Business account      |
| Facebook    | Images, videos, text posts | Pages and personal profiles                |
| X (Twitter) | Images, videos, text       | 280-character limit for text               |
| TikTok      | Videos                     | Video required; posts as private by default |
| LinkedIn    | Images, carousels, text    | Great for B2B and commercial trades        |
| Reddit      | Images, videos, text       | Choose your subreddit during posting       |
| Pinterest   | Images                     | Requires board selection                   |
| YouTube     | Videos                     | Full videos with title and description     |
| Threads     | Images, text               | Meta's text-based platform                 |
| Mastodon    | Images, videos, text       | Decentralized social network               |
| Tumblr      | Images, videos, text       | Long-form and media posts                  |
| Medium      | Articles with images       | For longer written content                 |

More platforms are added regularly. If you don't see one you need, let us know.

## How Publishing Works

### Immediate Publishing

When you click **Publish Now**:

1. Atlas validates your post (content, media, platform requirements)
2. Your post is sent to each selected platform simultaneously
3. You see a confirmation with the status of each delivery
4. The published post appears in your **Social Media > Published** history

Most posts go live within 10-30 seconds.

### Scheduled Publishing

Prefer to plan ahead? Schedule posts for any future date and time:

1. Create your post normally
2. Instead of "Publish Now," click **Schedule**
3. Pick your date and time (shown in your local timezone)
4. Atlas publishes automatically at the scheduled time

Scheduled posts appear in **Social Media > Scheduled** with a countdown to their publish time. You can edit or cancel them anytime before they go out.

**Tip:** Scheduling posts for consistent times each week helps build an audience. Many trade businesses find that morning posts (7-9 AM) perform well, since homeowners check their phones before the day gets busy.

### Draft-to-Publish Flow

If your post started as a draft (see [Editing Drafts](editing-drafts.md)), publishing works the same way. Open the draft, make any final edits, and hit Publish or Schedule.

## What Happens During Publishing

Behind the scenes, Atlas UX:

1. **Formats for each platform** -- Adjusts text length, hashtag style, and media dimensions to match each platform's requirements
2. **Uploads media** -- Images and videos are uploaded in the format each platform expects
3. **Applies platform settings** -- For example, TikTok posts default to "self only" visibility until you change it; Pinterest posts link back to your website
4. **Records the result** -- Each delivery is logged with success/failure status and a link to the live post

If publishing fails on one platform, it doesn't affect the others. You'll see the error and can retry just that platform.

## Delivery Status

After publishing, check the status of each post in **Social Media > Published**:

- **Delivered** -- Post is live on the platform
- **Pending** -- Still processing (usually resolves within a minute)
- **Failed** -- Something went wrong (see the error message for details)
- **Scheduled** -- Waiting for its publish time

For failed posts, common fixes include:
- Reconnecting your social account (token expired)
- Reducing image file size
- Adjusting content that triggered a platform's content policy

## Platform-Specific Tips

**Instagram:** Use square or portrait images for best results. Carousel posts (multiple images) get higher engagement than single images.

**Facebook:** Longer captions work well here. Include a question to encourage comments.

**X (Twitter):** Keep it punchy. Use 1-2 relevant hashtags max.

**TikTok:** Video only. Keep it under 60 seconds for best reach. Atlas sets posts to private by default so you can review before making public.

**LinkedIn:** More professional tone. Good for commercial work, certifications, and business milestones.

**Pinterest:** Every pin links back to a URL (your website by default). Great for before-and-after project photos.

## Common Questions

**Can I publish to platforms I haven't connected yet?**
No. You need to connect each platform first. See [Connecting Social Accounts](connecting-social-accounts.md) for a step-by-step guide.

**What if I want to post different content to different platforms?**
Create separate posts for each platform, or use the [Broadcasting](broadcasting.md) feature which lets you customize per-platform content from a single starting point.

**Is there a daily posting limit?**
Yes, Atlas UX enforces a daily posting cap to protect your accounts from being flagged for spam. The limit is generous for normal business use. You'll see it on your dashboard.
