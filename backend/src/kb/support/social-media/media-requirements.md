# Media Requirements for Social Posts

Every post you create in Atlas UX must include at least one image or video. No exceptions. This page explains why, what formats are supported, and how Atlas helps when you don't have media ready.

## Why Every Post Needs Media

This isn't a rule we made up to be difficult. It's driven by two realities:

**Engagement:** Posts with images or video get 2-3x more engagement (likes, comments, shares) than text-only posts. For a trade business trying to attract local customers, that difference matters.

**Platform requirements:** Several major platforms simply won't accept text-only posts:
- **Instagram** -- Image or video required
- **TikTok** -- Video required
- **Pinterest** -- Image required
- **YouTube** -- Video required

Even on platforms that allow text-only posts (X, Facebook, LinkedIn), adding media dramatically increases visibility. The algorithm favors visual content.

Bottom line: if you're going to take the time to post, make it count by including media.

## Supported Image Formats

| Format | Extensions         | Max Size | Notes                    |
|--------|--------------------|----------|--------------------------|
| JPEG   | .jpg, .jpeg        | 10 MB    | Best for photos          |
| PNG    | .png               | 10 MB    | Best for graphics/logos   |
| WebP   | .webp              | 10 MB    | Modern format, smaller files |
| GIF    | .gif               | 15 MB    | Animated images supported |

### Recommended Image Dimensions

Different platforms display images differently. Here are the safe sizes:

- **Square (1:1):** 1080 x 1080 px -- Works everywhere, best for Instagram feed
- **Portrait (4:5):** 1080 x 1350 px -- Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest
- **Landscape (16:9):** 1920 x 1080 px -- X, LinkedIn, Facebook, YouTube thumbnails
- **Story/Reel (9:16):** 1080 x 1920 px -- Instagram Stories, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

When you upload an image, Atlas UX automatically crops and resizes it for each platform. But starting with a high-resolution image (at least 1080 px wide) gives the best results.

## Supported Video Formats

| Format | Extensions    | Max Size | Max Length | Notes                         |
|--------|---------------|----------|------------|-------------------------------|
| MP4    | .mp4          | 500 MB   | 10 min     | Preferred format              |
| MOV    | .mov          | 500 MB   | 10 min     | Common from iPhones           |
| WebM   | .webm         | 500 MB   | 10 min     | Web-optimized format          |

### Video Tips

- **Keep it short.** 15-60 seconds performs best on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and Facebook
- **Shoot vertically** (9:16) for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts
- **Shoot horizontally** (16:9) for YouTube, LinkedIn, and Facebook feed
- **Good lighting matters more than fancy equipment.** Your phone camera is fine

## What If You Don't Have Media?

No photo from the job site? No problem. Atlas UX has three fallbacks to make sure your post always goes out with media:

### 1. Asset Library

Every image or video you've uploaded in the past is saved to your asset library. When you create a post without attaching media, Atlas checks your library for something relevant. If you uploaded a kitchen remodel photo last month and you're posting about kitchen work today, Atlas can automatically pull that image.

Build your library over time by uploading photos from jobs. Even quick phone photos add up.

### 2. AI Image Generation

If your asset library doesn't have a match, Atlas generates a custom image using Flux, an AI image generation model. Here's how it works:

- Atlas reads your post content
- It creates a relevant, professional image based on the topic
- The image is unique -- not a stock photo and not recycled from someone else

For example, if your post says "Spring is the perfect time to check your AC filters," Atlas might generate an image of a clean air filter in a well-lit home setting.

AI-generated images are a solid fallback, but real photos from your business will always perform better. Customers want to see your actual work.

### 3. Video from Library

For video-first platforms (TikTok, YouTube), Atlas checks your library for video content first. If you regularly upload short clips from job sites, Atlas can match them to relevant posts automatically.

## Best Practices for Trade Businesses

**Take photos on every job.** Make it a habit. Before-and-after shots are gold. Even a quick snapshot of a finished install is content you can use for weeks.

**Keep your phone camera clean.** Seriously. A smudged lens ruins otherwise good photos.

**Natural light wins.** If you're photographing indoor work, open the blinds or turn on all the lights. Bright photos get more engagement.

**Show the work, not just the result.** Photos of you and your team actually working -- tools in hand, boots on the ground -- build trust and show authenticity.

**Video doesn't need to be polished.** A 15-second clip of a drain being cleared or a fresh coat of paint is more engaging than a professionally produced commercial. Customers want real.

## Common Questions

**Can I use stock photos?**
You can upload any image you have the rights to use. But stock photos perform worse than real photos from your business. Customers can tell the difference.

**What if Atlas generates an image I don't like?**
You can regenerate it. Click **Regenerate Image** in the editor and Atlas will create a new one. You can also add a description to guide the generation -- for example, "show a bathroom renovation, modern style."

**Do I need media for every platform in a broadcast?**
Yes. If you're posting to five platforms at once, all five need media. Atlas uses the same image across platforms (resized appropriately) unless you specify different media for different platforms.

**Can I add multiple images to a single post?**
Yes, for platforms that support carousel posts (Instagram, LinkedIn). Upload multiple images and Atlas will format them as a carousel.
