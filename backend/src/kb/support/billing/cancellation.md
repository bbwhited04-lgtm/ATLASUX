# Cancellation

Atlas UX has no long-term contracts. You can cancel anytime, and we have made the process straightforward. Here is what happens when you cancel, what happens to your data, and how to come back if you change your mind.

## How to Cancel

1. Log in to your Atlas UX dashboard
2. Go to **Settings** and then **Billing**
3. Click **Cancel Subscription**
4. Confirm your cancellation

That is it. No phone call required, no talking to a retention specialist, no hoops to jump through. You click cancel, and it is done.

## What Happens When You Cancel

### Your Service Continues Until the End of Your Billing Period

If you cancel on day 10 of a 30-day cycle, Lucy keeps answering your calls for the remaining 20 days. You have already paid for the full month, and you get the full month. Your cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period.

### Lucy Stops Answering Calls

Once your billing period ends, Lucy stops picking up calls to your business number. Callers will no longer reach your AI receptionist. Make sure you have an alternative in place -- forward your number back to your phone, set up voicemail, or arrange another answering solution.

### Your Dashboard Becomes Read-Only

You can still log in and view your data after cancellation. Your dashboard switches to read-only mode, so you can review call history, download invoices, and export any information you need.

## What Happens to Your Data

We do not delete your data the moment you cancel. Here is the timeline:

- **Immediately after cancellation** -- Everything stays. Your call history, messages, appointments, business settings, and configurations are all preserved.
- **30-day retention period** -- Your data is kept for 30 days after your subscription ends. This gives you time to export anything you need, or to reactivate without losing anything.
- **After 30 days** -- Your data is scheduled for deletion in accordance with our privacy policy.

If you need more time to export your data, reach out to support before the 30-day window closes and we will work with you.

## Your Phone Number

When you cancel, what happens to your phone number depends on your setup:

- **If you ported your number to Atlas UX** -- Contact our support team to port it back to your carrier before your service ends. We will help you through the process.
- **If you are using a number we assigned** -- The number will be released after cancellation. If you reactivate within the 30-day window, we will do our best to reassign the same number, but availability is not guaranteed.

**Important:** If keeping your phone number matters to you, handle the porting process before your service ends. Once a number is released, getting it back may not be possible.

## Reactivation

Changed your mind? Reactivating is easy:

1. Log in to your Atlas UX dashboard
2. Go to **Settings** and then **Billing**
3. Click **Reactivate Subscription**
4. Add or confirm your payment method

If you reactivate within 30 days of cancellation:

- Your data is fully restored
- Your business settings and Lucy's configuration are exactly as you left them
- Your phone number is reassigned if still available

After 30 days, you would need to set up your account fresh, but the process is quick. See [Setting Up with the Onboarding Wizard](../getting-started/setup-wizard.md).

## We Would Love Your Feedback

When you cancel, we will ask why. This is optional, and there is no wrong answer. Whether it is price, features, timing, or something else entirely -- your feedback helps us build a better product for trade businesses. If there is something we could have done differently, we genuinely want to know.

## Before You Cancel

If you are thinking about canceling because of a specific issue, consider reaching out to support first. Common situations we can help with:

- **Lucy is not handling calls the way you want** -- We can adjust her configuration and training
- **You are not using all the features** -- We can walk you through what you might be missing
- **Billing issue** -- See [Payment Methods](payment-methods.md) to update your card or resolve a payment problem
- **Taking a break from business** -- Ask us about pausing your subscription instead of canceling

We are here to help, not to pressure you. If canceling is the right move, we respect that completely.
