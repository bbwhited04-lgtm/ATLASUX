# Invoices and Receipts

Every payment you make to Atlas UX generates an invoice. You can view, download, and use these for your business accounting, tax filings, or expense tracking. Here is where to find everything and how it all works.

## Finding Your Invoices

To view your billing history:

1. Log in to your Atlas UX dashboard
2. Go to **Settings** and then **Billing**
3. You will see a list of all past invoices with dates, amounts, and payment status

Each entry shows:

- **Date** -- When the charge was made
- **Amount** -- How much was charged
- **Status** -- Paid, failed, or pending
- **Invoice number** -- A unique reference for your records

## Downloading Receipts

You can download any invoice as a PDF:

1. Find the invoice in your billing history
2. Click the **Download** or **View Invoice** button
3. A PDF opens that you can save or print

These PDFs include all the details you need for bookkeeping: your business name, the charge amount, the billing period, and the payment method used (last four digits of the card).

## Email Receipts

After each successful payment, we send a receipt to the email address on your account. These emails come from Stripe, our payment processor, and include:

- The amount charged
- The date
- A link to view the full invoice online

If you are not receiving email receipts, check your spam or junk folder. Make sure the email on your account is current -- you can update it in [Updating Your Profile](../account/updating-profile.md).

## Billing History

Your billing history shows every charge since you subscribed. This includes:

- **Regular monthly charges** -- Your $99/mo subscription payment
- **Prorated charges** -- If you changed plans mid-cycle (see [Upgrading and Downgrading](upgrading-downgrading.md))
- **Failed payment attempts** -- So you can see if and when a charge did not go through
- **Credits or adjustments** -- Any billing corrections

The history is sorted newest-first, so your most recent charge is always at the top.

## Tax Information

Your invoices include the information most businesses need for tax purposes:

- **Business name** -- As entered in your Atlas UX profile
- **Invoice date and amount** -- For matching to your accounting records
- **Service description** -- "Atlas UX Subscription" with the billing period

Atlas UX is a software subscription service. Depending on your state and local tax laws, it may or may not be subject to sales tax. We recommend checking with your accountant or tax advisor about how to categorize the expense.

**Tip for trade businesses:** Your Atlas UX subscription is a business expense. Most accountants categorize it under "Software and Subscriptions" or "Office Expenses." Keep your invoices organized -- downloading them quarterly makes tax time much easier.

## Using Invoices for Expense Tracking

If you use accounting software like QuickBooks, FreshBooks, or Wave, you can:

1. Download the PDF invoice from your dashboard
2. Upload or attach it to the corresponding transaction in your accounting software
3. Categorize it as a recurring business expense

Since Atlas UX charges the same amount each month, setting up a recurring expense rule in your accounting software can save you time.

## Common Questions

**Can I get a single invoice for multiple months?**
Each month generates its own invoice. For a summary view, use the billing history in your dashboard to see all charges at a glance.

**Can I change the business name on my invoices?**
Yes. Update your business name in [Updating Your Profile](../account/updating-profile.md) and future invoices will reflect the change. Past invoices keep the name that was on file when they were generated.

**I need an invoice for a specific date range. How do I find it?**
Your billing history is sorted by date. Scroll to the month you need, or use the date filter if available.

**My payment failed but I see a charge. What happened?**
Check the status column. If it shows "failed," the charge was attempted but not completed -- you were not billed. See [Payment Methods](payment-methods.md) for how to resolve failed payments.
