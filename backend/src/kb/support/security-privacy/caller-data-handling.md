# Caller Data Handling

When a customer calls your business and Lucy answers, information is exchanged during that conversation. This article explains exactly what Lucy captures, how that data is stored, and what options exist for caller privacy.

## What Lucy Captures During Calls

During a phone call, Lucy may collect the following information based on the conversation:

**Automatically available:**
- **Caller's phone number** -- Provided by the phone network via caller ID (through Twilio). This is captured before the conversation begins.
- **Call timestamp** -- When the call started and ended.
- **Call duration** -- How long the conversation lasted.

**Provided by the caller during conversation:**
- **Caller's name** -- If they introduce themselves.
- **Reason for calling** -- The service they need (e.g., "I need a drain unclogged").
- **Appointment preferences** -- Preferred date, time, and any scheduling notes.
- **Contact details** -- Additional phone numbers or email addresses the caller shares.
- **Messages** -- If the caller wants to leave a message for you, Lucy captures the content.

Lucy only captures what the caller voluntarily provides during the conversation. She does not access external databases or look up callers using their phone number.

## How Caller Data Is Stored

Caller information collected during calls is stored in your Atlas UX account:

- **Tenant-scoped** -- All caller data is tied to your business's tenant ID. No other Atlas UX customer can see your callers' information. See [Data Privacy](data-privacy.md) for how tenant isolation works.
- **Database storage** -- Appointment records, messages, and call metadata are stored in our PostgreSQL database hosted on AWS Lightsail.
- **Encrypted connections** -- All data in transit is protected by HTTPS/TLS encryption. See [How We Protect Your Data](how-we-protect-your-data.md).
- **Audit logged** -- Every appointment booking and message capture is recorded in the audit trail. See [Audit Trail](audit-trail.md).

## Voice Processing

Lucy's voice is generated by ElevenLabs (see [ElevenLabs Voice](../integrations/elevenlabs-voice.md)), and calls are routed through Twilio (see [Twilio Telephony](../integrations/twilio-telephony.md)). Here is how voice data flows:

1. The caller's voice is transmitted from their phone through the carrier network to Twilio.
2. Twilio routes the audio to ElevenLabs for real-time conversation processing.
3. Lucy's responses are generated by ElevenLabs and sent back through Twilio to the caller.
4. Structured data from the conversation (appointments, messages) is sent to Atlas UX via secure webhooks.

The voice audio itself is processed in real time for the conversation. Structured outputs (what was booked, what message was taken) are what gets stored in your account.

## Caller Privacy

Atlas UX respects caller privacy through several measures:

**No cold data mining:** Lucy does not use caller information to build marketing profiles, send unsolicited messages, or share data with advertisers. Caller data is used only to serve your business operations (booking appointments, delivering messages).

**Minimal data capture:** Lucy captures only what is relevant to the call's purpose. If a caller asks about your hours and hangs up without booking, the only data stored is the call metadata (phone number, timestamp, duration).

**SMS opt-out:** If Lucy sends an SMS confirmation to a caller, the message includes standard opt-out language. Callers can reply STOP to opt out of future text messages, and Twilio will automatically honor that request.

## Opt-Out Options

Callers have several options if they prefer not to have their information stored:

1. **During the call** -- A caller can ask Lucy to not store their information. Lucy can take the call without creating a record if configured to support this.
2. **After the call** -- Callers can contact your business directly to request their information be removed.
3. **SMS opt-out** -- Reply STOP to any SMS from your business number to stop receiving text messages.

As the business owner, you can also manually delete individual caller records from your dashboard.

## Compliance With Telecom Regulations

Atlas UX is designed to comply with US telecom regulations:

**TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act):**
- SMS messages are sent only in connection with a service the caller requested (appointment confirmation).
- Callers can opt out of SMS at any time.
- Atlas UX does not make unsolicited robocalls to consumer phone numbers.

**State regulations:**
- Recording laws vary by state. If call recording is enabled in your configuration, it is your responsibility to ensure compliance with your state's consent requirements (one-party vs. two-party consent states).
- Lucy can be configured to announce that the call may be monitored or recorded for quality purposes.

**Do Not Call (DNC):**
- Lucy's primary function is inbound call handling (your customers calling you). For any outbound calling features, DNC list compliance is the business owner's responsibility.

## Your Responsibilities as a Business Owner

As the operator of your Atlas UX account, you are responsible for:

- Informing your customers about how their data is used, consistent with your own privacy policy.
- Complying with your state's call recording consent laws if recording is enabled.
- Responding to customer requests to delete their data.
- Using caller information only for legitimate business purposes.

Atlas UX provides the tools and security infrastructure. How you use caller data within your business is governed by your own privacy practices and applicable laws.
