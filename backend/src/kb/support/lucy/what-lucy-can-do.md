# What Lucy Can Do (and What She Cannot)

Lucy is a powerful AI receptionist, but she is not magic. This guide gives you a clear picture of what she handles well and where her limits are, so you know exactly what to expect.

## What Lucy can do

### Answer every call, 24/7

Lucy picks up on the first ring, day or night, weekday or weekend. She never misses a call, never puts anyone on hold, and never sends anyone to voicemail. Even at 2am on a holiday, she answers with your business name and a friendly greeting.

### Book appointments in real time

While the caller is still on the phone, Lucy can schedule an appointment. She collects the caller's name, the service they need, and their preferred time, then books it within your available hours. The caller hangs up with a confirmed appointment -- no callbacks needed.

See [Booking Flow](../appointments/booking-flow.md) for how this works step by step.

### Send SMS confirmations

After booking an appointment, Lucy sends a text message to the caller's phone with the details -- date, time, and service. This gives the caller a written record they can refer back to and reduces no-shows.

See [SMS Confirmations](../phone-sms/sms-confirmations.md) for more details.

### Take detailed messages

When a caller needs to reach a specific person or has a request that requires follow-up, Lucy captures everything: caller name, phone number, who the message is for, the message itself, and how urgent it is. Nothing gets lost.

### Answer common questions

Lucy knows your business hours, services, and any other details you provided during setup. When callers ask "what time do you close?" or "do you do water heater installs?" she answers immediately from that information.

### Notify you on Slack

Every call, booking, and message triggers a notification in your Slack channel. You stay in the loop without being tethered to the phone. See [Slack Alerts](../notifications/slack-alerts.md) to set this up.

### Handle multiple calls at once

Unlike a human receptionist, Lucy can handle several calls at the same time. Every caller gets the same attentive, patient experience regardless of how busy the phone lines are. No one hears a busy signal or gets put on hold.

### Adapt to different callers

Lucy reads the room. A frustrated caller gets empathy and patience. A caller in a hurry gets straight answers. A chatty caller gets warmth. She adjusts automatically -- you do not need to configure this.

### Work after hours gracefully

When someone calls outside your business hours, Lucy still answers. She lets callers know your hours, offers to take a message, and can book appointments for the next available time. See [Business Hours](business-hours.md) for setup details.

---

## What Lucy cannot do (yet)

### Process payments on calls

Lucy cannot take credit card numbers or process payments during a call. If a caller wants to pay over the phone, Lucy takes a message so your team can call back and handle it.

### Access external systems that are not connected

Lucy works with the tools integrated into Atlas UX -- your calendar, SMS, and Slack. She cannot look up information in systems she is not connected to, like your accounting software or inventory management. If a caller asks about something outside her knowledge, she honestly says she does not have that information and offers to have someone follow up.

### Make outbound calls

Right now, Lucy answers incoming calls only. She does not make outbound calls to customers, though this capability is on the roadmap for a future update.

### Provide medical, legal, or financial advice

Lucy is a receptionist, not a licensed professional. She will not diagnose problems, give legal opinions, or provide financial advice. She sticks to scheduling, messaging, and answering the business questions you have authorized.

### Override your business rules

Lucy follows the guardrails you set. She will not promise pricing you have not authorized, commit to timelines you have not approved, or share internal details about your team or operations.

---

## The bottom line

Lucy handles the work that keeps your phone ringing all day: answering calls, booking appointments, taking messages, and keeping you informed. For everything else, she takes a message and makes sure your team follows up. She is honest about what she can and cannot do, and callers appreciate that.

For a walkthrough of a typical call, see [Call Handling](call-handling.md). For technical details on Lucy's during-call actions, see [Mid-Call Tools](mid-call-tools.md).
