# Your First Steps After Signing Up

You've created your account and finished the onboarding wizard -- nice work. Now let's make sure everything is connected and working so Lucy can start handling real calls for your business.

Think of this as your setup checklist. Work through it from top to bottom, and by the end, you'll have a fully operational AI receptionist.

## Your Setup Checklist

### 1. Complete Your Business Profile

If you went through the setup wizard quickly, now's a good time to go back and fill in any details you skipped. The more Lucy knows about your business, the better she handles calls.

Head to **Settings > Business Profile** and make sure these are filled in:

- Business name (as customers know it)
- Industry / business type
- Phone number
- Services you offer (be specific -- "water heater repair" is better than just "plumbing")
- Service area (cities or ZIP codes you cover)

If you need a refresher, check out [Setting Up Your Business with the Onboarding Wizard](setup-wizard.md).

### 2. Set Your Business Hours

Lucy uses your hours to decide when to offer same-day appointments and how to handle after-hours callers. Go to **Settings > Business Hours** and set your schedule for each day of the week.

Remember: Lucy works 24/7 regardless of your hours. Outside your posted hours, she still answers calls, takes messages, and lets callers know when you'll be back. She just won't book appointments during times you're closed.

### 3. Configure Lucy's Voice and Greeting

This is where you make Lucy feel like part of your team. Go to **Settings > Lucy** and customize:

- **Greeting** -- What Lucy says when she picks up. Something like "Thanks for calling Smith Plumbing, this is Lucy, how can I help you?" works great.
- **Voice style** -- Choose how Lucy sounds. Friendly and warm works well for most service businesses.
- **Business personality** -- Tell Lucy a bit about your brand tone. Casual and approachable? Professional and buttoned-up? She'll match it.

For a deeper dive into what Lucy can do, see [How Lucy Works](../lucy/how-lucy-works.md).

### 4. Connect Your Calendar

Lucy can book appointments directly into your schedule, but she needs access to your calendar first. Go to **Settings > Calendar** and connect your preferred calendar app.

Once connected, Lucy will see your real-time availability and only offer time slots that are actually open. No double-booking, no guesswork.

For detailed calendar setup instructions, see [Calendar Setup](../appointments/calendar-setup.md).

### 5. Set Up SMS Confirmations

When Lucy books an appointment, she can send your customer a text confirmation right away. This cuts down on no-shows and saves you from making confirmation calls.

Go to **Settings > Notifications > SMS** to configure this. You'll need to verify your business phone number for sending texts.

For phone and SMS configuration details, see [Twilio Setup](../phone-sms/twilio-setup.md).

### 6. Enable Slack Notifications

Want to know instantly when Lucy books a job or takes a message? Connect Slack and get real-time notifications on your phone or desktop.

Go to **Settings > Notifications > Slack** and follow the connection steps. You can choose which events trigger notifications -- new bookings, messages, missed calls, or all of the above.

### 7. Make a Test Call

This is the fun part. Once everything is set up, call your business number and hear Lucy in action. Listen for:

- Does she greet callers with your business name?
- Can she answer questions about your services?
- Does she correctly offer available appointment times?
- Does the SMS confirmation come through after booking?
- Does the Slack notification pop up?

If something sounds off, go back to your settings and adjust. Most issues are solved by updating your services list or tweaking the greeting.

## Quick Reference

| Step | Where to Find It | Time Needed |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| Business profile | Settings > Business Profile | 5 minutes |
| Business hours | Settings > Business Hours | 2 minutes |
| Lucy's voice | Settings > Lucy | 5 minutes |
| Calendar | Settings > Calendar | 3 minutes |
| SMS confirmations | Settings > Notifications > SMS | 3 minutes |
| Slack notifications | Settings > Notifications > Slack | 2 minutes |
| Test call | Call your business number | 2 minutes |

Total setup time: roughly 20 minutes. That's less time than it takes to train a real receptionist on answering the phone.
