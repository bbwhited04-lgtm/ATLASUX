# Choosing a Plan

Atlas UX keeps pricing simple. No confusing tier charts, no hidden fees, no "contact us for pricing" runaround. One plan, one price, everything included.

## The Standard Plan -- $99/Month

For $99 a month, you get the complete Atlas UX platform. That includes:

- **Lucy, your AI receptionist** -- Available 24/7, 365 days a year. She answers every call professionally, whether it's 2 PM on a Tuesday or midnight on a holiday.
- **Unlimited inbound calls** -- No per-minute charges. No call caps. Lucy handles every call that comes in, whether you get 10 a day or 100.
- **Appointment booking** -- Lucy schedules appointments directly into your calendar based on your real-time availability, services, and business hours.
- **SMS confirmations** -- Every booked appointment triggers a text message to your customer with the details. Fewer no-shows, less phone tag.
- **Slack notifications** -- Get instant alerts when Lucy books a job, takes a message, or flags something that needs your attention. Works on your phone and computer.
- **Call history and message log** -- Every call Lucy handles is logged with details. Review what was discussed, what was booked, and what messages were left.
- **Full dashboard** -- Manage your business settings, update services, adjust hours, and review your schedule from one central place.
- **Voice customization** -- Configure how Lucy sounds, what she says in her greeting, and how she represents your business.

That's not an "up to" list. Every feature is included at $99/month. No premium tier required.

## How It Compares

Think about what you're replacing. A part-time receptionist costs $1,500 to $2,500 a month, plus benefits and scheduling headaches. An answering service charges per call and often sounds generic. With Lucy:

- She never calls in sick
- She never puts a customer on hold for five minutes
- She knows your services, your hours, and your service area
- She works nights, weekends, and holidays at no extra charge

For a hundred bucks a month, you never miss a call again. For a plumber, one booked job from a call you would have missed more than covers the cost.

## How to Upgrade

When you're ready to move from your free trial to a paid plan:

1. **Log in** to your Atlas UX dashboard.
2. Click **Upgrade** or go to **Settings > Billing**.
3. Enter your payment information. We accept all major credit and debit cards.
4. Confirm your subscription.

That's it. Lucy keeps working without any interruption. Your settings, call history, and appointments all carry over -- nothing changes except your trial status.

## Billing Details

- **Billed monthly** -- Your card is charged on the same date each month.
- **No long-term contracts** -- You can cancel anytime. There's no annual commitment and no cancellation fee.
- **Receipts** -- You'll get an email receipt after each payment. You can also view your billing history in the dashboard.
- **Payment processing** -- We use Stripe, one of the most trusted payment platforms in the world. Your card details are never stored on our servers.

## Canceling Your Plan

If you ever need to cancel, go to **Settings > Billing** and click **Cancel Subscription**. Your plan stays active until the end of your current billing period -- you've already paid for that time, so Lucy keeps working through it.

After cancellation:

- Lucy stops answering calls at the end of the billing period
- Your account and data remain saved
- You can resubscribe anytime and pick up right where you left off

We don't make canceling difficult. No phone calls required, no guilt trips. Click the button and it's done.

## Common Questions

**Is there an annual plan with a discount?**
Not at this time. We're keeping things simple with one monthly plan. If annual billing becomes available, we'll let existing customers know first.

**Are there overage charges?**
No. Unlimited means unlimited. No surprise fees on your bill.

**Can I pause my subscription instead of canceling?**
Not currently, but canceling and resubscribing later preserves all your settings and history. It takes two minutes to get back up and running.
