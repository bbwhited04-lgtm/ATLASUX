# Setting Up Your Business with the Onboarding Wizard

Right after you create your account, Atlas UX walks you through a quick setup wizard. This takes about five minutes, and when you're done, Lucy will know enough about your business to start answering calls like a real team member.

The wizard was designed specifically for trade and service businesses -- plumbers, electricians, HVAC techs, salon owners, landscapers, and more. So you won't see questions about things that don't apply to you.

## What the Wizard Covers

### 1. Business Name and Contact Info

First, confirm your business name and enter your main phone number. This is the number your customers currently call. Later, you'll connect it so Lucy can pick up when you can't.

### 2. Industry and Business Type

Tell us what kind of work you do. Pick from options like:

- Plumbing
- HVAC / Heating & Cooling
- Electrical
- Salon / Barbershop
- Landscaping
- General Contractor
- Cleaning Services
- And more

This matters because Lucy adjusts how she talks based on your industry. A salon customer calling about a haircut gets a different experience than a homeowner with a burst pipe. Lucy knows the difference.

### 3. Business Hours

Set your regular hours -- the times you're open and available. Lucy uses this to know when to offer same-day appointments versus next-day availability. You can set different hours for each day of the week, and mark days you're closed.

Don't worry about getting this perfect right now. You can update your hours anytime from your dashboard.

### 4. Services You Offer

List the services your business provides. For a plumber, that might be:

- Drain cleaning
- Water heater repair
- Leak detection
- Bathroom remodels

Lucy uses this list when talking to callers. If someone asks "Do you do tankless water heater installs?", Lucy can check your service list and give a confident answer instead of a vague one.

### 5. Service Area (Optional)

If you serve specific cities, ZIP codes, or a radius around your shop, you can set that here. Lucy can let callers know whether you cover their area -- saving you time on calls that won't turn into jobs.

### 6. Booking Preferences

Tell Lucy how you'd like appointments handled:

- **Appointment length** -- How long is a typical job or visit? 30 minutes? An hour?
- **Buffer time** -- Want a gap between appointments for travel or cleanup?
- **Booking window** -- How far out can customers book? A week? A month?

These settings help Lucy offer realistic time slots when booking for you.

## After the Wizard

Once you finish, you'll land on your dashboard with Lucy ready to go. From there, you can:

- Fine-tune Lucy's personality and greeting
- Connect your calendar so Lucy books directly into your schedule
- Set up SMS confirmations so customers get a text after booking
- Enable Slack notifications so you hear about new appointments instantly

## Can I Change These Settings Later?

Absolutely. Everything you enter in the wizard can be updated from your business settings page. Seasonal hours? New services? Expanded service area? Just update it and Lucy adapts right away.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Setup

- **Be specific with your services.** Instead of "plumbing," list individual services like "faucet repair" and "sewer line inspection." The more detail Lucy has, the better she handles calls.
- **Set realistic hours.** If you sometimes work Saturdays, include them. You can always block off specific days later.
- **Don't skip the service area.** It saves you time by filtering out callers from areas you don't serve.
