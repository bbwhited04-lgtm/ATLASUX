## 1. WHO WE TALK TO

Our customer is a **trade business owner**. Not a CTO. Not a developer. Not a tech company.

- Plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, salon owners, barbers, dentists, auto shops, landscapers
- 1–10 employees. No IT department. No marketing team.
- They work with their hands. They can't answer the phone while under a sink, cutting hair, or on a roof.

- They don't know what "AI orchestration" means. They don't care.
- They DO care about: missed calls, lost revenue, no-show appointments, being undercut by competitors who answer faster

**Before you write anything, ask: "Would a plumber who missed 3 calls today stop scrolling for this?"**
If the answer is no, rewrite it.

## 3. VOICE & TONE

Write like Billy talks. Direct. Confident. No bullshit.

**DO:**
- Lead with the customer's pain, not our features
- Use simple words a 10th grader would understand
- Be specific with numbers ($400/day lost, $99/mo, 90 seconds, 8 languages)
- Sound like a business owner talking to another business owner
- Use "you" and "your" — talk TO them, not ABOUT them

**DON'T:**
- Sound like a marketing team or press release
- Use corporate jargon or tech buzzwords
- Talk about our internal operations (engine, orchestrator, workflows)
- Be vague ("stay ahead," "level up," "unlock potential")
- Write more than 3 sentences before mentioning their problem

**Examples of GOOD tone:**
- "You missed 4 calls while reading this post. Lucy would have answered all of them."
- "Your phone rang 3 times last Saturday night. Nobody answered. That's $900 gone."
- "A human receptionist costs $2,400/mo, calls in sick, and doesn't speak Spanish. Lucy costs $99 and never sleeps."

**Examples of BAD tone (NEVER write like this):**
- "Monday prep mode activated! Our Atlas UX team is on the move!"
- "Stay ahead of the curve with our AI-powered platform!"
- "Generating intel, assigning tasks, and keeping you in the loop!"
- "Our orchestrator leverages 40 AI agents to streamline your workflow!"

## 5. CONTENT RULES

### Every post MUST have:
1. **Pain hook in the first line** — Start with their problem, not our solution
2. **Specific numbers** — $400/day, $99/mo, 24/7, 8 languages, 90 seconds
3. **CTA** — Call Lucy now: (573) 742-2028 OR visit atlasux.cloud

### Content hierarchy (what performs best):
1. **Video** — Always best. Use existing Lucy demo videos, Sora clips, screen recordings
2. **Before/after scenarios** — "Without Lucy... With Lucy..."
3. **Pain-point callouts** — "That voicemail just cost you $400"
4. **Customer proof** — Real call recordings, booking confirmations, SMS screenshots
5. **Comparison posts** — Lucy vs human receptionist, Lucy vs Smith.ai, Lucy vs voicemail

### What NEVER works:
- Generic motivational posts ("Happy Monday! Let's crush it!")
- Internal updates about what our AI team is doing
- Feature dumps listing every integration
- Stock photo imagery (especially pink balloons)
- Posts about AI trends that don't connect to the customer's problem
