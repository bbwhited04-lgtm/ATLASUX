# Agent 03: Strategy

## Role
Define Atlas UX's positioning, business model, pricing validation, and phased roadmap.
Every recommendation tied to the reality of a solo founder with limited burn.

## Atlas UX Context
- Solo founder (Billy), pre-revenue, bootstrapped
- Product: Lucy (inbound AI voice), Mercer (outbound AI calls), SlackWorker
- Target: trade businesses, starting with salons/barbers/plumbers/HVAC
- Current pricing: $39 (limited) / $99 (standalone) / $149 (business 5-seat) / $249 (standalone business) / $35-40/seat enterprise
- Marketing: multi-platform livestreaming + short-form video
- Landing page: atlasux.cloud — needs conversion optimization
- Zoom integration in progress (marketplace resubmission needed)

## 1. Positioning

```
CURRENT POSITIONING:
FOR trade business owners WHO miss calls because they're doing the actual work
ATLAS UX IS an AI receptionist THAT answers calls, books appointments, and never misses a beat
UNLIKE voicemail or expensive answering services ($200-600/mo)
WE cost $99/mo and work 24/7 with zero sick days

TEST YOUR POSITIONING:
□ Can a plumber understand this in 10 seconds? If not, simplify.
□ Does it answer "why should I care?" Not "what is it?"
□ Does it make the competitor look bad by comparison?
□ Can you prove the claim in a 60-second demo?
```

## 2. Business Model

```
REVENUE MODEL: SaaS subscription (monthly/annual)

CURRENT TIERS:
| Tier | Price | Target Customer | Lucy/Mercer |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------|
| Limited | $39/mo | Price-sensitive, want Slack tools only | No |
| Standalone | $99/mo | Solo trade owner, wants Lucy | Yes |
| Business | $149/mo (5 seats) | Small shop with employees | Yes |
| Standalone Business | $249/mo (5 seats) | Premium small shop | Yes |
| Enterprise | $40/seat | Multi-location businesses | Yes |
| Enterprise 50+ | $35/seat | Large operations | Yes |

PRICING QUESTIONS TO VALIDATE:
□ Is $39 without Lucy/Mercer a dead tier? What's the value prop?
□ Should there be a 14-day free trial for the $99 tier?
□ What's the difference between Business ($149) and Standalone Business ($249)?
□ Is annual pricing offered? (2 months free = $990/yr vs $1,188)
□ Should Mercer be an add-on rather than bundled?

PRICING PSYCHOLOGY FOR TRADE OWNERS:
- Frame monthly cost against lost revenue: "$99/mo = less than ONE missed haircut"
- Show daily cost: "$3.30/day for a 24/7 receptionist"
- Compare to human alternative: "Ruby charges $230/mo for 50 minutes. Lucy is unlimited."
```

## 4. Roadmap

```
HORIZON 1 — NOW → 4 WEEKS: Get first 10 paying customers
- Fix landing page for conversion (demo, pricing, trust signals)
- Lucy demo: let visitors call a number and hear Lucy work
- 30-day launch engine execution (see framework)
- Mercer cold-calling trade businesses in target verticals
- Short-form video content: missed call scenarios per trade

HORIZON 2 — MONTHS 2-3: Get to 50 customers
- Onboarding optimization (signup → Lucy answering < 15 min)
- Customer success: check in with every customer weekly
- Referral program: "Give $25, get $25" for trade owners
- Content: case studies from first 10 customers
- SEO: target "AI receptionist for [trade]" keywords

HORIZON 3 — MONTHS 4-6: Get to 200 customers, validate unit economics
- Mercer self-serve (customers set their own outbound campaigns)
- Zoom integration (if demand validates)
- Integration with trade-specific tools (Jobber, ServiceTitan, Square)
- Hire first support person or build self-serve support

HORIZON 4 — MONTHS 7-12: Scale what works
- Expand to additional trade verticals
- Enterprise tier for multi-location businesses
- Partner channel (trade associations, vendor partnerships)
- Evaluate fundraising if unit economics are strong
```
