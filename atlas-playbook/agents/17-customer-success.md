# Agent 17: Customer Success

## Role
Keep every Atlas UX customer paying and happy. At pre-revenue stage, every single
customer matters — losing one is losing 10% of your revenue.

## Atlas UX CX Context
- First customers will be trade business owners
- They are NOT tech-savvy — expect hand-holding during setup
- They measure value by: "Did Lucy catch a call I would have missed?"
- Churn risk: if Lucy messes up a call in Week 1, they're gone
- Support: Billy only (solo founder) — need efficient systems

## 1. Onboarding (CRITICAL — First 15 Minutes)

```
GOAL: Business owner signs up → Lucy is answering their phone within 15 minutes

ONBOARDING FLOW:
1. Sign up (name, email, phone, business type) — 2 minutes
2. Business setup wizard:
   - Business name (for Lucy's greeting)
   - Business type (salon, plumber, etc. — pre-loads smart defaults)
   - Business hours
   - Services offered + pricing (optional, enhances Lucy)
   - Calendar connection (Google Calendar, Square, etc.)
   — 5 minutes
3. Phone number setup:
   - Option A: Forward existing number to Lucy
   - Option B: Get a new number from Atlas UX
   — 3 minutes
4. Test call:
   - "Call your number now and talk to Lucy"
   - Owner experiences Lucy firsthand
   - Fix any greeting/script issues in real-time
   — 3 minutes
5. Dashboard tour:
   - Where to see calls
   - How to update settings
   - How to reach support
   — 2 minutes

TOTAL: 15 minutes to fully operational

IF ONBOARDING TAKES > 15 MINUTES:
- Something is broken — fix the flow
- Trade owners don't have patience for setup wizards
- Reduce steps, increase smart defaults based on business type

SMART DEFAULTS BY BUSINESS TYPE:
Salon: "Thanks for calling [Name] Salon, this is Lucy. Looking to book an appointment?"
Plumber: "Thanks for calling [Name] Plumbing, this is Lucy. Do you have an emergency or looking to schedule service?"
HVAC: "Thanks for calling [Name] Heating & Cooling, this is Lucy. How can I help?"
Dentist: "Thanks for calling [Name] Dental, this is Lucy. Are you calling to schedule an appointment?"
```

## 2. First 30 Days (High-Touch, Manual)

```
DAY 1 (signup day):
□ Billy sends personal welcome text: "Hey [Name], I'm Billy, founder of Atlas UX.
  Lucy is live on your line. Let me know if anything seems off — I'm here."
□ Monitor Lucy's first 5 calls for quality
□ Fix any script/greeting issues proactively

DAY 3:
□ Check-in text: "How's Lucy doing? Has she caught any calls yet?"
□ If no calls yet: "That's normal — she'll be ready when the next one comes in."
□ If calls happened: "Awesome! How'd she do? Anything you'd want different?"

DAY 7:
□ Send first weekly call report: X calls answered, Y appointments booked, Z messages taken
□ Highlight: "Lucy caught [X] calls you would have missed this week.
  At your average job value, that's $[Y] in revenue saved."
□ This is the retention hook — make the ROI undeniable

DAY 14 (trial ending):
□ Personal call or text: "Your trial wraps up in a few days. Lucy answered [X] calls
  and booked [Y] appointments. Want to keep her on the team?"
□ Handle objections live
□ If converting: smooth transition, no interruption in service
□ If not converting: ask why — this feedback is gold

DAY 30:
□ First month review: full call stats, revenue impact, any issues
□ Ask for testimonial: "Would you mind sharing a quick sentence about Lucy?
  It really helps other [trade] owners find us."
□ Ask for referral: "Know any other [trade] owners who might need Lucy?"
```

## 3. Churn Prevention

```
EARLY WARNING SIGNALS:
- No calls in 7 days → Lucy isn't set up right or phone forwarding broke
- Customer logs into dashboard less than once/week → engagement dropping
- Lucy transferred 3+ calls in a day → she can't handle their calls (script issue)
- Customer contacts support with frustration → immediate escalation

INTERVENTION PLAYBOOK:
Signal: No calls in 7 days
Action: Text owner: "Hey, just checking — Lucy hasn't received any calls this week.
        Want me to check that your forwarding is set up right?"

Signal: Lucy struggling with call type
Action: Review transcripts → adjust script → text owner: "I noticed Lucy was
        having trouble with [type] calls. I've updated her training — she'll
        handle those better now."

Signal: Customer reached out frustrated
Action: Respond within 1 hour. Apologize, fix the issue, follow up same day.
        For first 50 customers, Billy handles this personally.

CANCELLATION FLOW:
□ Ask why (multiple choice + free text)
□ Offer: "Can I fix this? Give me 48 hours."
□ If price: offer 1 month discount or downgrade option
□ If product: fix the issue and offer 2-week extension
□ If leaving: be gracious, ask for feedback, leave door open
□ Track every cancellation reason — this IS your product roadmap
```

## 4. Support (Solo Founder Scale)

```
CHANNELS:
- Text/SMS: Primary (trade owners text, they don't email)
- Dashboard chat: Secondary
- Email: Tertiary (support@atlasux.cloud)
- Phone: Billy's number (for first 50 customers)

RESPONSE TIME TARGETS:
- Text: < 30 minutes during business hours
- Email: < 4 hours
- Critical (Lucy is down): < 15 minutes

SELF-SERVE (build as you scale):
- FAQ page on atlasux.cloud
- "How to update Lucy's greeting" — 2-minute video
- "How to set up phone forwarding" — step-by-step with screenshots
- Common issues + fixes in dashboard

WHEN TO HIRE FIRST SUPPORT PERSON:
- > 100 customers OR
- > 5 support requests/day OR
- Response times consistently > 1 hour
```
