# Agent 02: Discovery

## Role
Deep, hypothesis-driven market research for Atlas UX. Understand who trade business owners
are, how they operate, what they fear, and what makes them buy.

## Atlas UX Context
- Product: AI voice agents (Lucy inbound, Mercer outbound)
- Target: trade businesses — salons, barbers, tattoo shops, plumbers, electricians,
  HVAC, mechanics, landscapers, contractors, dentists, vets
- Price: $39-$249/mo SaaS tiers
- Core problem: missed calls = lost revenue

## 1. Trade Business Personas

Build personas using this template for each trade vertical:

```
PERSONA: [Name — e.g., "Dana the Salon Owner"]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

BUSINESS:
- Type: [Salon / Plumbing / HVAC / etc.]
- Size: [Solo / 2-5 employees / 5-15]
- Revenue: [Monthly gross]
- Location: [Urban / suburban / rural]

DAILY REALITY:
- What are their hands doing from 8am-6pm? (Can't answer phones while ___)
- When do customers typically call? (Overlap with busy hours?)
- How many calls/day? How many go to voicemail?
- What's their current "receptionist"? (Voicemail, spouse, front desk, nothing)

MISSED CALL ECONOMICS:
- Average job/appointment value: $___
- Estimated missed calls per week: ___
- Estimated lost revenue per month: $___
- Annual cost of the problem: $___

TECH PROFILE:
- Phone: iPhone or Android?
- Comfort with apps: [High / Medium / Low]
- Current software: [QuickBooks? Square? Pen and paper?]
- How they find new tools: [Word of mouth / Google / Facebook groups / Trade shows]

BUYING TRIGGERS:
- What event makes them LOOK for a solution? (Lost a big job? Spouse complained?)
- What do they Google? ("answering service for plumbers" / "virtual receptionist")
- What makes them say YES? (Demo? Free trial? Referral from another trade owner?)
- What makes them say NO? (Too expensive? Too complicated? Don't trust AI?)

SWITCH BARRIERS:
- Risk: "What if the AI says something wrong to my customer?"
- Effort: "I don't have time to set this up"
- Trust: "I've never heard of this company"
- Cost: "Another monthly bill I don't need"

JTBD (Jobs to Be Done):
- Functional: Never miss a customer call, book appointments automatically
- Emotional: Peace of mind that business runs even when I'm on a job
- Social: Look professional — "my receptionist will schedule you"
```

### Priority Verticals for Atlas UX

Rank by: frequency of missed calls × average job value × tech readiness

```
TIER 1 (highest value, most missed calls):
- Salons / Barbershops: High call volume, $50-200/appointment, front desk often missing
- Plumbers: Emergency calls at all hours, $150-500/job, always on a job site
- HVAC: Seasonal spikes (summer AC, winter heating), $200-1000/job, high urgency
- Dentists: High appointment value, $200-2000/visit, front desk bottleneck

TIER 2 (good fit, medium urgency):
- Electricians: $100-400/job, similar to plumbing
- Auto mechanics: $200-800/repair, phone rings while under a car
- Landscapers: Seasonal, $100-500/job, outdoors all day
- Tattoo shops: $100-500/session, artists can't stop mid-tattoo

TIER 3 (longer sales cycle):
- Contractors/GC: Higher job value ($5K-50K) but fewer calls, longer decision
- Vets: High value but more complex scheduling needs
```

## 2. Competitive Intelligence

Research these DIRECTLY — don't guess:

```
DIRECT COMPETITORS (AI answering):
- Smith.ai: AI + human hybrid receptionist, $200-$600/mo
- Ruby Receptionists: Human-only, $230-$1,500/mo
- AnswerConnect: Human, $200-$500/mo
- Numa: AI for auto dealers/home services, ~$150-300/mo
- Goodcall: AI phone agent for SMBs
- GoHighLevel: Missed-call-text-back (not voice, just texts back)

FOR EACH:
□ Sign up or get a demo — experience their product firsthand
□ Screenshot their pricing page
□ Read their last 50 reviews on G2/Capterra/Google
□ Categorize 1-star complaints — what do users hate?
□ Check their SEO — what keywords are they ranking for?
□ Check their ads — what messaging are they running?

ATLAS UX ADVANTAGE:
- Lucy ANSWERS and BOOKS — competitors just text back or transfer
- $99/mo vs $200-$600/mo for human services
- Lucy works 24/7/365 — no holidays, no sick days, no turnover
- Mercer adds outbound prospecting — nobody else bundles this
```

## 3. Market Sizing

```
TAM (Total):
- ~33M small businesses in the US
- ~6M are service/trade businesses
- Average willing to pay for phone solution: ~$100/mo
- TAM: ~$7.2B/year

SAM (Serviceable):
- Trade businesses with 1-15 employees
- Currently missing 5+ calls/week
- In English-speaking US markets
- SAM: ~$1.2B/year

SOM (Obtainable Year 1):
- 100 paying customers × $99/mo average = ~$120K ARR
- 500 customers × $120/mo average = ~$720K ARR
- Conservative target: $120K-$250K ARR Year 1
```
