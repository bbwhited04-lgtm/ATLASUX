# Physical Operations & Facility Management

## For Companies with Physical Presence (offices, warehouses, retail, field teams)

### Office / Facility Security
```
ACCESS CONTROL:
□ Badge/card access system with audit trail (who entered when)
□ Visitor management: Pre-registration, ID verification, escort policy, visitor badges
□ CCTV: Entrances, exits, server rooms, cash handling areas. 30-day retention minimum.
□ Server room: Biometric access, separate HVAC, fire suppression, UPS
□ Clean desk policy: No sensitive documents left unattended
□ After-hours access: Restricted, logged, manager-approved

PHYSICAL SAFETY:
□ Fire safety: Extinguishers, alarms, sprinklers, evacuation plan, quarterly drills
□ Fire safety certificate from local fire department (mandatory in India)
□ First aid: Trained personnel, stocked first aid kits on each floor
□ Emergency exits: Clearly marked, unobstructed, illuminated
□ Earthquake/natural disaster preparedness (region-specific)
□ Ergonomic assessments (cross-reference Agent 24 Wellness)

FACILITY MANAGEMENT:
□ Housekeeping: Daily cleaning, pest control (monthly), deep cleaning (quarterly)
□ HVAC maintenance: Quarterly servicing, filter replacement
□ Electrical: Annual inspection, PAT testing, generator maintenance (monthly test run)
□ Plumbing: Regular inspection, emergency plumber on-call
□ Internet: Redundant connections (primary + backup ISP), SLA with provider
□ Power backup: UPS for critical systems, generator for extended outages (India: essential)
□ Vendor contracts: AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract) for all equipment
```

### Fleet Management (if delivery/field operations)
```
□ Vehicle tracking: GPS-enabled, real-time location, route optimization
□ Driver management: License verification, background check, periodic training
□ Vehicle maintenance: Scheduled servicing, breakdown protocol, insurance
□ Fuel management: Fuel cards, consumption tracking, anomaly detection
□ Safety: Dashcam, speed monitoring, fatigue detection for long routes
□ Compliance: Commercial vehicle permits, pollution certificate, insurance validity
```

### Occupational Health & Safety
```
LEGAL REQUIREMENTS:
□ India: Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020
  - Applicable to establishments with 10+ workers
  - Safety committee for 250+ workers
  - Annual health checkup for hazardous work
□ US: OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Act)
  - OSHA 300 log for injuries/illnesses (10+ employees)
  - Workplace hazard assessment
□ EU: Framework Directive 89/391/EEC + country-specific implementations

IMPLEMENTATION:
□ Risk assessment of workplace hazards (annual)
□ Safety training for all employees (during onboarding + annual refresher)
□ Incident reporting system (near-miss reporting encouraged, not just injuries)
□ Safety officer appointed (mandatory above certain thresholds)
□ PPE provided where required (warehouse, manufacturing, field work)
□ Working hour compliance: Maximum hours, mandatory breaks, overtime limits per jurisdiction
```

# Post-Merger Integration (PMI) Framework

## For Companies Acquiring or Being Acquired

### Pre-Close Planning (signing → closing, typically 2-6 months)
```
INTEGRATION PLANNING OFFICE (IPO — not that IPO):
□ Appoint Integration Lead (dedicated, not part-time)
□ Integration team: Representatives from every function (Eng, Product, HR, Finance, Legal, Ops)
□ Day 1 readiness plan: What MUST work on the first day after close?
□ Communication plan: Who knows what, when, through what channel
□ Clean room: Sensitive competitive info shared only with approved personnel (antitrust)

KEY WORKSTREAMS:
| Workstream | Owner | Day 1 Priority | 90-Day Priority |
|-----------|-------|---------------|----------------|
| People & Culture | CHRO | Org announcement, reporting lines | Retention packages, culture integration |
| Technology | CTO | System access, security | Architecture consolidation plan |
| Product | CPO | Product roadmap alignment | Feature rationalization |
| Finance | CFO | Financial consolidation | System integration |
| Legal | GC | Contract assignment, regulatory | Entity consolidation |
| Operations | COO | Process continuity | Process standardization |
| Customer | CMO+CX | Customer communication | Unified experience |
| Brand | CMO | Brand transition plan | Unified brand rollout |
```

### Day 1 (close date — everything must be ready)
```
□ All-hands announcement (both companies, simultaneously)
□ Welcome message from CEO to acquired team
□ Reporting structure communicated (who reports to whom)
□ System access: Acquired employees can access necessary systems
□ Payroll continuity: No gaps in salary for anyone
□ Benefits continuity: Health insurance, leave balances preserved
□ Customer communication: Joint announcement, FAQs, support contacts
□ External communication: Press release, social media, partner notifications
□ Legal: Entity changes filed, contracts assigned, regulatory notifications
```

### First 100 Days
```
PEOPLE (most important — losing key people = losing the acquisition value):
□ Retention bonuses: For critical employees, typically 6-18 month retention packages
□ Career path clarity: Where does each person fit? What's their growth path?
□ Cultural integration: Joint team events, buddy system (acquirer ↔ acquired)
□ Redundancy plan: If overlapping roles, communicate early, treat people with dignity
  - Severance: Minimum statutory + additional (good practice for reputation)
  - Outplacement support: Resume help, job search assistance, references
□ Flight risk monitoring: Which key people are interviewing elsewhere? Proactive retention.

TECHNOLOGY:
□ Security audit: Acquired company's systems, access controls, vulnerabilities
□ Architecture assessment: What to keep, migrate, or sunset
□ Data migration plan: Timeline, risk mitigation, rollback capability
□ Integration vs. coexistence: Not everything needs to merge immediately
  - Quick: SSO integration, shared communication tools
  - Medium: API integration between systems, shared data warehouse
  - Long: Full platform consolidation (6-18 months, if ever)

PRODUCT:
□ Feature overlap analysis: Both products' features mapped side-by-side
□ Customer segmentation: Which customers use which product? Why?
□ Migration plan: If sunsetting one product, how do users move to the other?
□ Pricing harmonization: Align pricing models, grandfather existing customers

OPERATIONS:
□ Process alignment: Identify conflicting processes, standardize gradually
□ Vendor consolidation: Eliminate duplicate vendors, renegotiate with combined volume
□ Support integration: Unified support or separate queues during transition?

METRICS:
□ Revenue synergy realization: Track against acquisition business case
□ Cost synergy realization: Track headcount, vendor, infrastructure savings
□ Key talent retention: % of identified key people still employed at 6/12 months
□ Customer retention: % of acquired company's customers retained at 6/12 months
□ Integration milestone completion: On track vs. plan
□ Employee engagement: Survey both teams at 30/60/90 days — catch issues early
```

### Common PMI Mistakes
```
⛔ Announcing "nothing will change" then changing everything → Trust destroyed
⛔ Rushing technology integration → Data loss, outages, customer impact
⛔ Ignoring culture → Best people leave within 6 months
⛔ Focusing only on cost synergies → Missing the growth opportunity
⛔ No dedicated integration lead → Chaos, everyone assumes someone else is handling it
⛔ Not communicating enough → Rumor mill fills the vacuum with worst-case scenarios
```
