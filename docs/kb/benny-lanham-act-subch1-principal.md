# Lanham Act -- Subchapter I: Principal Register

## Overview

Subchapter I establishes the Principal Register of the USPTO and governs the registration of trademarks, service marks, collective marks, and certification marks. Registration on the Principal Register provides the strongest federal trademark protection, including nationwide constructive notice of ownership, prima facie evidence of validity, and the potential for incontestable status.

---

## Section 1051 (section 1) -- Registration of Trademarks

### (a) Use-Based Applications
- Owner of a trademark **used in commerce** may request registration on the Principal Register
- Application must include:
  - Date of first use in commerce
  - Specimen showing mark as used
  - Drawing of the mark
  - Filing fee
  - Verified statement of use

### (b) Intent-to-Use Applications
- Person with a **bona fide intention** to use a trademark in commerce may apply
- No specimen required at time of filing
- After Notice of Allowance, applicant must file:
  - **Statement of Use** within 6 months (or extended period)
  - Specimen showing actual use
  - Dates of first use
- Mark does not register until use is proven

### (c) Amendment to Allege Use
- May be filed before approval for publication
- Converts ITU application to use-based
- Cannot be filed between approval for publication and issuance of Notice of Allowance (blackout period)

### (d) Verified Statement Required
- All applications require a verified statement (declaration) that:
  - Applicant believes they are entitled to use the mark
  - No other person has the right to use the mark in a confusingly similar manner
  - Facts stated are accurate

## Section 1052 (section 2) -- Marks Registrable on Principal Register; Refusal Grounds

### Absolute Bars to Registration
- **(a)** Immoral, deceptive, or scandalous matter
- **(a)** Matter that may disparage or falsely suggest a connection with persons, institutions, beliefs, or national symbols
- **(a)** Flag, coat of arms, or insignia of the U.S., any state, municipality, or foreign nation
- **(b)** Mark that consists of or comprises the name, portrait, or signature of a living individual (without consent) or a deceased U.S. president during the life of the surviving spouse (without consent)
- **(c)** Mark that so resembles a previously registered mark or prior pending mark as to be likely to cause confusion, mistake, or deception
- **(d)** Mark that is merely descriptive or deceptively misdescriptive of the goods/services
- **(e)** Mark that is primarily geographically descriptive or primarily geographically deceptively misdescriptive
- **(e)** Mark that is primarily merely a surname

### Exceptions and Acquired Distinctiveness
- **(f)** Marks under (d), (e)(geographical), or (e)(surname) MAY be registered if they have become distinctive through use (acquired distinctiveness / secondary meaning)
  - Evidence: substantially exclusive and continuous use for at least 5 years, or other proof of distinctiveness
  - May also claim acquired distinctiveness based on ownership of prior registrations for the same mark

---

## Section 1053 (section 3) -- Service Marks Registrable
- Service marks are registrable on the same basis as trademarks
- All provisions applicable to trademarks apply to service marks

---

## Section 1054 (section 4) -- Collective Marks and Certification Marks
- **Collective marks**: Used by members of cooperatives, associations, or other collective groups
- **Certification marks**: Used to certify regional origin, material, mode of manufacture, quality, accuracy, or that labor was performed by union members
- Both registrable on the Principal Register

---

## Section 1055 (section 5) -- Use by Related Companies
- Use of a mark by a related company (licensee) inures to the benefit of the registrant/applicant
- Related company: Any person whose use of the mark is controlled by the owner with respect to the nature and quality of the goods/services

---

## Section 1056 (section 6) -- Disclaimers
- Director may require disclaimer of an unregistrable component of an otherwise registrable mark
- Disclaimer does not prejudice or affect the applicant's rights outside the disclaimer

---

## Section 1057 (section 7) -- Certificates of Registration

### (a) Certificate as Prima Facie Evidence
- Certificate of registration is **prima facie evidence** of:
  - Validity of the registration
  - Registrant's ownership of the mark
  - Registrant's exclusive right to use the mark in commerce on the registered goods/services

### (b) Constructive Notice
- Registration constitutes **constructive notice** of the registrant's claim of ownership
- Eliminates good-faith defense of subsequent users

### (c) Application as Constructive Use
- Filing date of application constitutes **constructive use** of the mark
- Establishes nationwide priority as of filing date (contingent on registration)

---

## Section 1058 (section 8) -- Duration; Affidavits and Fees

### Filing Requirements
- Between the **5th and 6th year** after registration: File affidavit/declaration of continued use (or excusable nonuse) with specimen and fee
- Within the year before the end of every **10-year period**: File same affidavit/declaration
- **Grace period**: 6 months after each deadline (with surcharge)
- **Failure to file**: Registration **CANCELLED**

### Excusable Nonuse
- If not in use: Must describe special circumstances that excuse nonuse
- Nonuse must not be due to intent to abandon the mark

---

## Section 1059 (section 9) -- Renewal of Registration

- Registration may be renewed for successive **10-year periods**
- Renewal application must be filed within **1 year before** expiration
- **Grace period**: 6 months after expiration (with surcharge)
- Must include: Verified statement, fee per class, specimen of current use
- **Failure to renew**: Registration **EXPIRES**

---

## Section 1060 (section 10) -- Assignment of Marks
- Marks may be assigned with the goodwill of the business in which the mark is used
- Assignment without goodwill (assignment "in gross") is **VOID**
- Assignment must be recorded in the USPTO

---

## Section 1062 (section 12) -- Publication for Opposition
- **(a)** Approved marks published in the Official Gazette
- **30-day opposition period** from date of publication
- Any person who believes they would be damaged may file opposition with the TTAB

---

## Section 1063 (section 13) -- Opposition to Registration
- Opposition must be filed within 30 days of publication (or extended period)
- Filed with the Trademark Trial and Appeal Board
- Must state grounds for believing damage would result from registration

---

## Section 1064 (section 14) -- Cancellation of Registration

### Timing for Cancellation Petitions
- **Within 5 years**: On any ground that would have prevented registration
- **At any time**: On certain grounds including:
  - Mark has become generic
  - Mark has been abandoned
  - Registration was obtained fraudulently
  - Mark is being used to misrepresent source
  - Certification mark is not being properly controlled
  - Mark is functional

---

## Section 1065 (section 15) -- Incontestability

### Requirements for Incontestable Status
- Mark registered and in continuous use for **5 consecutive years** after registration
- No final adverse decision on registrant's right to use the mark
- No pending proceeding in USPTO or courts
- File affidavit/declaration with the USPTO setting forth the above facts

### Effect of Incontestability
- Registration becomes **conclusive evidence** (not merely prima facie) of:
  - Validity of the registration
  - Registrant's ownership
  - Registrant's exclusive right to use the mark
- Subject only to limited defenses under section 33(b)

---

## Section 1066 (section 16) -- Interference Proceedings
- When application is pending and would interfere with a registered mark
- TTAB may declare an interference
- Largely superseded in practice by opposition and cancellation proceedings

---

## Section 1067 (section 17) -- Interference; Concurrent Use Proceedings
- TTAB has jurisdiction over interferences and concurrent use proceedings
- May determine and fix the conditions and limitations of concurrent registrations

---

## Section 1068 (section 18) -- Refusal, Cancellation, or Restriction
- In inter partes proceedings, TTAB may:
  - Refuse registration
  - Cancel registration
  - Restrict registration with conditions and limitations

---

## Section 1069 (section 19) -- Appeal to Courts
- Any party to an inter partes proceeding who is dissatisfied with the TTAB decision may appeal
- Appeal routes: Federal Circuit or civil action in U.S. district court

---

## Section 1070 (section 20) -- Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit
- Has jurisdiction over appeals from TTAB decisions
- Reviews on the record unless new evidence is admitted

---

## Section 1071 (section 21) -- Appeal to Courts

### (a) Appeal to Federal Circuit
- Filed within 63 days of TTAB decision
- Record-based review
- Electing Federal Circuit waives right to district court action

### (b) Civil Action in District Court
- Filed within 63 days of TTAB decision
- De novo review; new evidence may be introduced
- Cross-appeal deadline: 14 days after service of notice of appeal or 63 days from TTAB decision, whichever is later

---

## Section 1072 (section 22) -- Registration as Constructive Notice
- Registration on the Principal Register constitutes constructive notice of the registrant's claim of ownership
- Nationwide effect from date of registration

## Key Takeaways for Practice

1. **Principal Register provides maximum protection**: Constructive notice, prima facie evidence, potential incontestability, and ability to record with U.S. Customs
2. **Two paths to registration**: Use in commerce (section 1(a)) or intent to use (section 1(b))
3. **Section 2 refusal grounds** are the most commonly litigated -- likelihood of confusion (section 2(d)) and descriptiveness (section 2(e)) are the most frequent refusals
4. **Maintenance is mandatory**: Miss the section 8 or section 9 deadlines and the registration is lost
5. **Incontestability** (section 15) dramatically strengthens the registration but requires 5 years of continuous use and an affidavit filing
6. **Assignment must include goodwill** or it is void -- critical for M&A transactions involving brands
