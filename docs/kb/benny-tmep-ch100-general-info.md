# TMEP Chapter 100 -- General Information

> Source: Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP), 8th Edition, October 2011
> Sections: 101--112

---

## 101 -- Trademark Statute and Rules

Federal trademark registration is governed by the Trademark Act of 1946, 15 U.S.C. sections 1051 et seq. (also known as the Lanham Act), and the implementing rules at 37 C.F.R. Parts 2, 3, 6, 7, 10, and 11. Both the statute and rules are available on the USPTO website at http://www.uspto.gov.

---

## 102 -- USPTO Website

The USPTO website provides access to a wide variety of information about patents and trademarks, including electronic filing of trademark documents.

The Trademark Electronic Business Center contains all information needed for the entire registration process. Through the USPTO website, users can:

- **Search for conflicting marks** using the Trademark Electronic Search System ("TESS")
- **File applications and other documents** using the Trademark Electronic Application System ("TEAS")
- **Check application/registration status** through the Trademark Applications and Registrations Retrieval ("TARR") database
- **View and print images** of trademark application and registration records through the Trademark Document Retrieval ("TDR") portal

Available downloads include: the trademark statute and rules, the TMEP, the Trademark Trial and Appeal Board Manual of Procedure (TBMP), the Acceptable Identification of Goods and Services Manual, Federal Register notices, Official Gazette notices, examination guides, and fee schedules.

---

## 103 -- Trademark Forms

Trademark documents can be filed electronically through TEAS on the USPTO website. See TMEP section 301 regarding TEAS.

Additional forms may be available online or through the Trademark Assistance Center ("TAC") (see TMEP section 108.02).

Key electronic filing systems:

- **TEAS** -- applications and most trademark documents (http://www.uspto.gov)
- **ETAS** -- recordation of assignments and ownership changes (http://etas.uspto.gov)
- **ESTTA** -- extensions of time to oppose, notices of opposition, petitions to cancel, appeals, motions, briefs, and other TTAB documents (http://estta.uspto.gov/)

## 104 -- Trademark Searching

**X-Search** is the USPTO's internal computerized search system, containing text and images of registered marks and marks in pending and abandoned applications. Examining attorneys use X-Search when searching for conflicting marks during examination.

**TESS** (Trademark Electronic Search System) is the public-facing equivalent, available free of charge on the USPTO website. TESS provides access to text and images of registered marks and marks in pending and abandoned applications.

Additional information about pending trademark applications and registered trademarks can be obtained by entering the serial number or registration number in the TARR database. TESS and TARR are available in Patent and Trademark Depository Libraries ("PTDLs") (see TMEP section 112).

### Public Search Facility

The public may use X-Search and TRAM without charge at the Public Search Facility on the first floor of the James Madison Building - East Wing, 600 Dulany Street, Alexandria, Virginia. Hours: 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday (except Federal holidays in DC).

The Public Search Facility contains copies of State emblems and official signs and hallmarks of member countries of the Paris Convention protected under Article 6ter (see TMEP section 1205.02). It does not contain copies of state- and federally recognized Native American tribal insignia -- X-Search or TESS can search for these.

### Design Code Searching

Marks with design elements can be searched using design codes. The Design Search Code Manual is available on the USPTO website.

### Pseudo Mark Field

For some marks, the USPTO adds a "pseudo mark" to search data containing alternate spellings that are phonetically equivalent. For example, "4U" would have "for you" added. Pseudo mark entries are not displayed in search results and are not part of the official record.

### Important Limitations

- USPTO personnel cannot conduct trademark searches for the public
- A private trademark attorney can search and opine on mark availability prior to filing
- The USPTO cannot aid in selecting an attorney (37 C.F.R. section 2.11)

## 106 -- The Official Gazette

The Official Gazette ("OG") is published by the USPTO every Tuesday and is available free of charge on the USPTO website.

### Trademark Official Gazette (TMOG)

The TMOG contains:
- An illustration of each mark published for opposition on the Principal Register
- Marks registered on the Principal Register under 15 U.S.C. section 1051(d)
- Marks registered on the Supplemental Register

Since July 15, 2003, the USPTO publishes the TMOG only in electronic form.

### USPTO Notices

The TMOG does not contain USPTO Notices. These are posted separately in the general information section of the Official Gazette. They include:
- Notices of changes in rules or Office procedures
- Notices to parties who cannot be reached by mail
- Indices of trademark registrations issued, renewed, cancelled, amended, or corrected

Effective January 1, 2008, the USPTO discontinued separate weekly publication of USPTO notices in paper form.

## 108 -- Status Inquiries

### 108.01 -- Internet Information

The **TARR database** (http://tarr.uspto.gov) provides detailed information about the status and prosecution history of trademark applications and registrations. It is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Key TARR limitations:
- Normally updated daily from 1:00 to 2:00 p.m. Eastern Time
- Does not include applications or amendments entered after the last update
- Shows applications registered after last update as pending rather than registered
- May not reflect changes in ownership recorded in the Assignment Services Branch

For ownership changes, the new owner must separately notify the Trademark Operation in writing and request that the Trademark Database be manually updated. See TMEP sections 503.01(a) and 505 et seq.

Assignment Services Branch information can be obtained through ETAS at http://etas.uspto.gov.

### 108.02 -- Personal Telephone Assistance

The Trademark Assistance Center (TAC) provides telephone assistance:
- **Phone**: (571) 272-9250 or (800) 786-9199
- **Phone hours**: 8:30 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday (except DC Federal holidays)
- **Walk-in hours**: 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday (except DC Federal holidays)

TAC will answer questions about the application process. However, USPTO employees **cannot**:
- Conduct trademark searches for the public (see TMEP section 104)
- Comment on the validity of registered marks (see TMEP section 1801)
- Answer whether a particular mark or type of mark is eligible for registration
- Offer legal advice about common-law rights, state registrations, or infringement claims
- Aid in selecting a private trademark attorney or search firm (37 C.F.R. section 2.11)

### 108.03 -- Due Diligence: Duty to Monitor Status

Trademark applicants and registrants should monitor the status of their applications or registrations when a notice or action from the USPTO is expected.

**Recommended monitoring schedule:**

1. **During pendency** -- check application status every six months between the filing date and issuance of a registration
2. **After filing affidavits/renewals** -- check registration status every six months after filing a section 8 or section 71 affidavit, or a section 9 renewal application, until receiving notice that it has been accepted

If a status inquiry reveals that a document is lost, that no action has been taken on correspondence, or that some other problem exists, the applicant or registrant must **promptly request corrective action** (37 C.F.R. section 2.146(i)). Failure to act diligently may result in denial of requested relief. The USPTO may deny petitions to reactivate abandoned applications and cancelled registrations when a party fails to inquire within a reasonable time. See TMEP sections 1705.05 and 1714.01(d).

Written status inquiries are discouraged -- use the TARR database whenever possible.

## 109 -- Access to Records

### Legal Framework

18 U.S.C. section 2071 makes it a federal crime to willfully conceal, remove, mutilate, obliterate, or destroy any record filed with any court or public office, punishable by fine and/or up to three years imprisonment.

No good cause or power to inspect from the applicant/registrant is needed to inspect the contents of a trademark application or registration record.

### 109.01 -- Electronic Image Records

The public may view and print images of trademark application and registration records through the TDR portal on the USPTO website. Electronic images of Board proceeding records are also available via TTABVUE (http://ttabvue.uspto.gov/ttabvue/).

TDR and TTABVUE are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, free of charge.

The public may also view and print records through the Trademark Image Capture and Retrieval System ("TICRS"), available in the Public Search Facility on the USPTO premises. See TMEP section 402.

### 109.02 -- Paper Files

Effective April 12, 2004, the USPTO no longer creates paper files for trademark applications or paper copies of documents submitted electronically. All documents can be viewed through TICRS and TDR.

**Requesting Paper Files:**
- Files within the Trademark Operation or TTAB: direct requests to TAC at 600 Dulany Street, Alexandria, VA (8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.)
- Files at the warehouse: direct requests to TAC or the File Information Unit at Randolph Square Building, 2800 South Randolph Street, Arlington, VA (7:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.)

Paper files for abandoned applications and cancelled/expired registrations are stored in the warehouse for two years after the date of abandonment, cancellation, or expiration, and then destroyed.

No file or related document may be removed from USPTO premises except as required by official process or specifically authorized by the Director (18 U.S.C. section 2071(b)).

### 109.03 -- Making Copies of Materials in Records

The public may print images of records through TDR or TTABVUE on the USPTO website (see TMEP section 109.01). Electronic copies of records pertaining to applications or registrations can be printed through TESS (see TMEP section 104), and prosecution histories through TARR (see TMEP section 108.01). There is no charge for use of these databases.

The public may also print trademark documents from TICRS, TTABVUE, TARR, or X-Search in the Public Search Facility on USPTO premises (fee applies for printing in the Public Search Facility).

## 110 -- Decisions Available to the Public

37 C.F.R. section 2.27(c) provides that decisions of the Director and the Trademark Trial and Appeal Board in applications and proceedings relating thereto are published or available for inspection or publication.

- **Precedential decisions** are noted as such and published in the United States Patents Quarterly (cited as USPQ or USPQ2d)
- **Non-precedential decisions** of the Director and the Board are not published

Both precedential and non-precedential decisions are available for viewing, downloading, and printing via TTABVUE.

A weekly summary of final decisions issued by the Board appears on the USPTO website and in each issue of the Official Gazette.

See TMEP section 1803 regarding decisions available under the Freedom of Information Act.

---

## 111 -- Requests for Copies of Trademark Documents

The public may print images of records through TDR or TTABVUE, print electronic copies through TESS, and prosecution histories through TARR, all at no charge.

The public may also purchase certified or uncertified copies of trademark documents (e.g., application or registration records, trademark title and status reports, etc.). Fee schedules are posted on the USPTO website. Requests are handled by the Document Services Branch of the Public Records Division.

Copies can be ordered through the USPTO website (paid by credit card, EFT, or deposit account) or by e-mail to dsd@uspto.gov.

Requests for certified or uncertified copies may also be mailed to: Mail Stop Document Services, Director of the United States Patent and Trademark Office, P.O. Box 1450, Alexandria VA 22313-1450. 37 C.F.R. section 2.190(d).

---

## 112 -- Patent and Trademark Depository Libraries

A network of PTDLs throughout the United States provides access to many of the same products and services offered at the USPTO. A list of PTDLs and their telephone numbers appears on the USPTO website and in each issue of the Official Gazette.

Information available free of charge at PTDLs includes:
- The trademark statute and rules (see TMEP section 101)
- The TMEP
- The TBMP
- The Acceptable Identification of Goods and Services Manual
- The Trademark Electronic Search System (TESS)

Photocopiers are generally provided for a fee. The scope of PTDL collections, hours, services, and fees may vary by library.
