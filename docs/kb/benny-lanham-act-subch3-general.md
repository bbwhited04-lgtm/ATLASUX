# Lanham Act -- Subchapter III: General Provisions

## Overview

Subchapter III contains the general provisions of the Lanham Act, including the civil action framework for trademark infringement, the definitions of key terms, remedies available to trademark owners, and defenses to infringement claims. This is the subchapter most frequently cited in federal trademark litigation.

---

## Section 1111 (section 29) -- Notice of Registration

### Registration Symbol
- Registrant may give notice of registration by displaying:
  - The federal registration symbol: **R-in-circle** (most common)
  - "Registered in U.S. Patent and Trademark Office"
  - "Reg. U.S. Pat. & Tm. Off."

### Effect of Failure to Give Notice
- If registrant fails to give notice, in an infringement suit:
  - No profits or damages recoverable **UNLESS** defendant had actual notice of registration
  - Injunctive relief still available regardless of notice

> **Practical Compliance**: Always use the (R) symbol with registered marks to preserve full range of monetary remedies.

## Section 1112 (section 30) -- Classification of Goods and Services
- Director establishes classification of goods and services
- International Classification system (Nice Classification) adopted
- Classification does not limit or extend applicant's rights

## Section 1113 (section 31) -- Fees
- Director sets fees by regulation (37 CFR 2.6)
- Fees must be paid in advance

## Section 1114 (section 32) -- Infringement Remedies (Registered Marks)

### (1) Civil Action for Infringement
- Any person who without consent of the registrant:
  - **Uses in commerce** any reproduction, counterfeit, copy, or colorable imitation of a registered mark
  - **In connection with** the sale, offering for sale, distribution, or advertising of goods/services
  - Where such use is **likely to cause confusion**, or to cause mistake, or to deceive
- Shall be liable in a civil action by the registrant

### Remedies Available
- **Injunctive relief** (section 34)
- **Defendant's profits** (section 35)
- **Damages sustained by plaintiff** (section 35)
- **Costs of the action** (section 35)
- **Destruction of infringing articles** (section 36)
- **Attorney's fees** in exceptional cases (section 35)

### Innocent Infringer Defense (Printers/Publishers)
- Printers and publishers who innocently infringe are liable only for injunction
- Not liable for profits or damages if they had no knowledge of infringing nature

---

## Section 1116 (section 34) -- Injunctive Relief

### (a) Power to Grant Injunctions
- Courts have power to grant injunctions according to principles of equity
- May grant preliminary and permanent injunctions to prevent trademark violations

### (b) Seizure Orders (Ex Parte)
- In extraordinary cases involving counterfeit marks, court may order ex parte seizure of:
  - Goods bearing the counterfeit mark
  - Means of making the counterfeit mark
  - Records documenting manufacture, sale, or receipt of counterfeit items

### (d) Temporary Restraining Orders
- Available in accordance with FRCP Rule 65
- Specific requirements for TROs in trademark cases

---

## Section 1117 (section 35) -- Recovery of Profits, Damages, and Costs

### (a) General Recovery
- Plaintiff entitled to recover:
  1. **Defendant's profits**: Subject to principles of equity; court may adjust upward or downward
  2. **Actual damages**: Damages sustained by the plaintiff
  3. **Costs of the action**
- Court may enter judgment for up to **3 times actual damages** (treble damages) -- but not meant to be a penalty
- **Attorney's fees**: Available to prevailing party in **exceptional cases**

### (b) Counterfeit Marks -- Statutory Damages
- Instead of actual damages and profits, plaintiff may elect **statutory damages**:
  - Not less than **$1,000** and not more than **$200,000** per counterfeit mark per type of goods/services sold
  - If court finds willful use: Up to **$2,000,000** per counterfeit mark per type of goods/services

### (c) Willful Infringement with Counterfeit Mark
- Treble profits or damages (whichever is greater) available
- Plus attorney's fees
- Unless court finds extenuating circumstances

---

## Section 1118 (section 36) -- Destruction of Infringing Articles
- Court may order that all labels, signs, prints, packages, wrappers, receptacles, and advertisements bearing the infringing mark be delivered up and destroyed

---

## Section 1119 (section 37) -- Power of Court Over Registration
- In any action involving a registered mark, the court may:
  - Order cancellation of registrations
  - Order restoration of cancelled registrations
  - Otherwise rectify the register
- Must provide notice to the Director

---

## Section 1120 (section 38) -- Liability for False or Fraudulent Registration
- Any person who procures registration of a mark through fraud or false representation is liable for damages to any person injured thereby

---

## Section 1121 (section 39) -- Federal Court Jurisdiction

### (a) Original Jurisdiction
- U.S. district courts have **original jurisdiction** over all actions arising under the Lanham Act
- No amount-in-controversy requirement
- No diversity of citizenship required

### (b) Supplemental Jurisdiction
- Courts may exercise supplemental jurisdiction over related state-law claims

## Section 1125 (section 43) -- False Designations of Origin; Dilution

### (a) Unfair Competition -- False Designation of Origin
- Any person who uses in commerce any word, term, name, symbol, or device, or false designation of origin, that is likely to cause confusion as to:
  - The affiliation, connection, or association of the person with another
  - The origin, sponsorship, or approval of goods/services/commercial activities
- Is liable in a civil action by any person who believes they are or likely to be damaged
- **Applies to both registered AND unregistered marks** (this is the federal unfair competition provision)

### (b) False Advertising
- Any person who misrepresents the nature, characteristics, qualities, or geographic origin of their own or another person's goods/services in commercial advertising is liable
- Standing: Any person who believes they are or likely to be damaged (typically competitors)

### (c) Dilution by Blurring or Tarnishment (Famous Marks)

#### (1) Injunctive Relief
- Owner of a **famous mark** may obtain injunction against another person who uses a mark in commerce that is likely to cause:
  - **Dilution by blurring**: Association arising from similarity that impairs distinctiveness of the famous mark
  - **Dilution by tarnishment**: Association arising from similarity that harms reputation of the famous mark

#### (2) Factors for Dilution by Blurring
1. Degree of similarity between the marks
2. Degree of inherent or acquired distinctiveness of the famous mark
3. Extent to which the famous mark owner is engaging in substantially exclusive use
4. Degree of recognition of the famous mark
5. Whether the user intended to create an association with the famous mark
6. Any actual association between the marks

#### (3) Exclusions from Dilution Claims
- **Fair use**: Advertising or promotion to identify competing goods/services; parody, criticism, or comment
- **News reporting and commentary**
- **Noncommercial use**

#### (4) Famous Mark Defined
- Widely recognized by the general consuming public of the U.S. as a designation of source
- Factors: Duration, extent, and reach of advertising/publicity; amount, volume, and geographic extent of sales; extent of actual recognition

## Section 1125a (section 43a) -- Cyberpiracy Prevention (ACPA)

### (d) Anticybersquatting Consumer Protection Act
- Prohibits bad-faith registration, trafficking in, or use of domain names that are identical or confusingly similar to a distinctive mark, or dilutive of a famous mark
- Bad-faith factors include:
  - Trademark or other IP rights in the domain name
  - Whether the domain name is the legal name of the person
  - Prior use of the domain name in connection with bona fide goods/services
  - Noncommercial or fair use of the mark
  - Intent to divert consumers from the mark owner's site
  - Offer to sell the domain to the mark owner for financial gain without prior use
  - Provision of misleading false contact information when registering
  - Registration of multiple domain names known to be identical/confusingly similar to marks of others
- **In rem jurisdiction**: Where personal jurisdiction cannot be obtained, action may be brought against the domain name itself in the judicial district where the domain name registrar is located

## Section 1126 (section 44) -- International Conventions

### (a) Reciprocal Rights
- Citizens of countries that are members of international trademark conventions (Paris Convention, etc.) have the same rights as U.S. nationals

### (b) Benefits of Section 44
- Foreign applicants may register marks in the U.S. based on:
  - **Section 44(d)**: Priority claim based on foreign application (within 6 months)
  - **Section 44(e)**: Registration based on foreign registration

### (d) Priority Filing
- Applicant from a treaty country who has filed in their home country may claim priority for a U.S. application filed within 6 months
- Priority date = foreign filing date

### (e) Registration Based on Foreign Registration
- Applicant who has a valid registration in their country of origin may obtain U.S. registration
- Not required to prove use in U.S. commerce at time of registration
- But must eventually use in U.S. commerce or show excusable nonuse to maintain registration

## Section 1127 (section 45) -- Key Definitions

### Critical Definitions for Practice

- **Trademark**: Any word, name, symbol, device, or any combination thereof used to identify and distinguish goods from those manufactured or sold by others, and to indicate the source of the goods
- **Service mark**: A mark used in the sale or advertising of services to identify and distinguish the services
- **Certification mark**: A mark used to certify regional or other origin, material, mode of manufacture, quality, accuracy, or that labor was performed by union members
- **Collective mark**: A trademark or service mark used by members of a cooperative, association, or other collective group
- **Mark**: Includes any trademark, service mark, collective mark, or certification mark
- **Trade name**: Any name used by a person to identify a business or vocation (NOT registrable as a trademark per se, but section 43(a) provides protection)
- **Commerce**: All commerce which may lawfully be regulated by Congress
- **Use in commerce**:
  - For goods: Mark placed on goods/containers/tags/labels/displays, AND the goods are sold or transported in commerce
  - For services: Mark used or displayed in the sale or advertising of services rendered in commerce or more than one state
- **Abandonment**:
  - Nonuse for **3 consecutive years** creates a prima facie presumption of abandonment
  - OR any course of conduct causing the mark to lose its significance as a mark (genericide, naked licensing)
- **Dilution by blurring**: Association arising from similarity between marks that impairs distinctiveness of famous mark
- **Dilution by tarnishment**: Association arising from similarity that harms the reputation of the famous mark
- **Person**: Includes any state, instrumentality, officer, or employee acting in official capacity (abrogating state sovereign immunity for trademark infringement)
- **Colorable imitation**: Includes any mark that so resembles a registered mark as to be likely to cause confusion, mistake, or deception
- **Counterfeit mark**: Spurious mark identical or substantially indistinguishable from a registered mark

## Section 1128 (section 46) -- Construction and Interpretation
- Act construed to carry out purposes of international conventions to which the U.S. is party

## Section 1129 (section 47) -- Separability
- If any provision is held invalid, remainder of Act continues in force
