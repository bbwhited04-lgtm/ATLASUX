# 37 CFR Part 2 -- Rules of Practice in Trademark Cases

> Source: 37 CFR Part 2 (7-1-24 Edition), U.S. Patent and Trademark Office, Department of Commerce
> Processed for Atlas UX Knowledge Base

## Subpart A -- General Information and Correspondence (sections 2.1-2.20)

### section 2.1 -- Definitions
- **Director**: Under Secretary of Commerce for Intellectual Property and Director of the USPTO
- **Office**: United States Patent and Trademark Office
- **Act of 1946**: Trademark Act of July 5, 1946 (Lanham Act), 15 U.S.C. 1051 et seq.
- **Trademark** (section 45 of the Act): Any word, name, symbol, device, or combination used to identify and distinguish goods
- **Service mark** (section 45): Same as trademark but used to identify and distinguish services
- **Collective mark** (section 45): Trademark or service mark used by members of a cooperative, association, or other collective group
- **Certification mark** (section 45): Mark used to certify regional origin, material, mode of manufacture, quality, accuracy, or that work was performed by union members

### section 2.2 -- Definitions (Supplemental)
- **(a) Domicile**: Permanent legal place of residence for individuals; state/country of incorporation or organization for entities
- **(k) Use in commerce** for trademarks/service marks: Bona fide use in ordinary course of trade
  - For goods: Mark placed on goods, containers, tags, labels, or documents associated with goods, and goods are sold or transported in commerce
  - For services: Mark used or displayed in sale or advertising of services rendered in commerce
- **(n) Applicant**: Includes assignee of record
- **(o) Domicile address**: Physical street address where applicant/registrant resides and intends to be principal home; for entities, principal place of business

### section 2.6 -- Fees (Key Fee Schedule)
- **Application filing fees** (per class):
  - Electronic application (TEAS): Base fee of $350/class (effective Jan 18, 2025)
  - Paper filing: $750/class
- **Additional fees (effective Jan 18, 2025)**:
  - Insufficient information fee: $100/class
  - Free-form text identification fee: $200/class
  - Excess character identification fee: $200 per 1,000 characters beyond first 1,000
- **Statement of Use**: $150/class
- **Extension of time for filing SOU**: $225/class
- **Section 8 affidavit/declaration**: $325/class (electronic); $425/class (paper)
- **Section 9 renewal**: $350/class (electronic); $550/class (paper)
- **Section 15 affidavit**: $250/class (electronic); $350/class (paper)
- **Opposition filing**: $600/class per party
- **Cancellation petition**: $600/class per party
- **Ex parte appeal**: $225/class
- **Letter of protest**: $50

> **Compliance Note**: Fees must be paid in advance. The Office will not refund amounts of $25 or less unless specifically requested. Fee refund requests must be filed within 2 years.

### section 2.11 -- Written Application Requirements
- Applications must be submitted electronically through TEAS
- Paper applications only accepted if TEAS is unavailable or with petition to the Director

### section 2.17 -- Representation and Correspondence
- **(a)** Applicants domiciled outside the U.S. MUST be represented by a U.S.-licensed attorney
- **(b)** Attorney requirements: Active member of bar of highest court of any U.S. state, D.C., or any U.S. Commonwealth or territory
- **(b)(3)** Attorney bar information required: Name, year of admission, state, membership number
- **(g)** Domestic representative: Required for applicants/registrants not domiciled in the U.S. who are not represented by an attorney

### section 2.18 -- Use of TEAS
- All trademark documents must be filed electronically through TEAS
- TTAB proceedings must be filed through ESTTA

### section 2.20 -- Declarations in Lieu of Oaths
- Verified statements may be made by declaration under 37 CFR 2.20 in lieu of oath
- Must include: "The signatory being warned that willful false statements and the like are punishable by fine or imprisonment, or both, under 18 U.S.C. 1001..."

## Subpart B -- The Written Application (sections 2.21-2.45)

### section 2.21 -- Filing Date Requirements
An application must include:
1. Name of applicant
2. Name and address for correspondence
3. A clear drawing of the mark (or standard character claim)
4. A listing of goods/services
5. The filing basis (sections 1(a), 1(b), 44(d), 44(e), or 66(a))
6. Verification/declaration
7. The filing fee for at least one class

> **Deadline**: Filing date is accorded on the date the Office receives minimum filing requirements.

### section 2.22 -- Application Requirements (Comprehensive)
Under section 2.22(a)(1)-(20), a complete application must include:
1. Applicant name
2. Applicant entity type and state/country of organization
3. Applicant domicile address
4. Applicant mailing address (if different from domicile)
5. Applicant email address
6. Designation of domestic representative (if applicant not domiciled in U.S. and not represented by U.S. attorney)
7. Attorney information (if represented)
8. Correspondence address
9. A clear drawing/image of the mark
10. Color claim (if applicable)
11. Description of the mark (if applicable)
12. Translation (if mark contains non-English wording)
13. Transliteration (if mark contains non-Latin characters)
14. Identification of goods/services from the ID Manual (or with sufficient specificity)
15. International class number(s)
16. Filing basis and required basis-specific information
17. Specimen (if section 1(a) basis)
18. Declaration/verification
19. Signature
20. Filing fee

### section 2.23 -- Filing Bases

#### Section 1(a) -- Use in Commerce
Requirements:
- Date of first use of mark anywhere
- Date of first use of mark in commerce
- One specimen per class showing mark as used in commerce
- Verified statement of use

#### Section 1(b) -- Intent to Use
Requirements:
- Verified statement of bona fide intention to use mark in commerce
- **No specimen required at filing** -- specimen required with Statement of Use or Amendment to Allege Use

#### Section 44(d) -- Foreign Priority
Requirements:
- Country of foreign filing
- Foreign application number
- Date of foreign filing
- **Must be filed within 6 months of foreign filing date**
- Does NOT provide a basis for registration by itself -- must also claim 44(e) or convert to 1(a)/1(b)

#### Section 44(e) -- Foreign Registration
Requirements:
- Country of foreign registration
- Foreign registration number
- Date of foreign registration
- Certified copy of foreign registration (or certified copy from International Bureau for Madrid Protocol)
- Expiration date of foreign registration

#### Section 66(a) -- Madrid Protocol
- Filed through International Bureau of WIPO
- No direct filing with USPTO -- request for extension of protection to the U.S.

### section 2.32 -- Applicant Information
- Name must be the name of the owner of the mark
- Entity type must be specified (individual, corporation, partnership, LLC, etc.)
- State/country of incorporation or organization required for entities

### section 2.33 -- Drawing Requirements
- Clear drawing of the mark required
- **Standard character mark**: No design element; letters, numbers, punctuation only
- **Special form mark**: Mark includes design, stylization, or color
- If color is claimed: Color claim statement required, AND description must specify where colors appear

### section 2.34 -- Specimens
- For **Section 1(a)** applications: At least one specimen per class
- For **goods**: Must show mark as used on or in connection with goods (tags, labels, packaging, displays at point of sale, website product pages)
- For **services**: Must show mark as used in sale or advertising of services (business cards, letterhead, advertisements, website pages offering services)
- Specimen must be a flat representation (JPG/PDF), no larger than 5MB
- Must show mark as actually used -- mockups not acceptable

> **Common Rejection**: Specimens that merely show the mark as an ornamental design on goods (e.g., large decorative word on front of t-shirt) may be refused as merely ornamental.

## Subpart C -- Examination of Application; Amendments (sections 2.61-2.75)

### section 2.61 -- Action by Examining Attorney
- Examiner reviews application for compliance with Lanham Act and rules
- If not approvable, examiner issues an **Office Action** stating reasons for refusal and requirements

### section 2.62 -- Response to Office Actions

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Response must be filed within **3 months** from the date of the Office Action.
> - May be extended by **3 additional months** (to a maximum of 6 months total) upon timely request and payment of extension fee
> - Extension request must be received by Office on or before the response deadline
> - If no response is filed within 6 months, the application is **ABANDONED**

- Response must address EVERY ground of refusal and requirement
- Applicant may amend application to overcome refusals

### section 2.63 -- Office Action After Response
- If response does not overcome all refusals: Examiner may issue a **Final Office Action**
- After Final: Applicant may file:
  1. Request for reconsideration (within 3 months of final action)
  2. Appeal to TTAB (within 3 months of final action)
  3. Both (request for reconsideration does not extend appeal deadline)

### section 2.64 -- Abandonment
- Application is abandoned if applicant fails to respond within the response period
- **Revival**: Petition to revive under section 2.66 if failure was unintentional
  - Must be filed within 2 months of actual knowledge of abandonment, and no later than 6 months after date of abandonment in electronic records

### section 2.71 -- Amendments to the Application
- May amend identification of goods/services to clarify or restrict (but not broaden)
- May not amend to add goods/services
- May amend filing basis

### section 2.72 -- Classification and Identification
- Examiner may require classification changes
- Identification must be specific enough that the nature of goods/services is clear

### section 2.73 -- Amendment to Allege Use (Before Publication)
- Available only for Section 1(b) applications
- Filed BEFORE approval for publication
- Requires: Specimen, dates of use, verified statement
- Cannot be filed during blackout period (between approval for publication and issuance of Notice of Allowance)

### section 2.75 -- Amendment to Recite Section 44(e) or 66(a) as Basis
- Section 44(d) applicants must perfect registration basis before registration issues

## Subpart D -- Publication; Post-Publication (sections 2.80-2.89)

### section 2.80 -- Publication for Opposition
- If examiner approves, mark published in the Official Gazette for 30-day opposition period
- Any party who believes it would be damaged may oppose

### section 2.81 -- Post-Publication Amendments
- After publication, amendments only in limited circumstances
- Voluntary amendments generally not permitted after publication

### section 2.84 -- Issuance of Certificate (Section 1(a) Applications)
- If no opposition filed (or opposition resolved), registration certificate issues

### section 2.88 -- Notice of Allowance (Section 1(b) Applications)
- If no opposition filed, USPTO issues **Notice of Allowance (NOA)**

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Statement of Use must be filed within **6 months** of the NOA date.

### section 2.89 -- Extensions of Time for Statement of Use

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Must request extension BEFORE expiration of current SOU filing period.

- **(a) First extension**: 6-month extension; requires:
  1. Written request
  2. Fee ($225/class)
  3. Verified statement of continued bona fide intention to use

- **(b)-(c) Subsequent extensions**: Up to **4 additional** 6-month extensions (maximum 24 months total extensions)
  - Requires showing of **good cause** for extensions beyond the first
  - Total time from NOA to SOU filing: Up to **36 months** (6 initial + 5 x 6-month extensions)

- **(d) Good cause** must include:
  - For TM/SM: Statement of ongoing efforts to use mark in commerce (product development, market research, manufacturing, seeking distributors, governmental approval)
  - OR satisfactory explanation for failure to make efforts to use

- **(g) Notice of grant or denial**: Applicant will be notified
  - After denial, if time remains in the SOU period, may file substitute request correcting defects
  - Otherwise: Petition to Director under section 2.66 or 2.146 within 2 months of denial

## Subpart E -- Ex Parte Expungement and Reexamination (sections 2.91-2.94)

### section 2.91 -- Petition for Expungement or Reexamination
- **Expungement**: Mark registered under sections 1, 44, or 66 and has **never** been used in commerce on/in connection with some/all goods or services
  - Filed 3-10 years after registration (for petitions after Dec 27, 2023)
- **Reexamination**: Mark registered under section 1 and was **not in use** at relevant date
  - Filed within 5 years of registration
- Must be filed through TEAS
- Complete petition includes: Fee, registration number, basis, petitioner info, attorney info (if applicable), identification of goods/services, verified statement with reasonable investigation of nonuse, documentary evidence

### section 2.92 -- Institution of Ex Parte Proceedings
- Director determines whether to institute based on prima facie case of nonuse
- May also institute on Director's own initiative

### section 2.93 -- Procedures
- **Office Action** issued to registrant requiring evidence of use
- **Response deadline**: 3 months (extendable by 1 month for non-final actions)
- Registrant must provide evidence of use or showing of excusable nonuse
- Final Office Action may issue if response insufficient
- After Final: Reconsideration or appeal to TTAB within 3 months

### section 2.94 -- Action After Termination
- If registrant rebuts nonuse: Notice of termination; registration maintained
- If registrant fails: Registration cancelled in whole or in part

## Subpart F -- Concurrent Use Proceedings (section 2.99)

### section 2.99 -- Application to Register as Concurrent User
- Examined same as other applications
- Published for opposition in Official Gazette
- If no opposition: TTAB sends notice of institution for concurrent use registration
- Answer due within 40 days (for concurrent users named in application)
- Court decree may establish concurrent use rights

## Subpart G -- Opposition (sections 2.101-2.107)

### section 2.101 -- Filing an Opposition
- Commenced by filing timely notice of opposition with required fee
- Any person who believes they would be damaged by registration may oppose
- **Must be filed through ESTTA** (Electronic System for Trademark Trials and Appeals)

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Opposition must be filed within **30 days** of publication in Official Gazette.

### section 2.102 -- Extension of Time for Filing Opposition
- **(a)(1)** First request: 30-day or 90-day extension
  - 30-day granted upon request
  - 90-day only for good cause
  - 60-day NOT available as first extension
- **(a)(2)** After initial 30-day extension: Additional 60-day extension (good cause required)
- **(a)(3)** After one or two extensions totaling 90 days: One final 60-day extension (requires written consent of applicant or showing of extraordinary circumstances)

> **Maximum extension**: 180 days from publication date. Extension request must be filed before 30 days from publication or before expiration of previously granted extension.

### section 2.104 -- Contents of Opposition
- Short and plain statement of why opposer believes they would be damaged
- ESTTA requires selection of relevant grounds for opposition

### section 2.105 -- Notification to Parties
- TTAB prepares notice of institution
- Answer due not less than 30 days from mailing of notice

### section 2.106 -- Answer
- Failure to file timely answer: Opposition may be decided as in case of default
- Answer must state defenses in short and plain terms, admit or deny allegations
- Counterclaims attacking validity of opposer's registrations are **compulsory** if grounds exist at time of answer

### section 2.107 -- Amendment of Pleadings
- May be amended same manner and extent as in U.S. district court
- After close of opposition period: May not add goods/services or add joint opposer

## Subpart H -- Cancellation (sections 2.111-2.115)

### section 2.111 -- Filing Petition for Cancellation
- Any person who believes they are or will be damaged by a registration
- **Must be filed through ESTTA**
- May be filed at any time for registrations on Supplemental Register
- For Principal Register registrations:
  - On grounds under section 14(3) or 14(5): At any time after 3-year period
  - On grounds under section 14(6) (nonuse): Within 5 years of registration
  - All other grounds: Within 5 years of registration or date of publication under section 12(c)

### section 2.112 -- Contents of Petition
- Short and plain statement of grounds
- Indicate name, address, and email of current owner (to best of petitioner's knowledge)

### section 2.114 -- Answer
- Same requirements as opposition answer
- Failure to file timely answer: Petition may be decided as in case of default
- If cancellation based solely on abandonment/nonuse, default judgment entered, $200/class petition fee refunded

## Subpart I -- Procedure in Inter Partes Proceedings (sections 2.116-2.136)

### section 2.116 -- Federal Rules of Civil Procedure
- Inter partes proceedings governed by FRCP where applicable
- Standard protective order automatically imposed

### section 2.119 -- Service and Signing
- All submissions (except notice of opposition/cancellation) must be served on adverse party
- Service by email required unless otherwise stipulated

### section 2.120 -- Discovery
- **Discovery period**: 180 days
- **Discovery conference**: No later than opening of discovery period
- **Initial disclosures**: Within 30 days of opening of discovery
- **Interrogatories**: Maximum 75 (counting subparts)
- **Requests for production**: Maximum 75 (counting subparts)
- **Requests for admission**: Maximum 75 (counting subparts)
- **Responses to discovery**: Within 30 days of service

### section 2.121 -- Assignment of Times for Testimony
- Trial order issued setting pretrial disclosure deadlines and testimony periods
- **Rebuttal testimony period**: 15 days
- **All other testimony periods**: 30 days

### section 2.122 -- Matters in Evidence
- Federal Rules of Evidence apply (with modifications)
- Registration certificates received as evidence by original or photocopy showing current status

### section 2.123 -- Trial Testimony in Inter Partes Cases
- Testimony may be by affidavit/declaration or deposition
- Oral cross-examination right preserved

### section 2.127 -- Motions
- Brief in response: Within 20 days of service
- Motion for summary judgment: Available after initial disclosures made
- **Potentially dispositive motions** suspend proceedings

### section 2.128 -- Briefs at Final Hearing
- Plaintiff's brief: Within 60 days after close of rebuttal testimony
- Defendant's brief: Within 30 days of plaintiff's brief
- Reply brief: Within 15 days of defendant's brief

### section 2.129 -- Oral Argument; Reconsideration
- Must be requested within 10 days after due date for last reply brief
- Limited to 30 minutes per party
- **Reconsideration of final decision**: Within 1 month of the decision

### section 2.132 -- Involuntary Dismissal
- If plaintiff fails to take testimony or offer evidence, Board may grant judgment for defendant

### section 2.134 -- Surrender or Voluntary Cancellation
- If respondent surrenders registration without consent of all adverse parties, judgment entered against respondent

### section 2.135 -- Abandonment of Application or Mark
- If applicant abandons during opposition/concurrent use/interference, judgment entered against applicant

### section 2.136 -- Status After Termination
- If judgment not adverse: Application/registration returns to prior status
- If judgment adverse: Application refused or registration cancelled

## Subpart J -- Appeals (sections 2.141-2.145)

### section 2.141 -- Ex Parte Appeals
- After final refusal by examining attorney, applicant may appeal to TTAB
- Appeal fee required per class

### section 2.142 -- Time and Manner of Ex Parte Appeals

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Notice of appeal must be filed within time specified in section 2.62(a) -- i.e., within the **3-month response period** from final refusal (extendable to 6 months total).

- For expungement/reexamination: Within 3 months of final Office action
- Appellant's brief: Within **60 days** of filing notice of appeal
- Examining attorney's brief: Within **60 days** after receiving appellant's brief
- Appellant's reply brief: Within **20 days** of examining attorney's brief

### section 2.144 -- Reconsideration of Decision on Ex Parte Appeal
- Request for rehearing: Within **1 month** of decision
- May be extended for sufficient cause

### section 2.145 -- Appeal to Court and Civil Action
- **(a)** Appeal to U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit under section 21(a) of the Act
  - Notice of appeal filed within **63 days** of final TTAB/Director decision
- **(b)** Election to proceed under section 21(b): Civil action in U.S. district court
  - Waives right to Federal Circuit appeal
- **(c)** Civil action under section 21(b):
  - Must be commenced within **63 days** of final TTAB/Director decision

## Subpart K -- Petitions and Action by the Director (sections 2.146-2.149)

### section 2.146 -- Petitions to the Director
- Available from any repeated or final formal requirement of examiner
- For questions of substance, not appropriate for petition (use appeal instead)

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Petition must be filed within **2 months** after issue date of action or date of receipt, unless different deadline applies.

### section 2.147 -- Petition to Accept Paper Submission
- When TEAS is unavailable on deadline date
- Petition must include proof of unavailability

### section 2.148 -- Director May Suspend Rules
- In extraordinary situations, Director may suspend rules

### section 2.149 -- Letters of Protest
- Third parties may submit evidence relevant to examination
- Filed through TEAS; fee of $50
- Must be filed within 30 days of publication for opposition
- Limited to 10 items of evidence and 75 total pages per refusal ground

### section 2.151 -- Certificate
- When mark found registrable, certificate issues on Principal or Supplemental Register
- States filing date, act of registration, date of issue, registration number

## Subpart M -- Post-Registration: Maintenance (sections 2.160-2.186)

### section 2.160 -- Section 8 Affidavit/Declaration of Continued Use

> **KEY DEADLINES**:
> - Must be filed between the **5th and 6th anniversary** of registration
> - And within the year before the end of every **10-year period** after registration
> - **Grace period**: 6 months after deadline (with surcharge)

- Must include: Verified statement of use (or excusable nonuse), specimen per class, fee per class
- If not filed: Registration **CANCELLED**

### section 2.161 -- Requirements for Complete Section 8 Affidavit
- Verified statement attesting to use or excusable nonuse
- U.S. registration number
- Fee per class
- Goods/services for which mark is in use (or excusable nonuse claimed)
- One specimen per class (unless claiming excusable nonuse)
- If excusable nonuse: Date use stopped, expected resume date, special circumstances

### section 2.163 -- Acknowledgment and Refusal
- If deficient: Office issues notice of deficiency
- Response to refusal: Within 3 months (extendable to 6 months)
- If no response: Registration cancelled (unless grace period remains)

### section 2.167 -- Section 15 Affidavit (Incontestability)
- Filed to acquire incontestable status under section 15 of the Act
- Requirements:
  - Verified statement
  - Mark in continuous use for 5 years after registration/publication
  - Still in use in commerce
  - No final adverse decision
  - No pending proceeding in Office or courts
  - Filed within 1 year after any 5-year period of continuous use following registration

### section 2.181 -- Term of Registration and Renewals
- Registrations issued/renewed before Nov 16, 1989: 20-year terms
- Registrations issued/renewed on/after Nov 16, 1989: **10-year terms**
- Renewable for additional 10-year periods

### section 2.182 -- Time for Filing Renewal

> **KEY DEADLINE**: Renewal must be filed within **1 year before** expiration of registration.
> - **Grace period**: 6 months after expiration (with surcharge)
> - If not filed within grace period: Registration **EXPIRES**

### section 2.183 -- Requirements for Complete Renewal
- Request for renewal signed by registrant or representative
- Fee per class
- Grace period surcharge per class (if applicable)
- List of goods/services/classes to renew (if fewer than all)

## Subpart N -- General Information and Correspondence (sections 2.189-2.200)

### section 2.189 -- Requirement to Provide Domicile Address
- Applicant/registrant must provide and maintain current domicile address

### section 2.190 -- Addresses for Trademark Correspondence
- **Paper**: Commissioner for Trademarks, P.O. Box 1451, Alexandria, VA 22313-1451
- **Hand delivery**: Trademark Assistance Center, James Madison Building, East Wing, Concourse Level, 600 Dulany Street, Alexandria, VA 22314
- **Electronic**: Through TEAS
- **TTAB proceedings**: Through ESTTA
- **Madrid Protocol**: Madrid Processing Unit, 600 Dulany Street, Alexandria, VA 22314-5796

### section 2.193 -- Signature Requirements
- Electronic signature: Any combination of letters, numbers, spaces, punctuation between forward slashes (e.g., /john doe/)
- Signatory must be identified by first and last name and title/position

### section 2.195 -- Filing Date of Correspondence
- Electronic: Date Office receives submission (Eastern Time)
- Paper: Date Office receives submission
- Priority Mail Express: Date of deposit with USPS
- Certificate of mailing: Date of deposit with USPS (if properly certified)

### section 2.196 -- Weekend/Holiday Deadlines
- If deadline falls on Saturday, Sunday, or Federal holiday in D.C., extended to next business day

## Subpart O -- Fees and Payment (sections 2.206-2.209)

### section 2.206 -- Fees Payable in Advance
- All fees paid in advance
- Must be itemized per application/registration/proceeding

### section 2.207 -- Methods of Payment
- U.S. dollars only
- Accepted: Cashier's check, certified check, Treasury note, national bank note, USPS money order, credit card, deposit account, EFT

### section 2.209 -- Refunds
- Refunds for fees paid by mistake or in excess
- No refund for amounts of $25 or less (unless specifically requested)
- Refund requests must be filed within 2 years

## Key Compliance Deadlines Summary

| Action | Deadline | Extension Available? |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| Response to Office Action | 3 months | Yes, to 6 months total |
| Statement of Use after NOA | 6 months | Yes, up to 36 months total |
| Opposition after publication | 30 days | Yes, up to 180 days total |
| Answer to opposition/cancellation | 30 days minimum | Set by Board |
| Section 8 affidavit | 5th-6th year; then each 10-year period | 6-month grace period |
| Section 9 renewal | 1 year before expiration | 6-month grace period |
| Ex parte appeal (after final refusal) | Within 3-month response period | Extendable to 6 months |
| Appeal to Federal Circuit | 63 days from TTAB decision | Extension on showing of good cause |
| Petition to Director | 2 months from action date | Not generally |
| Petition to revive abandoned application | 2 months from knowledge; max 6 months | No |
| Reconsideration of TTAB decision | 1 month from decision | May be extended for cause |
