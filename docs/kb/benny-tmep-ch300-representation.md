# TMEP Chapter 300 -- Filing and Receipt of Documents

> Source: Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP), 8th Edition, October 2011
> Sections: 301--309

---

## 301 -- Electronic Filing of Trademark Documents

The Trademark Electronic Application System (TEAS) is the USPTO's primary electronic filing system for trademark applications and related documents. TEAS is available on the USPTO website at http://www.uspto.gov.

### 301.01 -- Types of TEAS Forms

TEAS offers several filing options:

- **TEAS Plus** -- reduced filing fee; requires selection of goods/services from the USPTO's Acceptable Identification of Goods and Services Manual; all correspondence must be electronic
- **TEAS Regular (TEAS RF)** -- standard filing fee; allows free-form identification of goods/services
- **TEAS** -- used for most post-filing documents, including responses to Office actions, amendments, and other correspondence

### 301.02 -- Filing Date for TEAS Submissions

A TEAS submission receives a filing date as of the date and time (Eastern Time) the electronic transmission is received by the USPTO server. The system generates an automatic confirmation with a serial number for new applications.

If a TEAS submission is incomplete (e.g., missing required fields), the system may reject it before assigning a filing date. The applicant must correct and resubmit.

### 301.03 -- Advantages of Electronic Filing

- Immediate confirmation of receipt and serial number assignment
- Reduced processing time
- Lower filing fees (TEAS Plus)
- 24/7 availability
- Automatic formatting checks that reduce errors

## 302 -- Correspondence and Signature Requirements

### 302.01 -- Correspondence Address

Every trademark application must include an address for correspondence. The USPTO sends all communications to the correspondence address on file. It is the applicant's responsibility to keep the correspondence address current.

Changes to the correspondence address can be filed through TEAS or in writing. If an applicant changes attorneys, the new attorney should file a change of correspondence address.

### 302.02 -- Signature Requirements

Trademark documents must be signed by the appropriate person:

- **Declarations and verifications** -- must be signed by the applicant (or a person authorized to bind the applicant), with a declaration under penalty of perjury
- **Responses to Office actions** -- may be signed by the applicant or the applicant's attorney
- **Other correspondence** -- may be signed by the applicant, attorney, or other authorized representative

For TEAS filings, an electronic signature is accepted. The signature must be entered between two forward slashes (e.g., /John Smith/).

### 302.03 -- Who May Sign

- **Individual applicant** -- the individual
- **Corporation** -- an officer of the corporation (e.g., president, vice president, secretary, treasurer)
- **Partnership** -- a general partner
- **LLC** -- a member or manager authorized to bind the entity
- **Joint applicants** -- all joint applicants or one authorized to sign on behalf of all

The signatory must have firsthand knowledge of the facts stated in a declaration or verification, or a reasonable basis for belief.

## 303 -- Receipt of Documents at the USPTO

### 303.01 -- Filing Date Assigned

The filing date for paper correspondence is the date it is received at the USPTO. The filing date for TEAS submissions is the date and time of electronic receipt. The filing date for fax transmissions is the date the complete transmission is received by a USPTO fax machine.

If the last day of a filing period falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or Federal holiday in the District of Columbia, the filing period extends to the next business day.

### 303.02 -- Filing Receipts

For applications filed through TEAS, the system generates an immediate electronic filing receipt showing the serial number, filing date, mark, applicant name, and other key data.

For paper-filed applications, a filing receipt is mailed to the correspondence address after the application is entered into the USPTO database. Applicants should check TARR to verify application data.

## 304 -- Electronic Mail (E-mail)

### 304.01 -- USPTO E-mail Communications

The USPTO uses e-mail for certain communications with applicants who have authorized e-mail correspondence. Authorization for e-mail correspondence is granted when an applicant provides an e-mail address through TEAS.

TEAS Plus applicants are required to authorize and use e-mail correspondence.

### 304.02 -- Office Actions by E-mail

The USPTO may issue Office actions by e-mail to applicants who have authorized e-mail correspondence. The date of the Office action is the date it is sent by e-mail. Response periods run from that date.

### 304.05 -- Certificates of Transmission by E-mail

E-mail submissions to the USPTO generally do not receive a filing date unless submitted through an authorized USPTO electronic system (TEAS, ESTTA, ETAS). Informal e-mail correspondence to examining attorneys or other USPTO personnel is not considered a proper filing.

## 305 -- Mailing Procedures

### 305.01 -- Mailing Addresses

The general mailing address for trademark correspondence is:

> Commissioner for Trademarks
> P.O. Box 1451
> Alexandria, VA 22313-1451

Specific addresses exist for:
- **Madrid Protocol correspondence**: Commissioner for Trademarks, P.O. Box 1451, Alexandria, VA 22313-1451 (ATTN: Madrid Processing Unit)
- **Assignment recordation**: Director of the USPTO, P.O. Box 1450, Alexandria, VA 22313-1450 (ATTN: Assignment Services Division)

### 305.02 -- Certificate of Mailing Procedure

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.197, a certificate of mailing may be used to establish timely filing based on the date of deposit with USPS, rather than the date of actual receipt at the USPTO.

#### 305.02(a) -- When Certificate of Mailing May Not Be Used

The certificate of mailing procedure may be used for all trademark filings **except**:

- An application to register a mark
- International applications under 37 C.F.R. section 7.11
- Subsequent designations under 37 C.F.R. section 7.21
- Responses to notices of irregularity under 37 C.F.R. section 7.14
- Requests to record changes of ownership under 37 C.F.R. section 7.23
- Requests to record restrictions of the holder's right of disposal under 37 C.F.R. section 7.24
- Requests for transformation under 37 C.F.R. section 7.31

#### 305.02(b) -- Mailing Requirements

The correspondence must be deposited in the United States mail, properly addressed, with sufficient postage as first class mail. The certificate of mailing procedure may not be used for sending mail to the USPTO from a foreign country.

#### 305.02(c) -- Location and Form of Certificate

The certificate of mailing must:
1. State the date of deposit in the mail (must be within the filing period)
2. Be signed by a person with a reasonable basis to expect the correspondence to be deposited on the date indicated

The certificate signature must be separate from any signature for the correspondence itself. The best location is at the beginning of the correspondence.

Each piece of correspondence must have its own certificate of mailing, even when multiple items are sent in a single envelope.

#### 305.02(e) -- Effect of Certificate of Mailing

The filing date assigned to paper correspondence is the date of actual receipt in the USPTO. The certificate of mailing date is used only to determine whether the correspondence was deposited with the USPS within the filing period.

- If correspondence is received within the filing period, the certificate is ignored
- If correspondence is received after the filing period expires, the USPTO checks for a certificate of mailing
- If a valid certificate is found with a deposit date within the filing period, the correspondence is considered timely filed

#### 305.02(f) -- Correspondence Not Received by Office

If correspondence filed with a certificate of mailing is not received by the USPTO, the USPTO will consider it timely if the party:

1. Informs the USPTO in writing within two months of becoming aware the USPTO has no evidence of receipt
2. Supplies an additional copy of the correspondence, including a copy of the signed certificate of mailing
3. Includes a verified statement attesting to the previous timely mailing

#### 305.02(h) -- Requirements Strictly Enforced

The requirements of 37 C.F.R. section 2.197 are strictly enforced. Inadvertent failure to comply is not considered an extraordinary situation warranting waiver of the rule.

## 305.03 -- Express Mail

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.198, correspondence delivered by the "Express Mail Post Office to Addressee" service of USPS will be considered filed on the date of deposit with USPS. The Express Mail procedure does **not** apply to:

- Applications for registration of marks
- Amendments to allege use under section 1(c)
- Statements of use under section 1(d)
- Requests for extension of time to file a statement of use
- Affidavits of continued use under section 8
- Renewal requests under section 9
- Requests to change or correct addresses
- Combined filings under sections 8 and 9
- Combined affidavits under sections 8 and 15
- Responses to notices of irregularity under 37 C.F.R. section 7.14
- Requests for transformation under 37 C.F.R. section 7.31
- Requests to note replacement under 37 C.F.R. section 7.28

Documents excluded from the Express Mail filing date rule receive a filing date as of the date of actual receipt in the USPTO, not the date of deposit with USPS.

For documents not excluded by 37 C.F.R. section 2.198(a), the Express Mail procedure requires:
1. The document must be sent through "Express Mail Post Office to Addressee" service
2. It must be deposited prior to the last scheduled pickup on the relevant date
3. It must be properly addressed
4. The "Express Mail" mailing label number must be placed on the document prior to mailing

### 305.04 -- Interruptions in U.S. Postal Service

Under 35 U.S.C. section 21(a) and 37 C.F.R. section 2.195(e), if the Director designates a postal service interruption or emergency, a person who was unable to deposit correspondence with USPS due to the interruption may petition the Director to consider the correspondence filed on a particular date. The petition must:

1. Be filed promptly after the ending of the designated interruption
2. Include the original correspondence or a copy
3. Include a statement that the correspondence would have been deposited on the requested filing date but for the interruption

## 306 -- Facsimile Transmission (Fax)

### 306.01 -- Documents That May Not Be Filed by Fax

All trademark documents **except** the following may be filed by fax:

1. Trademark applications
2. Drawings submitted under 37 C.F.R. sections 2.51, 2.52, 2.72, or 2.173
3. Requests for cancellation or amendment of a registration under 15 U.S.C. section 1057(e)
4. Certificates of registration
5. Correspondence to be filed with the Board, except a notice of ex parte appeal
6. Madrid-related correspondence under 37 C.F.R. sections 7.11, 7.14, 7.21, 7.23, 7.24, or 7.31
7. Documents required by statute to be certified (e.g., certified copies of court orders)

When an excluded document is received by fax, it will not be accepted.

### 306.02 -- Fax Machines Designated to Accept Trademark Documents

Designated fax machines are attended between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday, excluding Federal holidays. The USPTO Contacts List with fax numbers is available on the USPTO website.

### 306.03 -- Effect of Filing by Fax

The filing date for fax correspondence is the date the complete transmission is received by a USPTO fax machine. If the complete transmission is received on a Saturday, Sunday, or Federal holiday in DC, the filing date is the next succeeding business day.

"Complete transmission" means the entire document was received. If a 10-page fax begins on Tuesday at 11:55 p.m. and the last page arrives Wednesday at 12:05 a.m., the filing date is Wednesday.

### 306.04 -- Procedure for Filing by Fax

- Each transmission session should be limited to one application or proceeding
- Include a cover sheet with serial/registration number and sender identification
- Document must be legible, on 8.5 x 11 inch or A4 paper
- Retain the sending machine's confirmation report as evidence
- Do not mail follow-up copies of faxed documents unless requested by the USPTO

### 306.05 -- Certificate of Transmission Procedure

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.197, a certificate of transmission may be used to establish timely filing when correspondence is faxed within the response period but received by the USPTO after the period expires, or is not received or lost within the USPTO.

The certificate certifies that the transmission would be initiated before midnight, local time, on the date specified. The filer must retain a copy of the correspondence including the signed and dated certificate.

#### 306.05(a) -- Location and Form

The certificate should be clearly labeled, include a reference to the registration or serial number, the date of transmission, and the signature of the person attesting to the transmission date.

#### 306.05(c) -- Effect of Certificate of Transmission

The certificate is used only to determine whether the correspondence was transmitted within the filing period. If the complete transmission is received within the filing period, the certificate is ignored. If received after the period, the USPTO checks for a certificate.

#### 306.05(d) -- Correspondence Transmitted by Fax But Not Received

If correspondence with a certificate of transmission is not received by the USPTO, it will be considered timely based on the certificate date if the party:

1. Informs the USPTO in writing within two months of becoming aware the USPTO has no evidence of receipt
2. Supplies an additional copy of the correspondence including the signed certificate
3. Includes a statement attesting to the previous timely transmission (a copy of the sending unit's transmission confirmation may support this)

### 306.06 -- Requirements Strictly Enforced

The requirements of 37 C.F.R. section 2.197 are strictly enforced. Petitions are denied when a party fails to comply. Inadvertent failure is not considered an extraordinary situation warranting waiver.

## 307 -- Hand Delivery

Correspondence may be hand-delivered between 8:30 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time, Monday through Friday (except Federal holidays in DC) to:

> Trademark Assistance Center
> James Madison Building - East Wing
> Concourse Level
> 600 Dulany Street
> Alexandria, VA

Trademark examining attorneys will not accept documents for filing. The Customer Service Window at the Randolph Building, 401 Dulany Street, is also available (8:30 a.m. to midnight).

## 308 -- Period Ending on Saturday, Sunday, or Federal Holiday

Under 35 U.S.C. section 21(b) and 37 C.F.R. section 2.196, when the last day for taking any action or paying any fee falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or Federal holiday in DC, the action may be taken or fee paid on the next succeeding business day.

Federal holidays in DC under 5 U.S.C. section 6103:
- New Year's Day (January 1)
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day (third Monday in January)
- Washington's Birthday (third Monday in February)
- Memorial Day (last Monday in May)
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Labor Day (first Monday in September)
- Columbus Day (second Monday in October)
- Veterans Day (November 11)
- Thanksgiving Day (fourth Thursday in November)
- Christmas Day (December 25)
- Inauguration Day (January 20 of each fourth year after 1965, except when it falls on Saturday)

If a holiday falls on Saturday, the preceding Friday is a legal public holiday -- except for Inauguration Day.

## 309 -- Unscheduled Closings of the USPTO

When the USPTO is officially closed by executive order or by the Office of Personnel Management for an entire day due to an unscheduled event (e.g., adverse weather), the USPTO will consider that day a Federal holiday in DC for purposes of calculating deadlines. Actions or fees due on such a day may be taken or paid on the next succeeding business day.
