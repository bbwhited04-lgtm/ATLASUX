# TMEP Chapter 500 -- Change of Ownership

> Source: Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP), 8th Edition, October 2011
> Sections: 501--505.02

---

## 501 -- Assignment of Marks

### 501.01 -- Assignability of Marks in Applications and Registrations

Under 15 U.S.C. section 1060(a), a registered mark or a mark for which an application has been filed is assignable with the good will of the business in which the mark is used, or with that part of the good will connected with the use of and symbolized by the mark. The primary purpose is to ensure a mark is assigned along with some business or goodwill, and to prevent "trafficking" in marks.

Assignments must include the good will of the business connected with the mark. It is not necessary to include the good will symbolized by any other mark used in the business or by the name or style under which the business is conducted.

Assignments must be by instruments in writing duly executed. 15 U.S.C. section 1060(a)(3).

As a general rule, the USPTO does not investigate or evaluate the validity of assignments. The examining attorney should issue an inquiry concerning compliance with section 10 only if:

1. The application itself includes a statement indicating the assignee is not a successor to the original applicant's business; or
2. **All** of these conditions are present: (a) the assignment is executed before filing an allegation of use; (b) the applicant submits the assignment document for inclusion in the record; and (c) the assignment document fails to include the relevant language from section 10 about the entire business or portion thereof.

#### 501.01(a) -- Assignability of Intent-to-Use Applications

In an application under section 1(b), the applicant cannot assign the application before filing an allegation of use (either an amendment to allege use under section 1(c) or a statement of use under section 1(d)), **except** to a successor to the applicant's business, or portion of the business to which the mark pertains, if that business is ongoing and existing.

The assignment of an intent-to-use application to someone who is not the successor to the applicant's business before filing an allegation of use renders the application and any resulting registration void.

#### 501.01(b) -- Assignability of Extensions of Protection of International Registrations

Under section 72 of the Trademark Act, an extension of protection to the United States may be assigned, together with the goodwill associated with the mark, only to a person who is a national of, is domiciled in, or has a bona fide and effective industrial or commercial establishment in a country that is party to the Madrid Protocol (or in a country that is a member of an intergovernmental organization that is party to the Madrid Protocol).

Because the extension of protection remains part of the international registration, assignments must be recorded at the International Bureau (IB) of WIPO. The IB will notify the USPTO of any changes in ownership, and the USPTO will automatically update TRAM and TARR.

### 501.02 -- Assignments Must Be in Writing

Assignments shall be by instruments in writing duly executed. 15 U.S.C. section 1060(a)(3).

### 501.03 -- Effect of Failure to Record Assignment

Acknowledgment of execution is prima facie evidence of execution. When the assignment is recorded in the USPTO, the record is prima facie evidence of execution.

An assignment is void against any subsequent purchaser for valuable consideration without notice, unless the assignment is recorded in the USPTO within 3 months after the date of the assignment or prior to the subsequent purchase. 15 U.S.C. section 1060(a)(4).

### 501.04 -- Foreign Assignee May Designate Domestic Representative

Under 15 U.S.C. section 1060(b), an assignee not domiciled in the United States may designate by a document filed with the USPTO the name and address of a person resident in the United States on whom may be served notices or process in proceedings affecting the mark.

The designation should be in a document separate from the assignment document. One copy should be sent to the Assignment Services Branch with the document to be recorded, and a separate copy should be sent to the Trademark Operation for each registration or application to which the assignment pertains.

### 501.05 -- Assignee Stands in Place of Applicant or Registrant

Under 15 U.S.C. section 1127, the terms "applicant" and "registrant" embrace legal representatives, predecessors, successors, and assigns. The assignee of a trademark application or registration may prosecute the application, submit documents to maintain the registration, or file papers against a third party, to the exclusion of the original applicant or previous assignee.

An assignee, like an applicant, must be a natural or juristic person.

### 501.06 -- Partial Assignments

A trademark may be owned by two or more persons, and a co-owner may assign his or her interest in a mark. A sole owner may transfer a portion (e.g., 50%) of interest to another party.

A trademark owner may also assign a separate portion of a business, together with the good will and trademarks associated with that portion, while retaining rights in the mark for uses pertaining to another part of the business.

One or more trademarks of a company can be validly assigned if the assignor transfers the good will associated with only some of the goods/services on which the mark appears. After a partial assignment of a registration, both owners must file the necessary renewal applications and affidavits to maintain the registration.

A trademark owner who files a partial assignment is strongly encouraged to file a separate request to divide, so the USPTO can more effectively track ownership.

A trademark owner may **not** use an assignment to impose geographic restrictions on a registration. Geographic restrictions must be done by way of a concurrent use proceeding before the TTAB or pursuant to a final determination by a federal court.

### 501.07 -- Assignment of Extension of Protection of International Registration

Under section 72 of the Trademark Act, an extension of protection to the United States may be assigned only to a person who qualifies under the Madrid Protocol (national of, domiciled in, or with establishment in a Contracting Party country).

Because extensions of protection are part of the international registration, assignments must be recorded at the IB. The IB will notify the USPTO, which will automatically update the Trademark Database. Section 10 of the Trademark Act and 37 C.F.R. Part 3 do not apply to assignments of section 66(a) applications and registered extensions of protection. 37 C.F.R. section 7.22.

## 502 -- Establishing Ownership of Applications or Registrations

The original applicant is presumed to be the owner of a trademark application or registration, unless there is an assignment. 37 C.F.R. section 3.73(a).

### 502.01 -- Establishing the Right to Take Action

Under 37 C.F.R. section 3.73(b), to request or take action in a trademark matter, the assignee must establish ownership by submitting to the USPTO a signed statement identifying the assignee, accompanied by either:

1. Documentary evidence of a chain of title from the original owner to the assignee (e.g., copy of executed assignment), with the documents being required to be recorded pursuant to section 3.11 in the assignment records; or
2. A statement specifying where documentary evidence of chain of title is recorded in the assignment records (e.g., reel and frame number).

The submission must show that the person signing is authorized to act on behalf of the assignee.

**Applications Under Sections 1 and 44**: An assignee is not required to record the assignment to take action on an application or registration. However, the assignee must record the assignment with the Assignment Services Branch to obtain a certificate of registration in the name of the assignee.

**Section 66(a) Applications and Registered Extensions of Protection**: The new owner must record the assignment with the IB to take action on an application or registration. The new owner does not have the option of submitting documentary evidence of ownership through the USPTO.

### 502.02 -- Pending Applications - Issuance of Registration Certificate in Name of Assignee

#### 502.02(a) -- Applications Under Sections 1 and 44

Under 37 C.F.R. section 3.85, the certificate of registration may be issued to the assignee of the applicant, or in a new name of the applicant, provided the party files a written request in the trademark application by the time the application is being prepared for issuance. The assignment or name change document must be recorded in the Office.

**Clear Chain of Title Required**: A new owner's request that a registration issue in a new name will not be granted unless documents recorded in the Assignment Services Branch show a clear chain of title from the original applicant to the party requesting the change.

The new owner bears the burden of recording **and** notifying appropriate USPTO personnel. However, for documents recorded on or after November 2, 2003, recording with the Assignment Services Branch will automatically update ownership in TRAM and TARR for assignments of entire interest and goodwill, nunc pro tunc assignments, mergers, and name changes.

**Time for Recordation**: To ensure the registration issues in the new name, the new owner should record the assignment before the mark is approved for publication or registration on the Supplemental Register, or prior to acceptance of a statement of use in an ITU application.

#### 502.02(b) -- Applications Under Section 66(a)

In section 66(a) applications, a new owner must record any assignment, change of name, or other document affecting title with the IB. The IB will notify the USPTO when the change is recorded in the International Register, and the USPTO will automatically update the Trademark Database.

#### 502.02(c) -- Examining Attorney's Action Regarding Assignment

If the examining attorney learns that an assignment has been recorded before approval for publication, the examining attorney should check the Assignment Database to ensure a clear chain of title, and should ensure TRAM is updated before approving the mark.

The examining attorney should generally only issue an Office action questioning an assignment if an entity attempts to take action with respect to the application and USPTO records show ownership in another party.

During initial examination, the examining attorney should not suspend action to await recordation of a document. However, if the applicant submits a request indicating the document has been submitted for recordation and the application is in condition to be approved for publication or registration, the examining attorney should suspend the application until the document has been recorded.

### 502.03 -- Issuance of New Certificate to Assignee of Registrant

**Registrations Under Sections 1 and 44**: The USPTO will issue a new certificate of registration in the name of the new owner for the unexpired part of the registration period, if the new owner: (1) records the appropriate document with the Assignment Services Branch; (2) files a written request that a certificate be issued in the new owner's name; and (3) pays the required fee.

The recorded documents must show a clear chain of title from the original registrant to the party requesting issuance of a new certificate.

**Registered Extensions of Protection**: The new owner must record the assignment with the IB. The IB will notify the USPTO, which will update the Assignment Database. The USPTO will issue a new certificate if the new owner: (1) records the appropriate document with the IB; (2) files a written request with the Assignment Services Branch; and (3) pays the required fee.

## 503 -- Recording With Assignment Services Branch

### 503.01 -- Effect of Recording a Document

Under 37 C.F.R. section 3.54, the recording of a document is not a determination by the Office of the validity of the document or the effect that document has on the title to an application or registration.

#### 503.01(a) -- Recording May Not Update Ownership Information

Recording a document with the Assignment Services Branch does not necessarily change or update the ownership of record shown in the Trademark Database (TRAM and TARR). Automatic updating occurs only for certain document types recorded on or after November 2, 2003. In all other cases, the new owner must separately notify the Trademark Operation in writing and request manual update. See TMEP section 505.

#### 503.01(b) -- Recording Does Not Update Correspondence Address

Recording an assignment document that includes a new correspondence address will **not** update the correspondence address in the Trademark Database. A separate written request to change the correspondence address must be made.

#### 503.01(c) -- Recording is Not a Determination of Validity

The Assignment Services Branch does not examine the substance of documents submitted for recording. Recording is a ministerial act, not a determination of the document's validity or its effect on title. The USPTO determines the effect of a document only when an assignee attempts to take action in connection with an application or registration.

#### 503.01(d) -- Recording Does Not Constitute Response to Office Action

Recording a document with the Assignment Services Branch does **not** constitute a response to an outstanding Office action.

#### 503.01(e) -- Conditional Assignment

If an assignment is conditional on a given act or event at the time of execution, the USPTO will view the submission of the assignment for recordation as an indication that the act or event has occurred.

### 503.02 -- Documents That the Office Will Record

The USPTO records:
- Assignments of trademark applications and registrations, accompanied by completed cover sheets
- Documents that affect title to a trademark application or registration, such as certificates showing a change of name of a business or a merger of businesses (a mere name change is a proper link in the chain of title)
- License agreements, security agreements, and agreements between parties limiting future use of a mark (to give third parties notice of equitable interests)
- Partial assignments of an interest in a mark

For section 66(a) applications and registered extensions of protection, the new owner must record changes with the IB, not the Assignment Services Branch.

### 503.03 -- Requirements for Recording

#### 503.03(a) -- Formal Requirements

All documents submitted for recording must be accompanied by a cover sheet that meets the requirements of 37 C.F.R. section 3.31. Documents filed electronically via ETAS (http://etas.uspto.gov) are recorded much faster than paper documents.

#### 503.03(b) -- Supporting Documents

To record an assignment or other title-affecting document, a legible cover sheet and one of the following must be submitted:

1. A copy of the document
2. A copy of an extract from the document evidencing the effect on title
3. A statement signed by both the party conveying the interest and the party receiving the interest explaining how the conveyance affects title

To record a name change, only a legible cover sheet is required. 37 C.F.R. section 3.25(b).

Paper documents must be on white, non-shiny paper, 8.5 x 11 inches or A4, with one-inch margins. Original documents should not be submitted because the USPTO does not return recorded documents.

#### 503.03(c) -- English Language Requirement

The USPTO will not record a document that is not in English, unless accompanied by an English translation signed by the translator.

#### 503.03(d) -- Fee for Recording

A fee is required for each application and registration against which a document is recorded, as identified in the cover sheet. The fee does not depend on the length of the document. If the materials do not meet recording requirements, the fee is not refunded.

#### 503.03(e) -- Cover Sheet

Each document submitted for recording must be accompanied by a cover sheet meeting 37 C.F.R. section 3.31. Required cover sheet contents:

- Name of the party conveying the interest
- Name and address of the party receiving the interest
- Entity type and citizenship of the receiving party
- Description of the interest conveyed (assignment, license, merger, etc.)
- Each application serial number or registration number
- Name and address for correspondence regarding the recording
- Date the document was executed
- Signature of the party submitting the document
- Number of applications/registrations identified
- Total fee
- Identification or description of the mark

Documents not accompanied by a completed cover sheet will not be recorded.

### 503.04 -- Address for Submitting Documents

Electronic filing through ETAS at http://etas.uspto.gov is encouraged. Paper documents should be sent to: Mail Stop Assignment Recordation Services, Director of the USPTO, P.O. Box 1450, Alexandria, VA 22313-1450.

### 503.05 -- Recording Procedure and Recordation Date

The date of recordation is the date the USPTO receives a cover sheet meeting 37 C.F.R. section 3.31 and a document affecting title meeting 37 C.F.R. section 3.25. The USPTO merely examines whether the requirements for recording have been met.

If materials do not meet recording requirements, the USPTO returns them to the sender with a letter stamped with the original date of receipt, indicating the documents can be corrected and resubmitted within a specified time to retain the original recordation date.

### 503.06 -- Correction of Errors in Cover Sheet or Recorded Document

#### 503.06(a) -- Typographical Errors in Cover Sheet

To correct a typographical error in a cover sheet, submit: (1) a copy of the originally recorded document; (2) a corrected cover sheet; and (3) the required fee for each application/registration. If the error does not affect title to the application/registration, the original date of recordation is kept. If it does affect title, the recordation date changes to the date the corrected cover sheet was received.

#### 503.06(b) -- Typographical Errors in Recorded Assignment Document

If an error is in the recorded document itself, the responsible party must draft a new document and record it, or make corrections to the original document and re-record it. If the assignor is unavailable, the assignee may submit an affidavit identifying the error and requesting correction.

#### 503.06(c) -- Assignment Improperly Filed Against Owner's Application/Registration

When another party has improperly recorded an assignment against the owner's application/registration, the owner must correct the error by filing a corrected cover sheet with the Assignment Services Branch, including: (1) a completed cover sheet; (2) an affidavit or declaration identifying itself as the correct owner; and (3) the recording fee.

#### 503.06(d) -- Owner Must Notify Trademark Operation

Recording a corrective document generally does not automatically update the Trademark Database. The owner must separately notify the Trademark Operation in writing.

#### 503.06(e) -- Recorded Documents Not Removed From Assignment Records

Once recorded, the Assignment Services Branch will not remove a document from the records, even if the assignment or document is subsequently found to be invalid. The goal is to maintain a complete history of claimed interests in a mark.

#### 503.06(f) -- Petitions to Correct or "Expunge" Assignment Records

Petitions to correct, modify, or "expunge" assignment records are rarely granted. Such petitions are granted only if: (1) the normal corrective procedures will not provide adequate relief; and (2) the integrity of the assignment records will not be affected. Even if an "expungement" petition is granted, the document images remain in the records -- the USPTO merely deletes the links to the application/registration.

### 503.07 -- "Indexing" Against Recorded Document Not Permitted

The USPTO does not process requests for "indexing" or "cross-referencing" additional serial numbers against a previously recorded document. A party wishing to record a document against additional applications must submit a new copy of the document, a new cover sheet, and the recording fee.

### 503.08 -- Accessibility of Assignment Records

The Assignment Services Branch maintains separate records for patents and trademarks. The public can search trademark assignment records on the USPTO website at http://assignments.uspto.gov/assignments. Assignments are open to public inspection upon recordation.

#### 503.08(a) -- Abstracts of Title

Members of the public may obtain abstracts of title to particular registrations or applications from the Document Services Branch upon payment of the required fee.

## 504 -- Automatic Updating of Ownership in Trademark Database

Effective November 2, 2003, the USPTO will automatically update ownership information in the Trademark Database when one of the following documents is recorded with the Assignment Services Branch:

- Assignment of entire interest and goodwill
- Nunc Pro Tunc Assignment of entire interest and goodwill
- Merger
- Name Change

In these situations, it is unnecessary for the new owner to separately notify the Trademark Operation or to file a request for the certificate to issue in the new name.

The Trademark Database shows only the last recorded owner. The complete chain of title can be obtained from the Assignment Database at http://assignments.uspto.gov/assignments.

### 504.01 -- Circumstances Where Automatic Updating Will Not Occur

Automatic updating will **not** occur in these circumstances:

1. **Execution date conflicts**: If a previously recorded document has an execution date the same as or later than the subsequently recorded document
2. **Blackout periods**: Between approval for publication and issuance of registration (for section 1(a) and 44 applications); between approval for publication and issuance of notice of allowance, and between registration approval and issuance of registration (for section 1(b) applications)
3. **Maximum ownership changes reached**: Up to nine changes prior to publication; up to nine additional changes between publication and registration

In all these cases, new owners must file a written request with the Trademark Operation to update the Trademark Database.

### 504.02 -- Processing Time for Automatic Updating

The Assignment Services Branch will extract trademark assignment information from the Assignment Database and transmit it to the Trademark Operation once a week. If the owner needs the Trademark Database updated faster, the owner should notify the Trademark Operation in writing of the change of ownership.

### 504.03 -- Correction to Automatic Update - Last Recorded Owner Does Not Have Clear Chain of Title

The Trademark Database will be automatically updated to show the last recorded owner regardless of whether the Assignment Database shows a clear chain of title. If the true owner is different from the last recorded owner, the true owner may file a written request for correction. The USPTO will grant the request if: (1) the Trademark Database was updated to show a party without a clear chain of title; and (2) at the time of the automatic update, the Assignment Database showed a clear chain of title in the party requesting correction.

### 504.04 -- Automatic Updating Does Not Apply to Requests Filed Before November 2, 2003

The automatic updating procedures apply to all documents recorded on or after November 2, 2003, even if the request for recordation was filed before that date. They do not apply to documents recorded prior to November 2, 2003.

### 504.05 -- Automatic Updating Does Not Apply to Section 66(a) Applications

The automatic updating procedures do not apply to section 66(a) applications and registered extensions of protection. Changes of ownership must be recorded with the IB. The Trademark Database will automatically reflect changes recorded in the International Register.

## 505 -- Notifying Trademark Operation of Recordation and Request to Update Ownership

When automatic updating does not apply, the new owner must separately notify the Trademark Operation in writing that ownership has changed, and request that the Trademark Database be updated.

### 505.01 -- Request to Update Ownership in Pending Application

Prior to registration, a request to update ownership should be made in writing and directed to the examining attorney. If filed after publication, it will be handled in accordance with standard procedures for processing amendments after publication.

### 505.02 -- Request to Update Ownership After Registration

For registrations, the Post Registration Section will not update the Trademark Database with new owner information unless either: (1) the owner files a written request to amend the registration under section 7(d) of the Trademark Act; or (2) the owner takes an action, such as filing a section 8 affidavit or section 9 renewal application.

If the owner is not taking such an action, the owner must:

1. Record the appropriate document (e.g., assignment, change of name) with the Assignment Services Branch
2. File a written request under section 7(d) to update the Trademark Database, signed by the new owner or a qualified practitioner
3. Pay the required fee

The owner must file a section 7 request even if it does not want a new registration certificate but only wishes the Trademark Database to be updated. New owners are encouraged to use TEAS to file the "Section 7 Request for Amendment or Correction of Registration Certificate" form.
