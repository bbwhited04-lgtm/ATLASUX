# TMEP Chapter 400 -- Processing Documents and Fees

> Source: Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP), 8th Edition, October 2011
> Sections: 401--405.06

---

## 401 -- Processing New Applications

Upon receipt in the USPTO, trademark applications filed under section 1 or section 44 of the Trademark Act are given a date of receipt and reviewed for compliance with the minimum filing date requirements (see TMEP section 202). If an application meets the minimum requirements of 37 C.F.R. section 2.21, it is given a filing date and serial number.

### 401.01 -- Filing Receipts

**TEAS Applications**: When filed electronically, the USPTO generally receives the application within seconds and TEAS displays a "Success" page confirming receipt. This page is evidence of filing and should be saved. TEAS also sends an e-mail acknowledgement with a summary of filed information. No paper filing receipt is sent.

The applicant should immediately review the summary for accuracy and notify the USPTO by e-mail to TEAS@uspto.gov of any inconsistencies.

**Section 66(a) Applications**: For requests for extensions of protection of international registrations to the United States, the USPTO sends an acknowledgment of receipt to the International Bureau (IB), and the IB sends the acknowledgment to the applicant.

**Paper Applications Under Section 1 or Section 44**: After a paper application is given a filing date, TRAM generates a filing receipt identifying: (1) the mark; (2) the serial number, filing date, and register; (3) the type of mark; (4) statutory basis for filing; (5) identification of goods/services and international classes; (6) applicant information; and (7) the correspondence address.

The applicant should review the filing receipt for accuracy and notify the USPTO in writing of any discrepancy. Requests for correction of a filing receipt should be directed to the Commissioner for Trademarks or sent to TMfilingreceipt@uspto.gov.

An applicant should request correction of a filing receipt **only** if the information on the receipt is inconsistent with the application as filed. If the applicant made an error in the application itself, the applicant should file an amendment rather than a request for correction.

### 401.02 -- Application Serial Numbers

Each application is assigned an eight-digit serial number comprising a two-digit series code and six additional numbers assigned by order of filing. Applicants must include the entire eight-digit serial number on all communications.

Key series codes:
- **70** -- applications filed prior to the 1905-1920 Acts
- **71** -- applications filed under the 1905-1920 Acts (through July 4, 1947)
- **72** -- began January 3, 1956
- **73** -- began September 4, 1973 (adoption of international classification)
- **74** -- began November 16, 1989 (Trademark Law Revision Act of 1988)
- **75** -- began October 1, 1995
- **76** -- began March 20, 2000
- **77** -- began September 14, 2006 (TEAS applications filed on or after that date)
- **78** -- TEAS applications filed prior to September 14, 2006
- **79** -- requests for extension of protection under section 66(a) (began November 2, 2003)
- **85** -- began April 1, 2010 (all TEAS applications on or after that date)
- Numbers beginning with **975,000** -- applications created through dividing

### 401.03 -- Data Entry

For TEAS applications, the applicant's data is loaded directly into TRAM. For paper applications, documents are scanned into TICRS and data is manually entered into TRAM.

TRAM may be used by USPTO employees to obtain information about location, status, prosecution history, ownership, and correspondence address. This information is available to the public through TARR at http://tarr.uspto.gov.

### 401.04 -- Creation of Application Records

For TEAS applications, the USPTO maintains an electronic record containing all incoming and outgoing documents. For paper applications, the USPTO scans all incoming documents and creates an electronic record, plus a paper file jacket. The examining attorney works from the electronic record, and outgoing communications are put into the electronic record only.

All applications are electronically routed to the law offices.

## 402 -- Electronic Application and Registration Records

The USPTO maintains TICRS, which includes images of the contents of trademark application and registration records. TICRS is available in the Public Search Facility, free of charge.

The public may also view and print records through the Trademark Document Retrieval (TDR) portal on the USPTO website. Electronic images of TTAB proceeding records are available via TTABVUE. TDR and TTABVUE are available 24/7, free of charge.

### 402.01 -- USPTO No Longer Creates Paper Copies

Effective April 12, 2004, the USPTO no longer creates paper file copies of certain trademark-related documents submitted electronically. Furthermore, the USPTO does not generate paper copies of certain trademark documents it creates, except for copies sent to recipients by mail.

All these documents can be viewed through TICRS and TDR. The USPTO still provides certified paper copies of all trademark documents upon request and payment of the appropriate fee.

## 403 -- Correspondence Returned as Undeliverable

If a paper Office action or notice is returned to the USPTO because USPS could not deliver it, the time for response will **not** be extended unless the USPTO sent it to the wrong address due to a USPTO error.

**USPTO Error**: The USPTO will reissue the Office action with a new response period if it sent the action to the wrong address because the USPTO either entered the correspondence address incorrectly or failed to properly enter a notice of change of address filed **before** the date on which the action was issued.

**Not a USPTO Error**: Transmitting a response on letterhead bearing a new address is not a proper notice of change of address. The applicant must specifically request a change of correspondence address.

If the Office action was sent to the correct correspondence address but was returned as undeliverable, the USPTO will try to obtain the correct address and forward the action, but the response time will **not** be extended.

If outgoing e-mail is returned as undeliverable, the USPTO will send a paper copy to the correspondence address of record.

## 404 -- Documents Not Returnable

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.25, documents filed in the USPTO by the applicant or registrant become part of the official record and will not be returned or removed. See TMEP sections 1702, 1705, and 1708 regarding petitions to the Director to waive this rule.

Documents ordered to be filed under seal pursuant to a protective order issued by a court or by the TTAB, pursuant to 37 C.F.R. section 2.27(e), are not made part of the official record.

## 405 -- Fees

### Payment Methods (37 C.F.R. section 2.207)

All payments of money required in trademark cases must be made in U.S. dollars and in the form of:
- Cashier's or certified check
- Treasury note
- National bank note
- United States Postal Service money order
- Credit card (except for replenishing a deposit account)
- Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

If sent in any other form, the Office may delay or cancel the credit until collection is made. Checks and money orders must be made payable to the Director of the United States Patent and Trademark Office. Payments from foreign countries must be payable and immediately negotiable in the United States for the full amount.

### 405.01 -- Credit Cards

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.207, the USPTO accepts payment by credit card, subject to actual collection.

Requirements for credit card authorizations:
1. A valid credit card number
2. A valid expiration date
3. The name of the cardholder with signature and date
4. A billing address including zip code
5. A description and purpose of the payment
6. A specific payment amount

The USPTO accepts American Express, Discover, MasterCard, and Visa. The USPTO will only accept a specific dollar amount -- no general authorizations. Refunds of fees paid by credit card will be credited to the same credit card account.

**Important**: If a party provides credit card information in the body of an application, cover letter, response, or other correspondence relating to a trademark application or registration (rather than using the USPTO's Credit Card Payment Form), the credit card information becomes part of the public record. The USPTO does not put the Credit Card Payment Form in application or registration records.

### 405.02 -- Checks

All payments must be in U.S. dollars via cashier's or certified check, Treasury note, national bank note, or USPS money order. Checks must be made payable to the Director of the USPTO.

#### 405.02(a) -- Personal Checks

The USPTO will accept an uncertified personal check as "conditional" payment. If the check clears, the fee is considered paid as of the date the check was received. If the check is returned unpaid, the fee remains unpaid and the accompanying document is processed as though the fee had been omitted. There is a processing fee for any returned check.

### 405.03 -- Deposit Accounts

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.208, deposit accounts may be established at the USPTO for the convenience of paying fees. Requirements:

- Minimum deposit of $1,000
- Monthly statements issued; remittance must be made promptly to restore the balance
- Sufficient funds must always be on deposit to cover all outstanding authorizations
- Charges to accounts with insufficient funds will not be accepted
- A service charge is assessed for each month the balance falls below $1,000

A general authorization to charge all fees (or only certain fees) to a deposit account may be filed for an individual application, either for the entire pendency or for a particular document.

**Critical**: An authorization to charge a fee to a deposit account is not considered payment of the fee on the date of authorization unless sufficient funds are present in the account to cover the fee. If a deposit account has insufficient funds to charge the initial filing fee, the USPTO will not grant a filing date.

A party cannot charge a fee to a deposit account without prior authorization. The Office of Finance maintains a list of authorized persons.

### 405.04 -- Refunds

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.209, the Director may refund any fee paid by mistake or in excess of that required. Key refund rules:

- A change of purpose (e.g., withdrawing an application) does **not** entitle a party to a refund
- The USPTO will **not** refund amounts of $25 or less unless specifically requested
- Refund requests must be filed within **two years** from the date the fee was paid (non-extendable)
- Credit card refunds go to the same credit card account
- The USPTO **will** refund the filing fee for an application denied a filing date, or a fee that is untimely
- The USPTO **will not** refund a filing fee after the application has been processed, even if registration is refused
- The USPTO **will not** refund a fee when a timely filed document (statement of use, section 8 affidavit) is rejected for failure to meet requirements
- If an examiner erroneously requires a fee, the USPTO will refund the fee submitted in response

Refund requests should be sent to: Mail Stop 16, Director of the USPTO, P.O. Box 1450, Alexandria, VA 22313-1450.

### 405.05 -- Electronic Funds Transfer

The EFT payment method allows customers to send a payment over the Internet through the Automated Clearing House and Federal Reserve system. The customer must establish a User ID and Password. See http://www.uspto.gov/teas/payment.htm.

### 405.06 -- Payments Refused or Charged Back By Financial Institutions

If a check is returned unpaid, or an EFT or credit card is refused or charged back by a financial institution, the document that accompanied the payment is processed as though the fee had been omitted.

If the document included an authorization to charge a deposit account with sufficient funds, the USPTO charges the fee plus a processing fee to the deposit account. If there is no deposit account authorization, the USPTO notifies the party of the fee deficiency.

**Processing fee**: Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.6(b)(12), there is a fee for processing any payment that is refused or charged back. This fee is strictly enforced. The USPTO will not approve a pending application for publication or registration, or take any other requested action, until all outstanding fees including the processing fee have been paid.

## 310 -- Computing Period for Response to Office Action or Notice

The deadline for responding to an Office action, notice of allowance, or other notice issued by the USPTO is computed from the date of issuance of the action or notice to the date the response is received in the USPTO.

For example, a response to an Office action dated August 31 is due on the following February 28 (or 29 in a leap year); a response to an Office action dated February 28 is due on August 28.

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.197, correspondence is considered timely filed even if received after the expiration of the filing period, if it was deposited with USPS as first class mail or transmitted by fax before the expiration of the filing period and accompanied by a certificate attesting to the date of mailing or transmission.

If a document or fee is due on a Saturday, Sunday, or Federal holiday in DC, it is considered timely if received on the following business day.
