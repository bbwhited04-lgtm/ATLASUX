# TMEP Chapter 200 -- Application Filing Date

> Source: Trademark Manual of Examining Procedure (TMEP), 8th Edition, October 2011
> Sections: 201--206.04

---

## 201 -- Filing Date

The filing date of a trademark application is the date the USPTO receives the application. The filing date is important because it determines the applicant's constructive use priority date under 15 U.S.C. section 7(c) and is used to calculate statutory deadlines.

An application filed through TEAS receives a filing date as of the date and time the electronic transmission is received by the USPTO server. An application filed on paper receives a filing date as of the date it is received at the USPTO.

---

## 202 -- Constructive Use Priority

### 202.01 -- General Principles

Under 15 U.S.C. section 1057(c), the filing of an application for registration on the Principal Register constitutes constructive use of the mark, contingent on registration. This gives the applicant nationwide priority as of the filing date, subject to the rights of:

- A person who has used the mark prior to the filing date
- A person who has filed an application that is pending or has resulted in registration, with an earlier filing date
- A person entitled to priority under 15 U.S.C. section 1126 (foreign applicants with treaty priority)

Constructive use priority applies only to applications on the Principal Register, not the Supplemental Register.

### 202.02 -- Limitations on Constructive Use

Constructive use does not apply retroactively to acts occurring before the filing date. It is contingent on the mark actually being registered -- if the application is abandoned or refused, the constructive use benefit is lost.

A party who has made actual use of a mark before the applicant's filing date has superior rights in the geographic area of actual use and natural expansion, even against the registrant's nationwide constructive use rights.

## 203 -- Minimum Filing Date Requirements

### 203.01 -- Elements Required

To receive a filing date, an application must include the following minimum elements:

1. **The name of the applicant** -- a person or entity that has a bona fide intent to use the mark, or is actually using the mark, in commerce
2. **A name and address for correspondence**
3. **A clear drawing of the mark** (or a typed representation for a standard character mark)
4. **A listing of the goods or services** -- need not be perfect but must be sufficient to indicate the general nature of the goods/services
5. **The filing fee** for at least one class of goods or services (the filing fee must be received or authorized)

For applications under section 1(a) (use-based), the following are also required for a filing date:
- A verified statement of use (declaration)
- A specimen showing use of the mark in commerce
- The date of first use anywhere and the date of first use in commerce

For applications under section 1(b) (intent-to-use), a verified statement of bona fide intent to use the mark in commerce is required.

### 203.02 -- Filing Date Denied

If an application lacks any of the minimum filing date requirements, the USPTO will not assign a filing date. The applicant will be notified and given an opportunity to submit the missing element(s). The filing date will be the date the USPTO receives the missing element(s), not the original submission date.

This can be critical because a later filing date means a later constructive use priority date.

## 204 -- Defective Applications

### 204.01 -- Applications That Do Not Meet Minimum Requirements

An application that does not include all minimum filing date requirements is defective. The Pre-Examination Unit will send a notice informing the applicant of the deficiency and allowing time to respond.

If the applicant corrects the deficiency within the allowed time, the filing date will be the date the correction is received by the USPTO. If the applicant does not respond, the application will be declared abandoned and the filing fee will not be refunded.

### 204.02 -- Correcting Filing Date Deficiencies

Common deficiencies include:

- **Missing drawing**: The applicant must submit a clear drawing of the mark
- **Missing goods/services identification**: The applicant must provide at least a general listing
- **Missing applicant name**: Must be corrected with the actual applicant's name
- **Missing filing fee**: The fee must be received
- **Missing verification/declaration**: The applicant must submit a signed declaration

The filing date is not correctable retroactively -- the filing date will always be the date on which all minimum requirements are met.

### 204.03 -- Incomplete Fee Payments

If the filing fee is insufficient (e.g., the applicant pays for one class but lists goods in multiple classes), the application may receive a filing date for the class(es) covered by the fee. The examining attorney will require either additional fees for the remaining classes or deletion of the goods/services in the unfunded classes.

## 205 -- Effective Filing Date

### 205.01 -- General Rule

The effective filing date of an application is the date on which the USPTO receives all minimum filing date requirements. For TEAS applications, this is the date and time of the electronic submission (Eastern Time). For paper applications, this is the date the application is received at the USPTO mail facility.

### 205.02 -- Express Mail Filing Date

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.195, correspondence sent by "Express Mail Post Office to Addressee" service of the United States Postal Service (USPS) will be considered received by the USPTO on the date of deposit with the USPS, if the "date-in" is shown on the Express Mail mailing label. The person filing the correspondence must:

1. Place "Express Mail" mailing label number on the correspondence
2. Address the correspondence to the Director of the USPTO
3. Maintain a copy of the Express Mail receipt

This provision does not apply to private courier services (e.g., FedEx, UPS).

### 205.03 -- Certificate of Mailing

Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.197, a certificate of mailing may be used to obtain a filing date based on the date of deposit with USPS for most trademark correspondence. This is not available for:

- New applications (new applications must use Express Mail or TEAS for a filing date earlier than actual receipt)
- Documents filed with the TTAB that have specific due dates

## 206 -- Priority Dates and Foreign Applications

### 206.01 -- Paris Convention Priority

Under 15 U.S.C. section 1126(d) and the Paris Convention, an applicant who has duly filed a trademark application in a foreign country that is a member of the Paris Convention (or WTO) may claim the filing date of the foreign application as a priority date, provided the U.S. application is filed within six months of the foreign filing date.

To claim priority, the applicant must:

1. File the U.S. application within six months of the earliest foreign filing date
2. Include a claim of priority in the U.S. application
3. Submit a certified copy of the foreign application (a true copy, certification, or translation as needed)

The priority date gives the applicant the benefit of the foreign filing date for purposes of determining priority against other marks in the United States.

### 206.02 -- Requirements for Priority Claim

A valid priority claim requires:

- The foreign application must have been filed in a Paris Convention or WTO member country
- The U.S. application must be filed within six months of the earliest foreign filing date
- The mark in the U.S. application must be the same mark as in the foreign application
- The goods/services must be the same as or within the scope of the foreign application

If the priority claim is defective, the examining attorney will require correction or withdrawal of the claim.

### 206.03 -- Certified Copy of Foreign Application

The applicant must submit a certified copy of the foreign application. Under 37 C.F.R. section 2.34(a)(7), the certified copy must be filed within the time period set by the examining attorney, typically before the application is approved for publication.

If the foreign application is not in English, a translation must be provided along with a statement that the translation is accurate.

### 206.04 -- Effect of Priority Claim

A valid priority claim gives the applicant the benefit of the foreign filing date for purposes of determining priority of rights in the United States. This means:

- The applicant's constructive use date is the foreign filing date, not the U.S. filing date
- Any conflicting U.S. applications filed between the foreign and U.S. filing dates will have a later priority date
- The priority claim does not extend the period for filing a statement of use or any other deadline based on the U.S. filing date

The priority claim does not excuse the applicant from meeting all U.S. registration requirements, including demonstrating use in commerce (for use-based applications) or filing a statement of use (for intent-to-use applications).
