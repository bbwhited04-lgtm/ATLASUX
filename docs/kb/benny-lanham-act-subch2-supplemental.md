# Lanham Act -- Subchapter II: Supplemental Register

## Overview

Subchapter II establishes the Supplemental Register, which provides a secondary form of federal trademark registration for marks that do not qualify for the Principal Register -- typically marks that are capable of distinguishing goods/services but have not yet acquired distinctiveness (secondary meaning). The Supplemental Register offers fewer benefits than the Principal Register but still provides important protections.

---

## Section 1091 (section 23) -- Marks Registrable on the Supplemental Register

### (a) Eligible Marks
- Any mark capable of distinguishing the applicant's goods or services that does NOT qualify for the Principal Register
- Includes marks that are:
  - Merely descriptive
  - Primarily geographically descriptive
  - Primarily merely a surname
  - Any other mark capable of distinguishing goods/services not registrable on Principal Register
- Mark must be **in use in commerce** -- no intent-to-use applications on the Supplemental Register

### (b) Ineligible Marks
- Marks that are **NOT capable of distinguishing** goods/services cannot be registered on either register
- Generic terms are NOT registrable on the Supplemental Register

### (c) Collective and Certification Marks
- Collective marks and certification marks may also be registered on the Supplemental Register

---

## Section 1092 (section 24) -- Publication; Cancellation Proceedings

### No Opposition Period
- Marks registered on the Supplemental Register are **NOT published for opposition**
- Any person who believes they are damaged by a Supplemental Register registration may petition to cancel at **any time**

### Cancellation
- Petition for cancellation may be filed at any time (no 5-year time limit)
- TTAB has jurisdiction over cancellation proceedings
- Same procedural rules apply as for Principal Register cancellations

---

## Section 1093 (section 25) -- Registration; Certificate

- Certificate of registration issued for marks on the Supplemental Register
- Certificate includes: Filing date, mark, goods/services, registration date, registration number

---

## Section 1094 (section 26) -- Provisions Applicable to Supplemental Register

### Provisions That DO Apply
- Assignment (section 10)
- Cancellation procedures (section 14, without the 5-year limitation)
- TTAB proceedings (sections 17-21)
- Notices of registration
- Term and renewal (section 9) -- same 10-year terms and renewal requirements
- Section 8 affidavit requirements (continued use or excusable nonuse)
- General provisions on correspondence, fees, etc.

### Provisions That Do NOT Apply
- **Section 7(b)**: No constructive notice of ownership
- **Section 7(c)**: No constructive use (no nationwide priority from filing date)
- **Section 12(a)**: No publication for opposition
- **Section 13**: No opposition proceedings
- **Section 15**: No incontestability available
- **Section 22**: No constructive notice

---

## Section 1095 (section 27) -- Registration as Evidence

### Limited Evidentiary Value
- Registration on the Supplemental Register is **NOT prima facie evidence** of:
  - Validity of the mark
  - Registrant's ownership
  - Registrant's exclusive right to use

### What Registration Does Provide
- Evidence of the date of first use
- Federal court jurisdiction under section 39 (28 U.S.C. 1121)
- Right to use the federal registration symbol (R-in-circle)
- Basis for filing in foreign countries under international conventions
- Protection against registration of confusingly similar marks (cited as bar during examination of later applications)

---

## Section 1096 (section 28) -- Effect on Previously Registered Marks

- Registration on the Supplemental Register does not affect marks previously registered on the Principal Register
- Does not prevent or impair the right of any person to register a mark on the Principal Register

## Supplemental Register vs. Principal Register -- Comparison

| Feature | Principal Register | Supplemental Register |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Constructive notice of ownership | Yes | No |
| Constructive use (nationwide priority) | Yes | No |
| Prima facie evidence of validity | Yes | No |
| Incontestability available | Yes (after 5 years) | No |
| Published for opposition | Yes (30-day period) | No |
| Cancellation time limit | 5 years (most grounds) | No time limit |
| Intent-to-use applications | Yes | No (must be in use) |
| Federal registration symbol | Yes | Yes |
| Recorded with U.S. Customs | Yes | No |
| Basis for foreign filing | Yes | Yes |
| Federal court jurisdiction | Yes | Yes |
| Bars later confusingly similar marks | Yes | Yes (cited during examination) |
| Section 8 maintenance required | Yes | Yes |
| Section 9 renewal required | Yes | Yes |

## Strategic Considerations

### When to File on the Supplemental Register
1. **Descriptive mark not yet distinctive**: When a mark is merely descriptive but the applicant needs some federal protection while building acquired distinctiveness
2. **Foreign filing basis**: Many countries require a home registration to file abroad; a Supplemental Register registration satisfies this requirement
3. **Deterrent effect**: Registration appears in trademark searches and may deter potential adopters of similar marks
4. **Path to Principal Register**: After 5 years of substantially exclusive and continuous use, the owner may file a new application for the Principal Register claiming acquired distinctiveness under section 2(f)

### Limitations to Be Aware Of
1. **No presumption of validity**: In litigation, the burden of proving trademark rights remains on the registrant
2. **No constructive notice**: Does not cut off good-faith defenses by subsequent users
3. **No incontestability**: Cannot achieve conclusive evidence status
4. **No Customs recordation**: Cannot block infringing imports at the border through Customs
5. **Cancellable at any time**: Vulnerable to cancellation petitions indefinitely
