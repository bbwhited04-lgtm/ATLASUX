  CONTENT CREATION (10 Prompts)
============================================================

1. Write a 300-word blog post about [TOPIC] that hooks the reader in the first sentence, includes 3 actionable takeaways, and ends with a call to action. Use a conversational tone.

2. Create 10 engaging social media captions for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Each caption should be under 150 characters, include an emoji, and have a clear call to action. Vary the tone between playful, urgent, and inspirational.

3. Write a YouTube video script (5 minutes) about [TOPIC]. Structure it with a pattern-interrupt hook (first 5 seconds), the main value section, and a strong outro with subscribe CTA. Include suggested B-roll notes.

4. Generate 20 headline variations for an article about [TOPIC]. Mix curiosity-driven, how-to, listicle, and contrarian angles. Rank them by predicted click-through rate.

5. Write a compelling product description for [PRODUCT] that addresses the customer's pain point, explains the solution, lists 5 key benefits (not features), and includes a money-back guarantee mention. Keep it under 200 words.

6. Create a 30-day content calendar for [NICHE] on Instagram. For each day, provide the content type (Reel, Carousel, Story, Static), a topic, a caption hook, and 5 relevant hashtags.

7. Write a newsletter welcome email sequence (3 emails over 5 days) for [BUSINESS]. Email 1: warm introduction + freebie delivery. Email 2: your story + credibility. Email 3: soft pitch + testimonial.

8. Rewrite this text to be more engaging and persuasive while keeping the same meaning: "[PASTE YOUR TEXT]". Add power words, shorten sentences, and improve the flow.

9. Create a Twitter/X thread (10 tweets) teaching [TOPIC]. Start with a bold claim that makes people stop scrolling. Each tweet should be self-contained but flow into the next. End with a recap + CTA.

10. Write 5 different email subject lines for a promotional email about [OFFER]. Include one with a number, one with urgency, one with curiosity, one personalized (using [FIRST NAME]), and one contrarian.

  BUSINESS & STRATEGY (10 Prompts)
============================================================

11. Act as a business consultant. I'm starting a [TYPE OF BUSINESS] with a budget of [AMOUNT]. Create a lean launch plan covering: target customer profile, minimum viable product, first 90-day milestones, marketing channels to prioritize, and key metrics to track.

12. Analyze my business idea: [DESCRIBE IDEA]. Give me an honest SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats). Be specific, not generic. Include 3 competitors I should study.

13. Create a pricing strategy for my [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. My costs are [AMOUNT], my target market is [AUDIENCE], and competitors charge [RANGE]. Recommend 3 pricing models with pros/cons of each.

14. Write a 60-second elevator pitch for [BUSINESS/IDEA] that covers: the problem, the solution, why now, why us, and the ask. Make it natural, not robotic.

15. I need to negotiate [SITUATION]. Give me a preparation framework: my BATNA (best alternative), their likely interests, 3 anchoring points, concession strategy, and 5 specific phrases to use during the negotiation.

16. Create a customer avatar for [BUSINESS]. Include demographics, psychographics, daily routine, biggest frustrations, where they spend time online, what they've already tried, and what would make them say "shut up and take my money."

17. Design an onboarding flow for new customers of [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Map out the first 7 days: welcome message, quick-win tutorial, feature discovery sequence, feedback request, and upsell opportunity.

18. I run a [TYPE OF BUSINESS]. Write a quarterly business review template that I can fill in each quarter. Include sections for revenue analysis, customer metrics, marketing performance, operational efficiency, and next-quarter priorities.

19. Act as a financial advisor. I have $[AMOUNT] in revenue and $[AMOUNT] in expenses. My biggest cost categories are [LIST]. Identify 3 areas where I can cut costs without hurting quality, and 3 ways to increase revenue in the next 90 days.

20. Create a partnership pitch email I can send to [TYPE OF COMPANY] to propose a [TYPE OF PARTNERSHIP]. Include what I bring to the table, what's in it for them, a specific proposal, and a low-commitment next step.

26. Write a case study about how [CLIENT/CUSTOMER] achieved [RESULT] using [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Structure it as: Challenge → Solution → Implementation → Results (with specific numbers) → Testimonial quote → CTA.

27. Create a competitive analysis for [MY BUSINESS] vs [COMPETITOR 1], [COMPETITOR 2], and [COMPETITOR 3]. Compare: pricing, features, target audience, brand positioning, marketing channels, and customer reviews sentiment.

28. Generate 30 blog post ideas for [NICHE] that target long-tail keywords. For each idea, include the target keyword, estimated search intent (informational/transactional/navigational), and suggested content format.

29. Write a launch announcement for [NEW PRODUCT/FEATURE]. Create versions for: email blast, Instagram post, Twitter/X thread, LinkedIn post, and press release summary. Each should match the platform's tone and format.

30. Design an A/B testing plan for my [LANDING PAGE / EMAIL / AD]. Identify the 5 highest-impact elements to test, write hypothesis statements for each test, define success metrics, and recommend minimum sample size.

  PRODUCTIVITY & PERSONAL (10 Prompts)
============================================================

31. Act as my executive assistant. I have these tasks to complete this week: [LIST TASKS]. Prioritize them using the Eisenhower Matrix (urgent/important), estimate time for each, and create a day-by-day schedule. Block 2 hours of deep work each morning.

32. I need to learn [SKILL/TOPIC] in 30 days. Create a structured learning plan: break it into weekly themes, recommend free resources for each week, include daily practice exercises (15-30 min), and define milestones to measure progress.

33. Write a professional but firm email declining [REQUEST/INVITATION] without burning the bridge. Acknowledge their effort, give a brief honest reason, suggest an alternative if possible, and leave the door open for future opportunities.

34. I'm preparing for a job interview for a [JOB TITLE] position at a [TYPE OF COMPANY]. Give me: 10 likely interview questions, a STAR method answer framework for behavioral questions, 5 smart questions to ask them, and 3 common mistakes to avoid.

35. Create a morning routine optimized for productivity. I wake up at [TIME], need to leave by [TIME], and my priorities are [LIST]. Include time blocks, habit stacking suggestions, and what to eliminate. Keep it realistic, not Instagram-influencer fantasy.

36. I'm feeling overwhelmed by [SITUATION]. Act as a therapist and help me break this down: identify what I can control vs what I can't, give me 3 immediate actions I can take today, and help me reframe the situation in a more empowering way.

37. Write a self-performance review for my role as [JOB TITLE]. Include sections for: key accomplishments with metrics, areas of growth, challenges overcome, skills developed, and goals for next review period. Use confident but not arrogant language.

38. Help me make a decision between [OPTION A] and [OPTION B]. Create a weighted decision matrix with these criteria: [LIST CRITERIA]. Score each option 1-10 on each criterion. Then give me your recommendation with reasoning.

39. I want to build a habit of [HABIT]. Using the framework from Atomic Habits, help me: make it obvious (cue), make it attractive (craving), make it easy (response), and make it satisfying (reward). Include specific implementation intentions.

40. Create a meal prep plan for the week. My dietary preferences are [PREFERENCES], budget is $[AMOUNT], and I have [TIME AVAILABLE] to cook on Sunday. Include a grocery list organized by store section, and prep instructions in order of efficiency.

  LEARNING & RESEARCH (10 Prompts)
============================================================

41. Explain [COMPLEX TOPIC] to me like I'm 10 years old. Then explain it like I'm a college student. Then explain it like I'm an expert in a related field. Highlight what changes at each level of understanding.

42. I just read about [TOPIC/CONCEPT]. Ask me 10 increasingly difficult questions to test my understanding. After I answer each one, tell me if I'm right, correct any misconceptions, and explain why.

43. Compare and contrast [CONCEPT A] vs [CONCEPT B]. Use a structured format: definition of each, key similarities, key differences, when to use each, common misconceptions, and a real-world analogy that makes the distinction clear.

44. Act as a Socratic tutor for [SUBJECT]. Don't give me answers directly — ask me guiding questions that lead me to discover the answer myself. Start with the fundamentals and progressively get more advanced. Correct me gently when I'm wrong.

45. Summarize the key ideas from [BOOK TITLE] by [AUTHOR]. Include: the central thesis, 5 most important concepts, 3 actionable takeaways I can implement today, legitimate criticisms of the book, and other books that complement it.

46. I'm writing a research paper on [TOPIC]. Help me create an outline with: thesis statement, 5 main arguments, supporting evidence types needed for each argument, potential counterarguments I should address, and a conclusion framework.

47. Create a vocabulary list of 20 essential terms for [FIELD/INDUSTRY]. For each term, provide: the definition, an example of how it's used in context, a common mistake people make with it, and a memory trick to remember it.

48. Design a 5-question quiz about [TOPIC] that tests understanding at different levels of Bloom's Taxonomy: remembering, understanding, applying, analyzing, and evaluating. Include answer key with explanations.

49. I'm trying to understand [CURRENT EVENT/TREND]. Give me the full context: what happened, why it matters, who the key players are, what different perspectives exist, what's likely to happen next, and what most people get wrong about it.

50. Create a mental model toolkit for [FIELD/DECISION TYPE]. Give me 5 mental models that apply, explain each one in plain English, provide an example of using it, and tell me when each model fails or misleads.
